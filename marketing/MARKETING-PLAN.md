# Worksop Workspace — Pre-Launch Marketing Plan

**Today:** Tue 5 May 2026
**Launch:** Mon 1 June 2026 (Open House)
**Window:** 27 days

This plan sits alongside `CONTENT-PLAN.md` (the social posting schedule). That covers what goes on FB and IG. This covers everything else: local discovery, press, partnerships, signage, paid ads, and launch-day amplification.

Each task below has a **deadline by which it must be done**. Treat them as hard.

---

## Phase A — Foundations · By **Sun 10 May**

> Stuff that has lead time. Get it started this week so it's ready when the date drops.

### A1. Google Business Profile · **By Wed 6 May**
- Go to `business.google.com` and create a profile for "Worksop Workspace" at 30 Carlton Road, S80 1PH.
- Pick category: "Coworking space" + secondary "Office space rental agency".
- Request verification immediately. Verification by post can take 5–14 days, so this is the single most time-sensitive task on the list.
- Add: opening hours (mark as "Opening soon"), website, phone, photos of the building exterior + interior shots, services list (Day Pass £12, Hot Desk £30/wk).
- Once verified, post weekly updates from the dashboard — Google rewards active profiles in local search.

### A2. Window vinyl + A-board on Carlton Road · **By Sat 9 May**
- Get vinyl applied to the front window: logo + "Opening 1st June 2026" + a big QR code → Instagram.
- Get an A-board for the pavement with the same. Put it out from now until launch every day the building's accessible.
- Why: Carlton Road is the route between the station and the town centre. Every commuter and shopper is a free impression.

### A3. Apple Maps + Bing Places + Yell + coworking directories · **By Sun 10 May**
Add the listing to:
- **Apple Maps Connect** — `mapsconnect.apple.com` (free)
- **Bing Places** — `bingplaces.com` (free)
- **Yell.com** — free basic listing
- **Coworker.com** — international coworking directory, free listing, decent SEO juice
- **Allwork.space** directory
- **Workfrom.co**

Each listing is a free backlink and a separate place where someone searching for coworking might find you.

### A4. LinkedIn Company Page · **By Sun 10 May**
- Set up a LinkedIn company page for Worksop Workspace separate from Connor's personal profile.
- Connor follows it from his personal account, then invites all his connections (LinkedIn lets you invite up to 250 connections per month to follow your page).
- This gives the page institutional credibility and a permanent home for B2B traffic.

### A5. Email capture / founders waiting list page · **By Wed 6 May**
- The founder posts (`09 / 15 / 18` in the social plan) drive traffic to a sign-up form. That form must exist before those posts go live (currently scheduled around Sat 30 May).
- Tool: Mailchimp / ConvertKit / Beehiiv / a simple Wix form — anything with capture and an automated welcome email.
- Welcome email should: thank them, link them to a 60-second video tour (record on phone), and tell them what happens next.

## Phase B — Local Discovery · By **Sun 17 May**

> Once foundations are in, focus on getting found by people actively searching.

### B1. Local SEO — three blog posts on the website · **By Fri 15 May**
- "Best places to work from in Worksop" (mention 3 cafés, then mention yourselves at the end as the dedicated option)
- "Coworking in Bassetlaw: what's actually available" (you'll come up first because you're the only one)
- "Hot desk vs working from home: the honest maths" (re-purposes the value content)

Each ~600–800 words, hyper-local language, internal links to the Open House page. These are the pages that pick up "coworking Worksop", "hot desk Worksop", "office space Bassetlaw" Google searches.

### B2. Schema markup + Google Business Profile linking · **By Fri 15 May**
- Make sure the website has LocalBusiness schema with the address, phone, opening hours, and price range.
- Link the website from the Google Business Profile and vice versa.
- Submit the sitemap to Google Search Console.

### B3. First 5 Google reviews lined up · **By Sat 30 May**
- Identify 5 people (founder members, friends with businesses, suppliers) who'll leave a Google review at 9am on launch day, **Mon 1 June**.
- Don't ask them to do it earlier — Google flags unusual review patterns. Do them on the day, real-time, real visits.
- Brief them: keep it natural, mention specifics (the coffee, the desk, the parking), no five-star templated text.

### F2. How to find genuine local micro-influencers (do this manually) · **By Fri 22 May**

Spend 60 minutes on Instagram doing this:

1. **Search by location tag.** Type "Worksop" or "Worksop, Nottinghamshire" into Instagram search → click the location → look at "Top" and "Recent" posts. Anyone posting consistently with 1k–10k followers is a candidate.
2. **Search hashtags:** `#Worksop`, `#WorksopLife`, `#Bassetlaw`, `#NorthNotts`, `#DiscoverWorksop`, `#WorksopFood`, `#BassetlawBusiness`.
3. **Categories that work for B2B coworking outreach:**
   - **Local foodies/café accounts** (foodie content travels in Worksop)
   - **Family / mum bloggers** (lots of self-employed parents who'd benefit from a desk)
   - **Photographers** (often freelance, often need a meeting space)
   - **Personal trainers / fitness coaches** (often self-employed, often need a base)
   - **Estate agents** (active locally, share business news)
   - **Independent business owners** with personal accounts
4. **Make a shortlist of 5–10 accounts.** DM each: "I'm opening Worksop's first coworking space on 1st June. Would love to offer you a free week to try it and post about it if you like the space — no obligation. Could I send a few details?"
5. **What to track in a spreadsheet:** account, followers, engagement (likes per post / followers × 100 = engagement rate; >2% is good), niche, your message sent, their reply.

### F3. Reciprocal pitch · **By Mon 25 May**
For the 3–5 who say yes:
- Block them in for a free week (any week before/after launch — flexibility helps them say yes)
- Offer to feature them on your page in return
- Send a press kit: 3 photos of the space, key facts, your IG handle, the address
- Don't dictate the post — let them write authentically

## Phase G — Paid Ads · From **Mon 18 May** through launch

> Ads work best when there's organic content for them to promote. Hold off until the page has 20+ posts on it. By 18 May you'll be at exactly that point.

### G1. Meta Pixel installed on website · **By Mon 18 May**
- Install Facebook/Instagram pixel on `worksopworkspace.com`.
- Triggers retargeting — anyone who visits the site can now be shown ads chasing them. Cheapest, highest-ROI ad spend you'll do.

### G2. Geo-fenced Meta ads · **From Wed 20 May**
- **Geography:** S80, S81, DN22 (Retford), NG18/NG19/NG20 (Mansfield area), 25-min drive radius around the postcode.
- **Demographics:** ages 28–55.
- **Interests:** small business owners, entrepreneurs, freelancers, remote work, productivity, coworking.
- **Behaviours:** "frequent travellers" (proxy for hybrid workers), small business page admins.
- **Budget:** £5–£10 per day. Even £5 is plenty for a town this size.
- **Ads:** boost the value posts (29-cafe-maths, 32-honest-comparison, 33-cost-of-distractions) — those convert because they've got a sharp hook.

### G3. Google Ads on hyper-local keywords · **From Wed 20 May**
- `coworking Worksop`, `office space Worksop`, `hot desk Worksop`, `coworking Bassetlaw`, `office space Retford`, `office space Mansfield`, `coworking S80`.
- Low search volume but very high intent. £5/day will dominate them.
- Send all clicks to a dedicated landing page with the Open House registration form.

### G4. Boost the best organic post each week · **Weekly from Mon 18 May**
- Wait 48 hours after each post, see which one is winning organically, boost that one with £15–£20.
- Don't boost weak posts. Let the algorithm tell you which message is resonating, then put petrol on the fire.
