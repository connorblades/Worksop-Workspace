## Phase 1 (live from 1 June 2026)

### Day Pass — £12 / day

**What it is.** One full day's access to the workspace during opening hours. Walk in, work, walk out.

**What's included.** Hot desk seating, Wi-Fi, unlimited tea/coffee/water, locker for the day, free on-site parking, full use of the refreshment area.

**Who it's for.**

- Someone trying us out before committing
- Occasional users who don't need a weekly plan
- A member's invited guest for the day
- Visiting clients
- Someone in town for the day who needs a quiet place to work

**How they buy.** Card at reception, via the app, or in advance via the website. No advance booking required for hot desks — they can walk in any time during opening hours.

**Sales talk track.**

> "Day pass is £12 — gives you the day, all the coffee you can drink, free parking. Walk in any time, no need to book ahead."

---

### Hot Desk Weekly — £30 / week (rolling)

**What it is.** A rolling weekly subscription to any available hot desk seat, Monday to Friday during opening hours. Cancel any time via the app.

**What's included.** Any available hot desk (first come, first served), all the refreshments, locker, Wi-Fi, parking, the social and quiet zones.

**Who it's for.**

- The largest expected segment: remote employees, freelancers, sole traders, tradespeople who do admin from a van
- Anyone here roughly 2+ days a week
- Anyone who's done a few Day Passes and wants to lock in the better rate

**How they buy.** Direct debit (or recurring card) via Stripe at sign-up. Rolling weekly — cancelled any time via app, takes effect at end of current billing cycle.

**Maths to share if asked.**

> "Works out at roughly £6 a day if you're in five days a week. About two and a half day passes' worth."

**Sales talk track.**

> "Weekly pass is £30 — best deal once you're in more than two days a week. It's rolling, so cancel any time, no notice. Like a gym."

### Private Office — from £79 / week (rolling)

**What it is.** A lockable, private office for 1–4 people, on a rolling weekly subscription.

**What's included.** Lockable door, dedicated room, furniture for the room, all the refreshments, Wi-Fi, locker access, parking, full use of the shared facilities (meeting room hourly, refreshment area, etc.).

**Who it's for.**

- Small business owners with a 1–4 person team
- Anyone needing privacy for confidential calls or work
- A professional who client-meets often and needs the polish of a private room

**How they buy.** Enquire (phone or email), have a viewing, sign up via Stripe. The £79 is "from" — actual price depends on the specific room and may be bespoke for longer commitments.

**Sales talk track.**

> "Private office starts at £79 a week — that's all-in. Lockable door, your space, your team. Have a look at the available rooms and we'll find one that fits."

**Open question** (per Connor): Phase 1 may include rooms that double as **meeting rooms when not let on a private-office plan**. Specifics TBC — the layout decision is open. Sales should describe the offer as "private office, weekly" and check current availability with Connor before committing a specific room.

### Meeting Room — £20 / hour

**What it is.** Hourly use of the meeting room for client-facing sessions, interviews, or any conversation that needs more than a hot desk.

**What's included.** The room for the booked hour(s), Wi-Fi, screen / display equipment (TBC — confirm spec before launch), refreshments available from the shared station.

**Who it's for.**

- Members hosting a client meeting
- Visitors paying hourly to use the space for a one-off meeting
- Anyone needing a more polished setting than a hot desk

**How they buy.** Book via the app. Card paid at booking.

**Sales talk track.**

> "Meeting room is £20 an hour, book by the hour via the app. We're one of the only places in town doing meeting rooms outside standard office hours — including weekends. So if you've got a Saturday morning client call, we've got you."

**Important context.** The local-market gap on meeting rooms is huge — The Turbine cuts at 4:30pm, Middletons doesn't open weekends. Lean into this without naming competitors. See `../marketing/competitor-analysis.md`.

## What's NOT in the offer

- **No boardroom.** Earlier drafts mentioned one. It's been removed. The meeting room covers client-facing sessions.
- **No 24/7 access in Phase 1.** Manned hours only (8am–5pm Mon–Fri).
- **No EV charging in Phase 1.**
- **No coffee shop / café service for non-members.**
- **No bar / event space rental** for now.
- **No long-term contracts.** Everything rolling.
- **No commitment discounts.** Same price whether you stay a week or a year.

## Comms playbook

- **Always lead with what's included** rather than what's excluded. "Day pass £12 — all the coffee, free parking, Wi-Fi, locker."
- **Quote prices confidently.** They're fixed and transparent. No need to caveat.
- **Don't push the most expensive option.** Match the right plan to what the person actually needs.
- **Cancellation is a feature, not a flaw.** Mention it early. "Rolling weekly, cancel any time" is a selling point against business centres' long contracts.
- **Compare gym-style only if it lands.** Some people get it instantly. Others find it weird that a workspace works that way. Read the room.
