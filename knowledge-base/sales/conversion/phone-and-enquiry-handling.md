# Phone and Enquiry Handling

**Worksop Workspace** · Training · *Phase 1 reference*

Status: Draft v1 — needs Connor review · Last updated: 14 May 2026

---

How to answer the phone, what to say to common enquiries, what to log, and when to put someone through to Connor. Most enquiries are simple — pricing, opening times, booking a tour. Handle them confidently and the caller already feels good about the brand.

---

## Standard greeting

When the phone rings:

> "Worksop Workspace, [your name] speaking — how can I help?"

Three things: business name first, your name, open question. Don't say "What's this regarding?" — that sounds like a call centre. People will tell you why they're calling without being asked.

## Common enquiries

### "How much is it?"

Quick, transparent answer. Don't deflect to a website.

> "Day pass is £12, weekly pass is £30, private office starts from £125 a week. No contracts on any of it — cancel any time. Want me to talk you through any of those in more detail?"

If they want detail, use plain language. Don't read out a price sheet.

### "Can I come and have a look?"

> "Yeah, of course. Are you in town this week? I can do a quick tour whenever suits — about 20 minutes."

Offer a couple of specific times rather than "whenever". If they need to think, give them a way back: "Or just turn up and ask for [name] — we don't need a booking."

### "When do you open?"

> "We're manned 8 till 5, Monday to Friday. We've got plans for evening and 24/7 access later in the year, but Phase 1 is daytime."

Don't over-promise on later phases. Date-tied claims need to be in points-of-truth.

### "Where exactly are you?"

> "30 Carlton Road, Worksop town centre — S80 1PH if you want to stick it in sat-nav. Free parking on site."

The parking line lands every time.

### "Do you do meeting rooms?"

> "We will do — meeting room and podcast studio are coming in a later phase. Right now we've got private offices for client-facing stuff if that helps. Whereabouts are you based?"

The closing question keeps the conversation going.

### "Can I bring a client / guest?"

> "Yeah, guests are welcome — just sign them in when they arrive. Same house rules apply."

### "How do I cancel?"

If it's a member calling to cancel:

> "Through the app — it's self-service, no notice needed. Same price waiting for you if you ever want to come back. Anything we could do better?"

Don't try to save the membership. Just listen if they want to tell you why. Log it in feedback.

### "I'm interested in being a sponsor / partner / advertiser"

> "Thanks for thinking of us. We're not running sponsorships at this stage — we're keeping the space as a single brand for now. Happy to put you in touch with Connor if you want to chat about future phases though."

Take their name and number. Pass to Connor.

### "Can my [organisation] host an event there?"

This is a future-phase thing.

> "Right now we're focused on the day-to-day workspace, but we are looking at hosting events from later this year — community lunches, speaker nights, that sort of thing. Want to leave your details and Connor will be in touch when we open events up?"

Take their name, email, what they'd want to host, and a rough audience size. Log in `../10-marketing/local-partnerships.md` (Drive) or send to Connor.

### "I'm a journalist / blogger / podcaster"

Pause before answering. Take their name, publication / outlet, what they're working on, and their deadline. Tell them Connor will call them back. **Don't give a quote on the spot.**

### "I want to make a complaint"

> "Of course — sorry to hear that. Walk me through what's happened."

Listen. Take notes. Confirm back what you've heard. Tell them what you'll do next and by when. Log it. Follow up.

Use the conflict-handling framework in `member-conflict-handling.md` if it's about another member.

## Mail and packages

### "Will you take a delivery for me?"

For members on rolling plans, yes — but tell them to flag it in advance:

> "Yeah, no problem — let us know when you're expecting it and what name to look out for."

For day-pass visitors, prospects, or random callers — no:

> "We'd usually only hold packages for active members — but if you're nearby you could pop in and pick something up if it lands before 5."

Don't accept deliveries for non-members. They pile up.

### When a delivery actually arrives

1. Check the recipient name — is it a current member?
2. If yes: accept, store in the locker behind reception, message the member via the app
3. If no: politely refuse and ask the courier to redirect
4. Never accept unmarked or suspicious packages — see `../03-operations/sops/sop-010-emergency-evacuation.md`

## What to log for every non-trivial call

In the member app's note system (or a dedicated enquiry log, however it's set up):

- **Date and time** of the call
- **Name** of the caller
- **Phone number** (and email if they offered it)
- **What they wanted**
- **What you said**
- **What's next** — tour booked, info sent, Connor to call back, etc.
- **Whether they're on the waiting list / a current member / new entirely**

This matters because:

- It's how marketing measures inbound enquiry quality
- It catches patterns (e.g. 5 different callers asking about evening hours = product signal)
- It's how Connor or a future colleague picks up a conversation you started

## When to put someone through to Connor

Right away, if any of these:

- Sponsor / advertiser / partnership pitch with apparent budget behind it
- Journalist, podcaster, or media call
- Anything legal-shaped (solicitor calling, council, ICO, HMRC, complaint mentioning lawyers)
- Anyone claiming to be a contractor / supplier of his that you don't recognise
- Anyone he's specifically asked you to put through (note that in a shared note somewhere)
- Any caller who's clearly angry, abusive, or threatening — you don't have to absorb that

## After-hours and voicemail

Phase 1 is manned 8–5. Outside those hours the phone goes to voicemail.

Voicemail greeting (suggested):

> "You've reached Worksop Workspace. We're not at our desks right now — we're manned 8 till 5, Monday to Friday. Leave your name, number, and a short message and we'll come back to you the next working day. Or pop us an email at hello@worksopworkspace.co.uk and we'll usually be quicker."

Check voicemail at the start of every shift. Return calls before lunch.

## What never to do on the phone

- **Never quote prices that don't exist on the website.** Pricing is fixed. If you don't know, say "let me check and call you back" and actually do.
- **Never promise dates for future phases.** "Phase 2 opens in [month]" is a commitment we can't pull back from. Say "later this year" if it's vague; specific only if Connor has confirmed.
- **Never criticise a competitor by name.** "Don't bother with The Turbine, they close at 4:30" reads as petty even when it's true. Lead with what *we* do, not what *they* don't.
- **Never speak for Connor on a strategic question.** "Do you accept investment / partnerships / acquisition offers?" — "I'll have Connor call you."
- **Never feel rushed.** It's better to take 30 seconds to think than to give a fast wrong answer.

## When you don't know the answer

> "Honestly I'm not sure — let me find out and call you back within the hour. What's the best number?"

That's a good answer. Better than a confident wrong one. Then actually call back.
