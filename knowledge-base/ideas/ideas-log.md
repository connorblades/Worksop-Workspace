# Ideas Log

**Worksop Workspace** · Ideas · *Future possibilities*

A running list of things we'd like to try, build, host, or roll out. Nothing here is committed. Nothing here is promised on the website, on the phone, or in conversations with members. **An idea moves out of this folder only when it has a date, a budget, and an owner.**

## Community & events

### Networking events

- **Idea:** Curated networking nights for members and invited locals. Drinks, light food, structured intros (e.g. five-minute speed intros). Quarterly.
- **Why it might work:** Members are mostly remote workers and small business owners — they don't have a natural networking channel locally.
- **Why it might not:** Brand position is explicitly "not a networking hub" during work hours. Need to be careful that *events* are clearly framed as out-of-hours and optional — and that members don't start treating the space itself as a networking floor.
- **Status:** Idea only.
- **Open questions:** Branded "Worksop Workspace networks" or sponsored by us as a host? Members-only or open?

### Chess club

- **Idea:** Weekly informal chess evening. Boards set up, drinks available, open to members and non-members.
- **Why it might work:** Calm, focused, community-building. Fits the brand voice (quiet, thoughtful, slightly characterful). Cheap to run.
- **Why it might not:** Niche. Might attract three regulars and stay there. That's OK — small can be good.
- **Status:** Idea only. Could be a low-risk pilot — try it for six weeks, see who turns up.
- **Open questions:** Member-led or operator-led? Bring-your-own-board or we provide?

### Co-working lunches

- **Idea:** Monthly shared lunch — we provide some food, members bring something to share, an hour blocked out to eat together.
- **Why it might work:** Lowest-risk community-building. Already a moment in the day (lunch); we just add a tiny bit of ceremony.
- **Why it might not:** People come here to focus, not to attend events. Some will skip it on principle. That's fine — it's optional.
- **Status:** Idea. Could trial in month 1 if there's appetite.

### Speaker nights

- **Idea:** Monthly evening event. One local business person, one talk, audience Q&A. Free for members, ticketed for non-members (small fee, covers drinks).
- **Why it might work:** Real value, drives local awareness, makes us a hub without making the space itself a networking floor.
- **Why it might not:** Hard to find good speakers regularly. Risk of becoming a self-promotional pitch night.
- **Status:** Phase 2 or 3 — needs the basement space (or repurposed ground floor) to host.
- **Open questions:** Local-only speakers, or open to bringing in from Sheffield / Nottingham / Doncaster? Theme per night, or open?

### Photography days

- **Idea:** Once a month or once a quarter, a photographer comes in to take new content — for the website, social, member testimonials, building shots. Members can opt in to have their workspace shot for content (with their permission).
- **Why it might work:** Solves the always-needed content problem. Members get a free professional photo (perk). Brand asset library grows organically.
- **Why it might not:** Cost of the photographer needs to land — could be a barter (their photos in exchange for free workspace access) or paid.
- **Status:** Idea. Probably starts informally in Phase 1 with phone photos, formalises if the brand grows.

### Workshops

- **Idea:** Half-day or evening workshops on topics relevant to our member base — bookkeeping for sole traders, productivity, marketing for small business, mental health for freelancers, etc.
- **Why it might work:** Genuine value, paid (small fee), local. Differentiates us from "just a space" to "a space that helps you do your work better."
- **Why it might not:** Hosting workshops is a real operational lift. Programming, marketing, attendance management — it adds up. Needs to be done well or not at all.
- **Status:** Phase 2 onwards. Could pilot one workshop in Phase 1 to see what demand looks like.
- **Open questions:** In-house teaching or hosted by external experts? Free to members, paid for non-members? Recorded?

## Space and facility ideas

### Suggestion box at reception

- A physical box and a feedback log doc. Members drop ideas anonymously. Reviewed weekly.
- Low effort, high signal. **Probably do this in Phase 1.**

### "Wall of regulars" / member photo wall

- Members opt-in to a small framed photo + their business name on a wall. Builds belonging, free marketing for them, visible community proof for visitors.
- Phase 2.

### Quiet booth(s)

- One or two small individual booths for deep-focus work or sensitive calls. Sit between hot desks and private offices. Bookable by the hour or 30-minute slots.
- Could be a Phase 2 build-out item. Note: somewhat overlapping with private offices.

### Bookable hot desks (premium seats)

- Some hot desks (e.g. window seats, corner spots) bookable in advance for a small premium (£2–£3 extra).
- Probably **not** for Phase 1. Adds complexity to a simple offer.

## Membership and pricing ideas

### Founding member discount

- First N sign-ups get a locked-in rate for life, or a one-off discount month.
- Decision point: still consistent with "no commitment discounts" brand position? Could be framed as a launch bonus rather than ongoing.
- Probably not — the brand position is fixed pricing. A free open day already serves the launch incentive role.

### Corporate / multi-desk plans

- A pre-packaged 5-pack or 10-pack of weekly passes a company can buy and assign to staff.
- Status: idea. Worth revisiting once a few small teams are using us.

### Drop-in evening hours

- A pilot week of staffed evening hours (e.g. 5pm–9pm) to see if there's demand for after-work coworking.
- Risk: extends staffing costs without guaranteed revenue.
- Status: idea. Trial once we have stable Phase 1 traffic.

### Day-pass bundles

- 5-pack of day passes at a small discount (e.g. 5 days for £55, saves £5).
- Could be a way for occasional users to commit without committing.
- Decision point: same as founding-member discount — does this conflict with the fixed-price brand position?

- Phase 2.

### Sponsor a desk / sponsor a chair (very-late phase)

- Could drive volume and PR.
- Phase 2.

## Marketing / content ideas

### "Worksop Workspace Stories" — short member portraits

- 60-second video portraits of members and what they do. Shot on phone, low production. Posted to Instagram and TikTok.
- Low cost, high authenticity. Helps with the "is this place for people like me?" question prospects ask.

### A founding-membership "wall of names"

- Names of every member who joined in the first 90 days on a wall (with opt-in). Mentioned in marketing as "founding members".
- Belonging, social proof.

### Photo-of-the-week competition

- Members submit one photo of their workspace, view from the window, coffee setup, etc. Best one wins a free week.
- Generates content. Engages the community.

## How to use this doc

- **Capture freely.** Every half-formed idea is welcome. It costs nothing to write down.
- **Don't promise externally.** Nothing here is committed until it leaves this doc and gets a date / budget / owner.
- **Review quarterly.** Pull the strongest ideas, kill the dead ones, propose pilots.
- **Members can contribute.** When the suggestion box (above) goes in, member ideas land here.

When an idea moves from "idea" to "active plan", move it out of this file and into the appropriate functional folder (Marketing, Operations, etc.) with a real plan attached.
