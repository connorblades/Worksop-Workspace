# Virtual Office Worksop. Product Decision

**Worksop Workspace** · Marketing · *Decision record*

Status: Decided · 17 May 2026

Related: `seo-keyword-research.md` §"Soften these claims" and `seo-gap-analysis.md` §1 (decision-needed row).

## The question

Should Worksop Workspace offer a virtual-office service, build a `/virtual-office-worksop` page and chase the keyword?

A virtual office in this context means: registered business address with mail handling, no physical desk usage included. Typically £35-£75 per month in the UK.

## The market context

- Three Worksop competitors offer it: Worksop Turbine, Middletons Yard, and Enterprise Business Centre (per the keyword research).
- "Virtual office Worksop" has commercial-intent search volume but is a smaller market than coworking itself.
- The keyword research flagged it as a clear gap we could close cheaply.

## The decision

**Skip for now. Revisit when 10+ private offices are booked.**

## Why

**For:**
- Easy to deliver operationally. We already handle post for active members (per the FAQ on `/private-offices`). Extending that to non-members is a registered-address service, a small mail tray, and a clear policy.
- Captures a keyword with three competitors and no current Worksop Workspace presence.
- Recurring monthly revenue stream that doesn't consume any desk space.
- Adds credibility for sole-trader and limited-company customers who need a registered address but don't want to use their home address.

**Against (the reasons we're parking it):**
- Phase 1 launch focus. We have a finite number of "first impression" launch decisions, and the brand is being positioned around modern, walk-in, transparent coworking. Adding a virtual office as a Phase 1 product risks diluting the launch message.
- Mail handling at scale carries operational overhead (logging, notification, storage, GDPR for non-member personal data). Worth it once we're staffed for it, not in the launch weeks.
- Customer-fit ambiguity: virtual-office customers don't visit the building. They're not part of the community we're trying to build. Pushing them now sends a mixed signal.
- The keyword target audience is small. We probably gain 1-3 customers per month from organic search at launch. Not the right launch lever vs the time cost.
- Regulatory: HMRC and Companies House have specific rules about registered offices. If we get the operations wrong, we cause problems for customers. Worth doing properly, not quickly.

**Revisit trigger:**
- 10+ private offices booked (signals the building is bedded in and operationally stable).
- Or, at the first quarterly review post-launch (2026-08-17), as a standing agenda item.

## What this means for the site

- **No** `/virtual-office-worksop` page is being built in Phase 4/5.
- The blog comparison post at `/blog/coworking-options-in-worksop` mentions competitors offering virtual offices but does **not** claim we offer one.
- The pre-seeded GBP Q&A (per the local-ranking-playbook) includes the question "Do you offer virtual office services?" with the honest answer: "Not yet. We hold post at reception for active members but don't offer registered-address-only services. If that's important to you, see [competitor list] or get in touch." This stops people drifting away from the listing without a reply.
- The waiting-list form retains the "I'm interested in: something else" option, which catches genuine virtual-office demand for tracking.

## If we change this decision later

The page is cheap to build (one HTML page following the template at `/private-offices`). The operational work needed:

1. Mail-handling SOP (logging, notification, storage, return process)
2. GDPR + ROPA update (we already have ROPA in operations; would need to add this processing)
3. Pricing decision (£35-£50 per month seems competitive given the local market)
4. Customer agreement (separate terms from the coworking member agreement)
5. Companies House registered-office acceptance check (some addresses are flagged if too many companies register; not an issue at our address yet)

Total operational ramp: ~1 week of work once we decide to launch it.
