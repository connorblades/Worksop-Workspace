# Worksop Workspace — Brand Guidelines

*A space to drop your shoulders.*

The visual and verbal system behind Worksop Workspace. Built around white space, warm wood, and quiet confidence. Designed to mirror the building itself.

**Internal & partner reference.** Version 2.4 · May 2026.

---

## 01 / The Brand

### What we are

A blend between a coffee shop and a business centre. The first dedicated coworking space in Worksop, built for people who are serious about their work but don't want the formality or cost of a conventional office.

### The Promise

A focused, professional, welcoming space where people can get their work done. Quiet when you need it, community when you want it.

### The Feeling

> "I can drop my shoulders here. This is a place for me."

Welcoming. Focused. Human. Local.

## 02 / Colour

### The palette

Every surface colour in the brand is in the building. White cabinets, oak surfaces, dark wood floors, grey doors, black chairs. The palette below is what someone sees online, and what they walk into. Navy sits separately as the reserved logo colour, carrying the brand mark without dominating the layout.

| Name | Hex | Role |
|---|---|---|
| White | `#FFFFFF` | Primary background. Mirrors the white cabinets and natural light. Dominant across all surfaces. |
| Linen | `#F5F1EA` | Soft section backgrounds. Picks up the warmth of oak surfaces without losing breathing room. |
| Oak Honey | `#C9A57B` | Warm wood tone. Mirrors the oak desks and tabletops. Used in graphics, dividers, and imagery framing. |
| Espresso | `#1A1714` | Authority anchor. Mirrors dark wood floors and black chairs. Headlines, key numbers, primary text. |
| Stone | `#5C5C58` | Neutral grey. Mirrors the grey doors and chair frames. Secondary text, structural elements. |
| Sage | `#8E9775` | Character note. Lives in plants, eyebrow text, dividers, supporting graphics. Soft, never dominant. |
| Terracotta Clay | `#C57B57` | Engagement accent. **Primary CTAs only.** One per view. Used sparingly. The colour that asks people to act. |
| Charcoal | `#2B2B2B` | Wordmark / supporting black. |

### Usage ratio

| Colour | % of layout |
|---|---|
| White | 55% |
| Linen | 20% |
| Espresso | 12% |
| Oak | 7% |
| Sage | 4% |
| Clay | 2% |

If a layout doesn't feel like there's room to breathe, there isn't. Pull back. White and linen should always dominate. Clay should feel rare. That's what makes it work.

### Reserved: Brand Navy `#203A5B`

The colour that lives in the logomark. Navy is **reserved for the brand mark itself**: logo, signage, favicon, business cards, brand chrome. It is **never used as a layout fill, section background, or large surface colour**. This keeps the logo distinctive without dragging the rest of the experience away from the building. When the logo is on screen, navy is doing its job. Everywhere else, espresso anchors the page.

## 03 / Materials & Imagery

### Where the brand meets the building

The website is a promise. The building is the delivery. The imagery is the bridge, and it has to honour both. When someone walks in, the room should feel exactly like the photographs they saw online.

### Building materials → brand colours

| Building element | Brand colour |
|---|---|
| White cabinets & walls | White `#FFFFFF` |
| Oak desks & tabletops | Oak Honey `#C9A57B` |
| Dark wood flooring | Espresso `#1A1714` |
| Grey doors & chair frames | Stone `#5C5C58` |
| Black chair seats | Espresso / Charcoal |
| Plants & soft touches | Sage `#8E9775` (bring this in through greenery and styling) |

### Cinematic photography direction

The website is cinematic. Full-bleed imagery, slow reveals, deep contrast. Every image should feel intentional.

**Shoot for this feeling:**

- Natural light, ideally morning or late afternoon. Soft, warm, directional.
- Wide aspect ratios (16:9 or 21:9) for hero imagery.
- Deep shadows in the dark wood floors. Let blacks be black.
- Oak surfaces front and centre. The warmth carries the brand.
- Empty desks and quiet corners. Calm, not crowded.
- One human in frame, working. Never staged group shots.
- Coffee, plants, notebooks. Small props that feel lived-in.
- Steam, dust in light beams, condensation. Texture earns trust.

**Avoid this:**

- Bright, even fluorescent lighting that kills depth.
- Generic stock photography of "office workers".
- Forced smiles, posed handshakes, faked collaboration.
- Heavy filters or oversaturated colour grading.
- Cluttered desks or busy backgrounds.
- Cool blue or teal tones that fight the oak warmth.
- Wide-angle distortion that makes the space feel commercial.
- Anything that looks like a property listing or estate-agent shot.

### The grading look

Lift the shadows slightly. Pull warmth into the highlights. Desaturate cool tones. Let the oak surfaces glow. That's what makes the space look like itself.

## 04 / Typography

### The voice on the page

One typeface, used with discipline. **Outfit** carries the entire brand. Clean, modern, confident without shouting. Weight and scale do the work, not decoration.

Source: Google Fonts. Weights used: 300 / 400 / 500 / 600 / 700 / 800.

### Type scale

| Element | Weight | Size | Letter-spacing |
|---|---|---|---|
| Display | 700 | 64–96px | -0.03em |
| Heading 1 | 600 | 44–56px | -0.02em |
| Heading 2 | 600 | 22–28px | — |
| Lead paragraph | 400 | 19–21px / 1.55 line-height | — |
| Body | 400 | 16–17px / 1.7 line-height | — |
| Eyebrow | 600 | 12–13px UPPERCASE | 0.14em |

## 05 / Whitespace

### The space around the words

Whitespace is not empty. It's the brand. It signals confidence, calm, and care. **If a design feels crowded, it has stopped sounding like Worksop Workspace.**

- **Minimum 50%** of any layout (page, slide, social post) should remain empty. White or linen.
- **One idea per view.** Don't compress. If something doesn't fit calmly, it belongs on its own section.
- **Generous margins.** Section padding 100–140px desktop. Container max 980–1200px. Never edge-to-edge text.

## 06 / Logo

### The mark

The Worksop Workspace logo is a navy square mark paired with a charcoal wordmark. It sits on white wherever possible, and is given room to breathe.

**Minimum clear space:** equal to the height of the cap letter on all sides.

### Lockup colours

- **Brand Navy `#203A5B`** — square mark
- **Charcoal `#2B2B2B`** — wordmark

### Logo file

Always reproduce from `/assets/img/logo.png` (or wherever the canonical file currently lives — see [`STRUCTURE.md`](../../STRUCTURE.md)). Never recreated. Never recoloured.

### Approved backgrounds

- On White — primary use
- On Linen — secondary, soft warmth
- On Oak — works in graphics and merchandise

### Dark backgrounds

The standard logo doesn't sit well on dark backgrounds. The navy mark loses contrast against espresso. **A white reversed version of the logo should be commissioned** for use on dark sections of the website and signage.

## 07 / Components

### Buttons

- **Primary (Clay).** Only one per view, reserved for the most important action.
- **Secondary (Espresso outline).** Supporting actions.

No gradients. No drop shadows. No heavy borders.

### Cards & pricing tiles

Structured. Calm. White background, soft border. Heading + lead price + short detail. See `/membership` on the live site for the current canonical implementation.

## 08 / Voice & Tone

### How we sound

Simple. Straightforward. Friendly. We write like Connor talks: direct, clear, no waffle. We speak to people like adults who know what they want, and make it easy for them to say yes.

### We are

- **Bubbly.** Warm energy, genuinely welcoming.
- **Characterful.** Not bland, not generic.
- **A good listener.** We hear what people need.
- **Community-minded.** Interested in our members.
- **Quietly confident.** We don't need to shout.

### We are not

- **Corporate.** No stiff language or buzzwords.
- **Salesy.** No pressure, no urgency tactics.
- **Try-hard.** We don't chase trends.
- **Generic.** Local business, local voice.
- **Exclusive.** Anyone serious about work fits.

### Words to avoid

*"Act now"* · *"Limited time only"* · *"Don't miss out"* · *"Game-changer"* · *"Synergy"* · *"Best-in-class"* · *"Unlock your potential"*.

Anything that sounds like marketing rather than a conversation.

### The "Get shit done" sign

Lives in the **physical space** and on **Instagram only**. Not in web copy, paid ads, or anywhere customers see before they're emotionally bought in. The site stays punchy-but-not-sweary so it ranks on Google, runs ads cleanly, and reads professionally to a corporate lead booking a meeting room.

## 09 / Application — Do & Don't

### Do

- Lead with white. Let the page breathe.
- Use one accent (Clay) per view, on the most important action.
- Set type in Outfit. Weight does the heavy lifting, not size alone.
- Shoot photography in natural light with warm tones and oak front-and-centre.
- Use Espresso for cinematic dark sections. Let blacks be true black.
- Bring Sage in through plants, soft furnishings, and styling props.
- Write like you'd talk to a neighbour. Plain, clear, kind.

### Don't

- Fill the page with dark colour. Use Espresso to anchor, not to dominate.
- Pull Brand Navy out of the logo. It stays reserved for the mark.
- Stack two or more bright accents in the same view.
- Use gradients, drop shadows, or heavy borders.
- Use cool-toned or generic stock imagery that fights the oak warmth.
- Cram sections together. If it feels crowded, cut something.
- Use sales language, urgency, or pressure tactics.
- Photograph the space under fluorescent or evenly-lit conditions.
