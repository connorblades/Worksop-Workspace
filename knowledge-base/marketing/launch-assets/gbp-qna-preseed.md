# Google Business Profile Q&A Pre-seed

**Worksop Workspace** · Launch-readiness asset

Ten questions and answers to post to the Q&A section of the Google Business Profile listing. Pre seeding answers the most common queries before real visitors ever have to ask. Real visitors then either upvote an existing answer or feel reassured enough not to ask anything.

---

## How to post these

1. Sign into the Google Business Profile dashboard on a personal Google account that is **not** an owner of the listing (the listing owner cannot post questions, only answer them).
2. Open the public listing on Google Maps.
3. Use the "Ask a question" function and post one of the ten questions below.
4. Sign back into the owner account and answer the question using the matching answer below. This puts the response under "Answered by the owner", which is the strongest signal both to Google and to readers.
5. Repeat across the ten questions, ideally spread over two or three days rather than all in one sitting.

Keep each answer short. Two or three sentences. Link to one relevant page on worksopworkspace.com where it helps. Do not stuff keywords.

## The ten

### Q1. How much does a day pass cost?

**A.** A day pass at Worksop Workspace is £12. It covers any weekday from 8am to 5pm, fibre Wi Fi, tea and coffee, free parking on site, and the quiet call booth for phone calls. No need to book, walk in and pay on the day. More on the pricing page: https://worksopworkspace.com/membership

---

### Q2. Do I need to be a member to use the meeting room?

**A.** No. The meeting room is open to anyone to book at £20 per hour, or £75 for a half day and £140 for a full day. It seats up to ten, has a screen for video calls and fibre Wi Fi. Book on the meeting room page: https://worksopworkspace.com/meeting-room

---

### Q3. Is there free parking?

**A.** Yes. Free on site parking for members, day pass visitors and meeting room bookings. No time limit, no permit. The car park sits right next to the entrance on Carlton Road.

---

### Q4. What broadband speed do you have?

**A.** A 900 megabit dedicated fibre line in the building. Video calls, large file uploads and remote desktop work without buffering. The line is checked every morning.

---

### Q5. Can I bring my child?

**A.** The coworking floor is a working environment and is not set up for children. For a one off school holiday situation, message Connor at hello@worksopworkspace.com and we will work something out. We are not a creche, but we will not turn a parent away over a single half day with no other option.

---

### Q6. Is the recording studio open yet?

**A.** Not yet. The studio is part of Phase 3 of the building and will open later in 2026. Drop your email on the waiting list at https://worksopworkspace.com/podcast-studio and we will let you know the week before bookings open.

---

### Q7. What is the closest train station?

**A.** Worksop railway station is an eight minute walk from the front door. Worksop sits on the Sheffield to Lincoln line and the Robin Hood Line to Mansfield and Nottingham. The bus stop on Carlton Road is one minute from the door.

---

### Q8. Do you offer virtual office services?

**A.** Not at the moment. Our focus for the first year is the physical workspace. If a registered address and mail handling service is what you need, we will signpost you to a local provider, send an email to hello@worksopworkspace.com and we will help.

---

### Q9. What are your opening hours?

**A.** Monday to Friday, 8am to 5pm. Bank holidays closed. Member access outside those hours will be available later in 2026 when Phase 2 of the building comes online. Members on the waiting list will be told the week before extended access goes live.

---

### Q10. Can I take a phone call from a hot desk?

**A.** Quick calls at the desk are fine. For anything confidential or longer than a few minutes, step into the call booth, which is soundproofed and on the same floor as the open coworking area. The booth is included in the day pass and every membership tier.

## A note on tone

These are pre seed answers, but they read as if a real owner sat down to answer real questions. Plain English. Specific numbers. No marketing phrases. No upselling. Where the answer is "no", say no, then say what the alternative is. The Q&A section is a trust signal, and trust comes from honesty more than from polish.

---

## Maintenance

- Every quarter, check the Q&A section. Real visitors will post real questions. Answer within 48 hours, same standard as reviews.
- If a real question makes one of the pre seed Q&As redundant or out of date, edit the answer. Real owner responses can be updated.
- If a pricing change happens (such as the May 2026 meeting room reprice from £30 to £20), update Q2 the same day.
- If Phase 2 or Phase 3 opens, update Q6 and Q9 the same day.
