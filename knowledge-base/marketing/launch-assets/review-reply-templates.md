# Review Reply Templates

**Worksop Workspace** · Launch-readiness asset

Templates for responding to Google reviews. Use these as a starting point. Always personalise: use the reviewer's first name and reference the specific thing they mentioned.

---

## The rules

1. **Respond to every review within 48 hours.** Positive and negative. The reply matters more than the review itself, because future prospects read both.
2. **Use the reviewer's first name.** It signals you actually read the review.
3. **Mention the specific thing they noted.** "Glad the booth worked for you" is stronger than "glad you enjoyed your visit". Google reads the reply and the specificity reinforces the topic of the review.
4. **Keep it short.** Two or three sentences for positive replies. Three or four for negative.
5. **Never argue with a negative review in public.** Acknowledge, apologise where fair, offer to take it offline. The reply is for the next prospect, not for the reviewer.
6. **Sign with a first name.** Connor or whoever is on duty. Not "the Worksop Workspace team".

## Negative review templates (5)

For each, the structure is the same:
1. Acknowledge the reviewer's first name.
2. Acknowledge the specific issue.
3. Apologise where fair, without grovelling or denying.
4. Offer to take it offline with a direct route.
5. Sign with a real first name.

### Template 1. Parking complaint (full, hard to find a space, blocked in, ticketed off site)

> Hi {{first_name}}. Sorry the parking did not work on the day you came in. The on site spaces are free for members and day pass visitors and we try to keep them flowing, but on a busy day they can fill up. If you would like to come back and try again, drop me an email at hello@worksopworkspace.com and I will make sure there is a space reserved for you. Connor

## Reviews that need a manager call, not a Google reply

Some reviews should not be answered with a public template. Escalate to a phone call, then post a short public reply once the call has happened.

- Accusations of discrimination, harassment or unsafe behaviour. Phone the reviewer the same day. Post a public reply only after the call has happened, and only the reply: "Hi {{first_name}}, thanks for raising this. We have spoken on the phone and are looking into it properly. Connor."
- Health and safety complaints (fire, electrical, slips, trips). Phone same day, document in the accident and incident log, then reply briefly in public.
- Disputes about money over £50. Phone same day. Resolve before posting any public reply.
- Reviews that look like they are about a different business. Phone or email if a contact is on the profile. Ask if it could be a mistaken business listing. Do not respond publicly until you have checked.

## Format checklist before posting any reply

- [ ] Reviewer's first name used.
- [ ] Specific issue named.
- [ ] Reply is under 60 words for positive, under 90 for negative.
- [ ] Sign off with a first name, not "the team".
- [ ] No emoji, no exclamation marks, no marketing phrases.
- [ ] No em dashes, no en dashes. Full stops only.
- [ ] Public reply not used to argue, deny or blame.
- [ ] If anything needs to go offline, hello@worksopworkspace.com is the route.

---

## Monthly review hygiene

Once a month:
- Pull the review list from the GBP dashboard.
- Spot any reply that was missed in the 48 hour window. Reply now, late is better than never.
- Spot any pattern in the complaints (three "parking" mentions in a month means the parking is becoming a real issue, not a one off).
- Pull a one line insight from the reviews into the monthly operations note.
