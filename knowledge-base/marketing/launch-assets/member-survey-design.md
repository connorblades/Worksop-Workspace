# Member Survey Design

**Worksop Workspace** · Phase 9 pre-launch asset

A 10 question survey to run with members after 30 days of operation. Built to generate one citation worthy insight per quarter from real Worksop Workspace member data. Original data is the kind of content local press and AI answer engines link to without being asked.

---

## When to run

**First run:** the start of the second month of operation. Approximately Monday 6 July 2026 if launch hits 1 June 2026 as planned.

**Recurring:** every quarter thereafter, on the first Monday of the month, in the second month of the quarter. So:

- **Run 1:** 6 July 2026 (first run, post launch)
- **Run 2:** 5 October 2026
- **Run 3:** 5 January 2027
- **Run 4:** 5 April 2027

Each run takes a member roughly four minutes to complete. We aim for a 60%+ response rate. With 30 active members, that is 18 responses, which is enough for a usable headline number for a blog post or a press pitch.

---

## How to deliver

Use Google Forms (free, fast, lets us export to CSV and chart easily). Save the form at a shareable link. Send the link by:

1. Email to every active member, from Connor, with a one paragraph why.
2. Short SMS reminder 5 days after the email, only to non responders.
3. Printed card at reception for two weeks alongside the email, with a QR code to the form.

Close the survey two weeks after the first email goes out. Anyone who fills it in after the close is welcome, but the dataset is locked at the close date.

**Anonymity:** the survey is anonymous by default. Optional name and email fields at the bottom for members who would like a follow up call. This balances response rate with the option of a deeper conversation later.

## The 10 questions

### Q1. How did you first find Worksop Workspace?

Multiple choice, single answer.

- Walked or drove past the building
- Google search
- Recommended by a friend or colleague
- Social media (Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn)
- Local press article
- Through a sponsor (Bullseye Properties or South Yorkshire Property Buyers)
- Other (text box)

**Why it matters:** tells us which marketing channel is actually working. Drives the marketing spend decision for the next quarter.

---

### Q2. Where do you live?

Short text box, town only. Postcode optional.

**Why it matters:** the catchment heatmap. Real data on whether the 15 mile radius assumption is correct. Likely citation worthy in a "where members commute from" press pitch.

---

### Q3. Before Worksop Workspace opened, where did you do this work?

Multiple choice, multiple answers allowed.

- Working from home
- Working from a coffee shop or cafe
- Working from a public library
- Working from a different coworking space (please name)
- A serviced office in Worksop or nearby
- Out on the road (vehicle, customer site)
- Did not have a regular working environment
- Other (text box)

**Why it matters:** tells us which option Worksop Workspace is replacing for each member. The "WFH alternative" share is the headline for one quarterly blog post.

---

### Q4. What broadband speed do you have at home?

Multiple choice, single answer.

- Under 30 Mbps
- 30 to 100 Mbps
- 100 to 300 Mbps
- 300 to 1000 Mbps
- Over 1000 Mbps
- Do not know
- Not applicable (do not work from home)

**Why it matters:** the Bassetlaw broadband angle, with real numbers. Headline citation: "X% of our members have under 100 Mbps broadband at home." This is exactly the kind of stat local press will quote.

---

### Q5. How many miles do you travel one way to get to Worksop Workspace?

Multiple choice, single answer.

- Under 1 mile
- 1 to 5 miles
- 5 to 10 miles
- 10 to 15 miles
- Over 15 miles

**Why it matters:** catchment validation and the case for a town centre over a business park location. Headline citation: "Worksop Workspace draws members from an average X mile catchment."

---

### Q6. How do you usually travel to Worksop Workspace?

Multiple choice, single answer.

- Drive
- Walk
- Bus
- Train
- Cycle
- Mixed (most often by [text box])

**Why it matters:** mode of transport drives the parking, the bus stop and the train station story. Useful for the "free parking" angle vs the "near the station" angle.

---

### Q7. Roughly how many days a month do you currently use Worksop Workspace?

Multiple choice, single answer.

- 1 to 2 days
- 3 to 5 days
- 6 to 10 days
- 11 to 15 days
- 16 to 20 days
- Over 20 days

**Why it matters:** the usage curve. Tells us which membership tier is actually right for which kind of user. Drives any pricing decision conversation.

---

### Q8. What do you mainly use the space for?

Multiple choice, multiple answers allowed.

- Focused individual work (writing, design, code, admin)
- Phone or video calls with clients or colleagues
- Meetings in person with clients
- Tradesperson admin (quotes, invoices, scheduling)
- A change of scene from working from home
- Networking and meeting other members
- Other (text box)

**Why it matters:** validates the audience pages on the website. If "tradesperson admin" is 20% of responses, the `/for-tradespeople` page is doing its job.

---

### Q9. What is the single biggest reason you keep coming back?

Open text. One sentence.

**Why it matters:** the single most useful question in the survey. Member words are testimonial gold and quoting them (with permission) is the strongest possible website copy and AEO citation source. Always ask permission to quote a specific response.

---

### Q10. Would you recommend Worksop Workspace to a friend or colleague?

Single answer, 0 to 10 scale (Net Promoter Score format).

Follow up text box: "What would make this score higher?"

**Why it matters:** the NPS number, tracked quarter by quarter, is the single best lagging indicator that the business is healthy. The follow up text box is where the next quarter's improvements come from.

## How to analyse the result

Within two weeks of the survey closing:

1. Export the Google Form responses to CSV.
2. Open in a spreadsheet. Build five summary charts.
3. Pull the one most surprising headline number. That is the citation worthy stat.
4. Write a 600 to 900 word blog post titled along the lines of "What 30 days of Worksop Workspace data tells us about working in Bassetlaw" (adjust headline and word count by run). Publish on the blog.
5. Send the headline number and the blog link to the press contacts in `press-pitches.md` as a follow up. Two sentence email, not a full pitch.

---

## What we are looking for in each run

The first run is exploratory. We do not yet know what the data will say. The strongest signals to watch for:

- **Q3 (where they came from):** what proportion came from working from home? From a coffee shop? From a different coworking space? The headline "X% of our members were previously working from a coffee shop" tells the WFH alternative story.
- **Q4 (broadband):** the proportion of members with under 100 Mbps. Underpins the broadband cornerstone post.
- **Q5 (miles travelled):** the average. Validates the catchment assumption. If members average 11 miles each way, the catchment is wider than we assumed.
- **Q9 (single biggest reason):** the words that recur. Build the next round of website copy around the words members use.
- **Q10 (NPS):** the actual score. Anything above +40 is strong. Above +60 is excellent. Below +20 needs investigation.

---

## What this survey is not for

- **Operational feedback** (the lights, the heating, the cleaning). Those belong in a different one question check in, run more frequently.
- **Pricing research.** Members do not give honest pricing answers to surveys, ever. Pricing decisions stay with the founder and the data we already have from competitor research.
- **Product roadmap brainstorming.** The space has a clear phase 1, 2 and 3 plan. Member surveys can validate or challenge that plan but should not generate it.

---

## After the fourth run

Four runs of the same survey gives a year of quarterly data. Plot the trends. Where has the catchment moved? Has NPS improved? What proportion of members are now repeat referrals?

Use the year of data as the basis for:

- A cornerstone "Worksop Workspace year one" blog post.
- A local press pitch with twelve months of insight.
- A decision on which audience pages to expand, retire, or split.

The fifth survey run, in July 2027, should also evolve. By then we will know which questions earned the data and which did not. Drop the questions that did not produce anything. Add new questions where the year one data raised new questions.
