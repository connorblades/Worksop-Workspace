## Notes on the ask

- **Body page beats footer.** A sentence in the body of an "about" or "community" page is what Google treats as an editorial link. A logo and link in the footer of every page on the site is what Google treats as a paid placement, even when it is not. The ranking value is much lower.
- **One sentence, not a paragraph.** Easy yes. Easy to fit. Easy to maintain.
- **Anchor text matters.** "Worksop Workspace" or "the Worksop Workspace" or the URL itself. Do not ask the partner to anchor the link as "best coworking space Worksop" or any keyword stuffed phrase. That looks like SEO link building (because it is), and is a quality signal in the wrong direction.
- **Reciprocity.** Both partners are already on `/sponsors`. The reciprocity is fine. Reciprocal links between three or four genuinely related local businesses are not a pattern Google flags.
- **What not to ask for.** Do not ask for a guest post, a press release, a banner ad, or a placement in a regular newsletter. Those are paid asks dressed up as favours. The right size of ask for a founding sponsor is one sentence on one page.

## After the ask is fulfilled

- Send a thank you. One line, by email, same day the link goes live.
- Note the live URL in `citation-tracker-template.md` under a new "backlinks" section so it can be checked in the quarterly audit.
- If the link disappears later, send a single polite "looks like the link came off, no drama, let me know if it was deliberate" email. If it was unintentional, the partner will reinstate. If it was deliberate, that is also fine.
