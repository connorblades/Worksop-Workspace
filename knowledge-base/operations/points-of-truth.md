## Section 2: The People

### Founder & Director — Connor Blades

Connor Blades is the Founder and Director of Worksop Workspace. He is also Founder and Director of Bullseye Properties Ltd (https://www.bullseyeproperties.co.uk/our-story), a property business he has run for nearly three years.

His background spans engineering, co-founding and working within other businesses, management, leadership positions, and working across different countries — including experience in the Philippines, where he observed dedicated co-working culture first-hand. That exposure to how people work abroad — particularly the concept of dedicated desk spaces — directly informed the vision for Worksop Workspace.

Connor was born and raised in Worksop and has lived here all his life. Worksop Workspace is a personal project as much as a commercial one: he saw a gap that affected people he knew and a community he cares about.

### Why This Exists

Worksop Workspace was born from a simple observation made from multiple angles:

- Builders working from their van just to get some quiet
- Parents doing Zoom calls from a friend's house or a library
- Remote workers taking over coffee shop tables without a proper space to focus
- People moving from a three-bedroom to a four-bedroom house just to have a home office
- People building sheds in their garden so they have somewhere that feels like work

Coming back to the UK after working overseas, Connor noticed that the major cities had co-working spaces, but there was nothing in between — nothing for the people in places like Worksop. No dedicated space. No community. No solution.

Worksop Workspace is that solution.

### Team

At this stage, Worksop Workspace is run entirely by Connor Blades. Staff and additional key people will be brought in as the business grows through its phases.

Worksop Workspace is not a business park. It is not a traditional business centre. It sits somewhere between a coffee shop and a workspace — and that is the point.

Depending on what a member needs on any given day, they can choose:

A prospective member can:

---

### Onboarding Journey

1. **Initial conversation** — understand what they need: private office, dedicated desk, or flexible hot desk access
2. **Match to the right plan** — based on their working style and the pricing model that suits them
3. **Tour of the space** — come in, see it in person, ask questions
4. **Sign up** — subscription set up via Stripe (direct debit for weekly rolling plans, card for day passes and hourly bookings)

---

### First Day

No formal induction required — it is not a gym. Members are shown around, connected to the Wi-Fi, and then they simply sit down and get on with their work. That is the entire point.

---

### Member App

Members get access to a dedicated app where they can:
- Book desks, rooms, and sessions
- Check real-time availability
- Access their QR code to enter and exit the building
- Raise any issues or complaints
- Manage their membership

Check-in and check-out is automatic when the QR code is scanned, giving the admin system a live view of occupancy at all times (for fire safety and compliance purposes).

---

### Cancellation

Members can cancel directly through their app portal. Cancellation deactivates the account. No excessive notice periods — the system is designed to be as flexible as the membership itself.

## Section 5: Areas & Markets

### Primary Audience

The ideal Worksop Workspace member is someone who works from home but finds home unworkable — or undesirable — as a working environment.

They might be:
- A remote employee who works from their kitchen table surrounded by kids and household distractions
- A tradesperson or sole trader who takes calls and does admin from their van because they have no office
- A freelancer who bounces between coffee shops looking for focus and Wi-Fi
- A small business owner with a startup who doesn't need — or can't afford — a full office
- Someone who works from home and wants a bit more community and professional structure to their day

The common thread: **they need somewhere that feels like work, without the overhead of a full office.**

---

### Target Demographics

- **Age:** 30–50
- **Employment type:** Self-employed, remote employees, sole traders, small teams (1–4 people)
- **Mindset:** Execution-focused — they come to do work, not to network or be sold to
- **Geography:** Worksop town centre and within a 10-mile radius (Retford, Dinnington, Kiveton, surrounding villages)

---

### Positioning

Worksop Workspace occupies a gap in the market that does not currently exist locally:

> *"A blend between a coffee shop and a business centre."*

It is not a full business park. It is not a traditional serviced office. It is not a café. It is a purposeful hybrid — accessible, flexible, and community-led — built for people who are serious about their work but don't want the formality or cost of a conventional office.

## Section 9: Brand Guardrails

### What Worksop Workspace Will Never Be

**Not a networking hub.**
People come here to execute on their work — not to pitch, sell, or prospect. Members who use the space to solicit other members for business will not be welcomed back. This is not a business networking event. It is a workplace.

**Not political.**
No political affiliations, commentary, or associations. The brand stays neutral and focuses entirely on work, community, and the local area.

**Not a pressure environment.**
No competition between members. No feeling that someone is going to interrupt your focus to hand you a business card. No sales pressure from the space itself. It should feel as low-friction as walking into a coffee shop.

**Not a youth club.**
The space has a clear purpose: work. Members are welcome to be sociable, have a conversation, read a book, or take a break — but never to the detriment of other people's focus. The environment must remain professional and calm.

**Not a billboard for other brands.**
No sponsors at this stage. Worksop Workspace is a single brand. The space is clean, consistent, and built around its own identity.

---

### What We Protect

**The environment.** The experience of coming to Worksop Workspace — quiet when you need it, community when you want it — must be protected at all costs. One bad actor can ruin it for everyone, and that will never be tolerated.

**The pricing integrity.** Pricing is fixed. Introductory offers are available at launch. Beyond that, discounting is not the model. Incentives (e.g. a free day pass for a Google review) are the exception, not the rule.

**The promise.** We will never promise business outcomes. We will never guarantee that someone will make more sales, meet the right contacts, or grow their business as a result of being a member. What we guarantee is this: a focused, professional, welcoming space where people can get their work done.

---

### Incentive Policy

Members and visitors who leave a Google review will receive a free day pass as a thank-you. This is a community gesture, not a transactional exchange.
