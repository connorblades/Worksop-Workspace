# Accident and Incident Report

**Worksop Workspace** · Operations · Health & Safety · *Phase 1 reference*

Status: Draft v1 — needs Connor review · Last updated: 15 May 2026

---

The template used to record every accident, near-miss, and incident at Worksop Workspace. Paired with the paper accident book at reception (use both — the paper book is the legal record, this template is the audit trail copy in Drive).

The reference policies are [`./health-and-safety-policy.md`](./health-and-safety-policy.md) and [`./first-aid-policy.md`](./first-aid-policy.md). The SOP is [`../sops/sop-011-first-aid-and-injury.md`](../sops/sop-011-first-aid-and-injury.md).

---

## 1. When to use this template

Use this for every:

- **Accident** — anyone is hurt or made unwell at or because of the space
- **Near-miss** — something happened that could have caused injury but didn't
- **Incident** — something happened that needs documenting even if no one was hurt (security, conduct, equipment failure with safety implications)

If unsure, log it. Over-recording is fine; under-recording has consequences.

---

## 2. The template

Copy the block below for each new entry. Filed by date, newest at the top.

```
---

### [YYYY-MM-DD HH:MM] — [One-line summary]

**Type:** Accident / Near-miss / Incident
**Reportable under RIDDOR?** Yes / No / Awaiting determination
**Affected person(s):** [Name(s) and role — member / visitor / staff / contractor]
**Reported by:** [Name and role]
**Recorded by:** [Your name]

#### What happened

[Plain account of what happened, in chronological order. Stick to the
facts. Where you're making an inference, mark it clearly: "Member said
they slipped on a wet floor (no witness)" rather than "member slipped
on wet floor".]

#### Location

[Exact location in the building.]

#### Immediate response

[What you did. First aid given (if any). Whether 999 was called.
Whether they were taken to hospital, by whom, in what vehicle.
Whether they left the space, when, accompanied or not.]

#### Witnesses

[Names and contact details of any witnesses. Note if there were no
witnesses.]

#### Equipment / location condition

[Anything relevant about the scene — wet floor, broken chair, exposed
cable, etc. Photograph and attach via filename reference.]

#### Photos / evidence

[Filenames in Drive's `06-legal-and-compliance/incident-photos/`, or
note "no photos taken".]

#### Follow-up

[What was done after the immediate event — checked on the person the
next day, repaired the chair, contacted the supplier, etc. Date each
follow-up step.]

#### Status

Open / Closed
[Date closed.]

#### Closure notes

[What was learned. What was changed (SOP edit, equipment replacement,
training tweak). Link to the changed doc if applicable.]

---
```

## 3. RIDDOR check

Before closing any entry, check whether it's RIDDOR-reportable:

- Worker killed or seriously injured (specified injury list — major fractures, amputations, loss of sight, scalping, etc.)
- Worker unable to work for over 7 consecutive days because of a workplace injury
- Non-worker (member, visitor) injured and taken directly from the scene to hospital
- A "dangerous occurrence" — e.g. fire causing 24+ hours stoppage, electrical incident, structural failure
- A reportable occupational disease

Full HSE guide: <https://www.hse.gov.uk/riddor/reportable-incidents.htm>

If any of these apply, the director files the report at <https://www.hse.gov.uk/riddor/>:
- Within **10 working days** for most
- **Immediately** by phone for fatalities and specified injuries (0345 300 9923)

The report reference number is added to the relevant entry.

## 4. Retention

Entries are retained for **3 years minimum** from the date of the event. Longer in cases involving:
- A member or visitor who may later bring a claim — 6 years from the event
- A child or young person — until they turn 21 plus 6 years
- An ongoing dispute or insurance claim — until resolution + 6 years

## 5. Worked example

```
---

### 2026-06-12 14:30 — Member slipped on coffee spill near the kitchen

**Type:** Accident
**Reportable under RIDDOR?** No (no hospital attendance, member walked on)
**Affected person:** [Member name redacted in this template]
**Reported by:** Connor Blades
**Recorded by:** Connor Blades

#### What happened

A member walked into the kitchen carrying a laptop. Another member had
spilled coffee on the floor approximately 60 seconds earlier and had
gone to fetch paper towels. There was no wet-floor sign in place. The
incoming member slipped, caught herself on the worktop, did not fall
to the floor. She bumped her knee on the cupboard.

#### Location

Kitchen, by the coffee machine.

#### Immediate response

Member was offered to sit down, ice pack applied to the knee, water
offered. She declined further first aid. The floor was cleaned and
dried immediately. A wet-floor sign was placed.

#### Witnesses

[Two other members named here.]

#### Equipment / location condition

Floor was wet from a coffee spill. No wet-floor sign was in place at
the time. The wet-floor sign is kept in the cleaning cupboard.

#### Photos / evidence

`2026-06-12-kitchen-floor.jpg` in Drive's `incident-photos/`.

#### Follow-up

- Same day: cleaning team briefed to always place a wet-floor sign immediately, even before going to fetch towels.
- Next day: checked on the affected member by phone. No further pain. No further follow-up needed.
- Within 7 days: edited [`../checklists/03-weekly-deep-clean.md`](../checklists/03-weekly-deep-clean.md) and the cleaner brief to include "spill protocol — sign first, towel second".

#### Status

Closed
Closed 2026-06-19.

#### Closure notes

A simple procedural gap. Cleaner brief and weekly checklist now both
say "sign first, towel second". The kitchen now has a small wet-floor
sign stored under the sink (faster to grab than the cleaning cupboard).

---
```

## 6. What this template is NOT

- It is **not** a substitute for the paper accident book. Both are completed.
- It is **not** the place for opinions about who was at fault. Facts only.
- It is **not** shared outside the responsible person except where required (HSE, emergency services, the affected person's own subject access request, insurer in the event of a claim).
