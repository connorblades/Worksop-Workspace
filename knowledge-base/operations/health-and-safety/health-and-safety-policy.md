## Important — what this doc is and isn't

This is the internal source of truth for Worksop Workspace's approach to health and safety. Under the Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974, businesses with five or more employees are required to have a written H&S policy. We currently operate with one director and no employees, so this document is **good practice, not a statutory obligation**, until we hire. We've written it now so the right culture and the right paper trail exist from day one.

When we have five or more employees, this document must be:
- Reviewed by a competent H&S adviser
- Signed and dated by the director
- Brought to the attention of all employees
- Updated at least annually and after any material change

## 1. Our commitment

Worksop Workspace is committed to providing a safe, healthy, and welcoming environment for members, visitors, staff, contractors, and anyone else affected by what we do. We will:

- Comply with all applicable health and safety legislation
- Identify and assess workplace risks, and act on them
- Provide and maintain safe equipment and facilities
- Ensure safe use, handling, storage, and disposal of any hazardous substances
- Provide information, instruction, training, and supervision so that everyone working at or visiting the space can do so safely
- Consult with employees (when we have them) on health and safety matters
- Review this policy at least annually and after any significant change

## 2. Responsibilities

### Director — Connor Blades

The director has ultimate responsibility for health and safety at Worksop Workspace. In Phase 1, Connor:

- Owns the Fire Risk Assessment and general workplace risk assessments
- Owns this policy and the supporting SOPs
- Ensures statutory inspections (electrical, fire safety, gas if applicable) happen on schedule
- Holds the relationship with any retained competent H&S adviser
- Is the first point of contact for any H&S concern from members, visitors, contractors

### Future staff

When we hire, staff are responsible for:

- Taking reasonable care of their own health and safety
- Taking reasonable care of others affected by their work
- Co-operating with the director and following the policies and SOPs
- Reporting hazards, near-misses, incidents, and accidents promptly
- Using equipment and substances correctly and as trained

### Members and visitors

Members and visitors are responsible for taking reasonable care of themselves and others while in the space. The [`../../marketing/members/house-rules.md`](../../marketing/members/house-rules.md) cover the practical expectations.

### Contractors

Contractors are responsible for their own competence, qualifications, insurance, and safe systems of work. See [`./risk-assessment-framework.md`](./risk-assessment-framework.md) §6 for the contractor competence check.

## 4. Risk assessment

We follow the HSE's five-step framework:

1. Identify the hazards
2. Decide who might be harmed and how
3. Evaluate the risks and decide on precautions
4. Record the findings
5. Review and update

Detail in [`./risk-assessment-framework.md`](./risk-assessment-framework.md). Active risk assessments are stored in Drive's `06-legal-and-compliance/risk-assessments/`.

We re-assess after:
- A new piece of equipment is brought into the space
- A new layout or fit-out change
- An accident, near-miss, or material incident
- A staff change
- An annual review point

## 5. Reporting and recording

All accidents, incidents, near-misses, and dangerous occurrences are recorded on the [`./accident-and-incident-report.md`](./accident-and-incident-report.md). Records are kept for **three years minimum** from the date of the entry (HSE guidance).

RIDDOR-reportable events are filed with the HSE within the statutory window — see [`../sops/sop-011-first-aid-and-injury.md`](../sops/sop-011-first-aid-and-injury.md) §8.

## 6. Training and competence

| Person | Required competence |
|---|---|
| Director | Aware of duties under the Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974. Refresher on appointing-officer responsibilities every 3 years. |
| Staff (when hired) | Induction including H&S basics on day 1. First-aid trained (at-work, 1-day minimum) before working a shift solo. Manual-handling refresher annually. |
| Contractors | Hold relevant trade qualifications and current public liability insurance (minimum £2m). Evidence checked before engagement. |

Phase 1 — Connor holds **an at-work first aid certificate** (Emergency First Aid at Work, 1-day, valid 3 years). Refresh date is logged in Drive's `06-legal-and-compliance/certificates/`.

When we have employees, we'll consult on H&S matters either directly (informal, regular check-ins given team size) or through a formal employee representative arrangement if the team grows beyond ~10. In Phase 1, the consultation channel is the standing item on monthly business reviews — [`../checklists/04-monthly-business-review.md`](../checklists/04-monthly-business-review.md).

## 9. Insurance

Worksop Workspace holds:

- **Public liability insurance** — minimum £5m (held; policy reference in Drive's `06-legal-and-compliance/insurance-summary.md`)
- **Employers' liability insurance** — minimum £5m (required from the moment we have an employee; obtained before first hire)
- **Property insurance** — covering our own equipment, fit-out, and contents
- **Business interruption cover** — included where the underwriter offers

Members are responsible for insuring their own equipment, laptops, and personal effects (set out in [`../legal-and-compliance/terms-of-business.md`](../legal-and-compliance/terms-of-business.md) §13).
