## 1. Our commitment

Worksop Workspace will ensure that adequate first aid provision is available at all times the space is open. We will:

- Have at least one trained first aider on shift whenever the space is staffed
- Maintain a stocked, signed first aid kit in a known location
- Maintain a working accident and incident reporting system
- Respond promptly to first aid needs without overstepping the limits of training
- Review provision against the actual usage patterns annually

This is consistent with the Health and Safety (First-Aid) Regulations 1981.

---

## 2. First aid needs assessment

We've assessed first aid needs based on:

- The nature of the work (light office and hospitality — low-hazard)
- The size of the workforce (Phase 1: 1; Phase 2+: anticipated 2–4)
- The number of members and visitors typically on site (Phase 1: 5–20 people)
- The premises (single ground floor, one accessible route, short distance to emergency services)

The conclusion: one **Emergency First Aider at Work (EFAW)** trained person on shift is sufficient. We may upgrade to **First Aider at Work (FAW)** when team size grows past four employees.

The full needs assessment lives in Drive's `06-legal-and-compliance/first-aid-needs-assessment.md`.

## 3. The first aid kit

Location: behind reception, on the second shelf, in a clearly marked green case. Contents are based on BS 8599-1 (Small workplace) but adapted for our setting:

- 1 guidance leaflet
- 20 individually wrapped sterile plasters (assorted sizes)
- 2 sterile eye pads with bandage
- 2 individually wrapped triangular bandages
- 6 safety pins
- 6 medium sterile dressings (12cm × 12cm)
- 2 large sterile dressings (18cm × 18cm)
- 1 pair disposable gloves (latex-free) — and a second pair as spare
- 30 antiseptic wipes (alcohol-free)
- 1 burn dressing (10cm × 10cm)
- 1 foil blanket
- 1 resuscitation face shield
- 1 conforming bandage (7.5cm)
- 1 instant ice pack (single-use)
- Scissors (round-tipped) and tweezers

We do **not** stock or dispense medication of any kind — no paracetamol, no antihistamines, no anything. This is the rule and it is firm.

## 4. Stock checks

- Daily — quick visual check that the kit is in place and unsealed items haven't been used (part of [`../sops/sop-001-opening-the-space.md`](../sops/sop-001-opening-the-space.md))
- Weekly — open the kit, check expiry dates, restock anything used (part of [`../checklists/03-weekly-deep-clean.md`](../checklists/03-weekly-deep-clean.md))
- Quarterly — replace any item within 90 days of expiry

The kit is restocked from a held inventory of spares (in the staff cupboard) and from the local pharmacy or our medical supplier (in Drive's suppliers register).

## 6. Mental health first aid

Mental health first aid (MHFA) is not a statutory requirement but is good practice for a hospitality / coworking environment. We aim to have at least one MHFA-trained person on the team within the first year of operating. Until then:

- Staff treat anyone in mental distress with the same calm-and-respectful approach as a physical injury — listen, offer water and a quiet space, ask if there's someone they want us to call
- Signposting:
    - Samaritans 116 123 (free, 24/7)
    - NHS 111 for urgent non-emergency
    - 999 for life-threatening
    - Local crisis service contact in Drive's emergency contacts

## 7. Records

Every first aid event is recorded on the [`./accident-and-incident-report.md`](./accident-and-incident-report.md). Records held for 3 years minimum from the date of entry.

---

## 8. Confidentiality

First aid records contain personal data and possibly special-category data (health information). They are:

- Stored securely (paper book locked behind reception during the day, in the safe overnight; digital copies in Drive's restricted folder)
- Accessed only by the director and any future named H&S lead
- Not shared with third parties except where required (HSE, emergency services, the affected person's own request)

See [`../legal-and-compliance/data-protection-and-gdpr.md`](../legal-and-compliance/data-protection-and-gdpr.md) for the wider data-protection framework.
