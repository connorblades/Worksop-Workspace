# Risk Assessment Framework

**Worksop Workspace** · Operations · Health & Safety · *Phase 1 reference*

Status: Draft v1 — needs Connor review · Last updated: 15 May 2026

---

The framework Worksop Workspace uses to identify, evaluate, and act on workplace risks. Sits behind the [`./health-and-safety-policy.md`](./health-and-safety-policy.md). Live risk assessments live in Drive at `06-legal-and-compliance/risk-assessments/`.

We follow the HSE's five-step method: <https://www.hse.gov.uk/simple-health-safety/risk/>

---

## 1. The five steps

1. **Identify the hazards.** Walk the space, look at the work, ask people, review past incidents.
2. **Decide who might be harmed and how.** Staff, members, visitors, contractors, vulnerable persons.
3. **Evaluate the risks and decide on precautions.** What's already in place? What more is reasonably practicable?
4. **Record the findings and implement them.** Written assessment, agreed actions with dates.
5. **Review and update.** At least annually, and after any change.

---

## 2. The scoring matrix

We score risk as **likelihood × severity** on a 1–5 scale.

| Score | Likelihood | Severity |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Rare / very unlikely | Trivial — minor inconvenience |
| 2 | Unlikely | Minor — first aid, no time off |
| 3 | Possible | Moderate — over-3-day absence or hospital visit |
| 4 | Likely | Major — major injury, RIDDOR |
| 5 | Almost certain | Catastrophic — fatality, multiple major injuries |

| Risk score | Action |
|---|---|
| 1–4 | Low — usual controls, monitor |
| 5–9 | Medium — additional controls warranted; review within 12 months |
| 10–15 | High — significant controls needed; review within 3 months |
| 16–25 | Unacceptable — must reduce or stop the activity |

## 4. Standard risk assessments to maintain

Phase 1 — we should have a written risk assessment for at least:

- **General workplace** — desks, chairs, floors, lighting, ventilation, DSE basics
- **Kitchen / refreshments** — coffee machine, kettle, knives, hot liquids, slip risk
- **Cleaning** — chemicals (COSHH-style), wet floors, manual handling
- **Electrical equipment** — member devices, our equipment, charging points, cable management
- **Fire** — covered by the standalone FRA (Drive's `06-legal-and-compliance/fire-risk-assessment/`)
- **Lone working** — when someone is on shift solo
- **Contractor work on site** — generic; specific contractors have their own RAMS
- **Member behaviour / conflict** — pairs with [`../sops/sop-018-complaints-handling.md`](../sops/sop-018-complaints-handling.md) and [`../training/member-conflict-handling.md`](../training/member-conflict-handling.md)
- **Slip / trip / fall** — focus on shared corridors, kitchen, entrance
- **Manual handling** — receiving deliveries, moving furniture

Phase 2+ — additionally:

- **Multi-floor operation** — stairs, lifts (if installed), upper-level evacuation
- **Out-of-hours / 24-7 access** — different risk profile
- **Podcast studio** — equipment, electrical, soundproofing materials
- **Self-catering kitchen** — fuller hazard profile

## 5. Specific risk-assessment notes

### Display screen equipment (DSE)

Members supply their own laptops; DSE assessments aren't statutory for non-employees. For staff (when hired), we will:
- Provide a DSE self-assessment on day 1
- Offer an eye test reimbursement per the DSE Regulations 1992
- Address any reported problem promptly

### COSHH (cleaning chemicals)

We hold safety data sheets for every cleaning product in use. Chemicals are stored in the cleaning cupboard, locked, away from food and refreshment areas. Anyone handling them uses gloves and ventilation. No chemicals are decanted into unlabelled containers.

### Manual handling

The typical heaviest single item is around 15kg (a case of UHT milk or a fresh coffee bean bag). Two-person lifts for anything over 15kg. No lifting from floor to over-shoulder without breaking the lift.

### Lone working

Phase 1 — typically one staff member on shift. The risks are: medical emergency with no one to call 999, hostile encounter with a person on the street, fall or injury with no witness. Controls:
- Phone always on the person
- Connor's mobile is the primary backup
- A neighbour business has been briefed as a friendly second pair of eyes — see Drive's emergency contacts
- The space is on a main road with reasonable foot traffic

## 6. Contractor competence check

Before any contractor works on site we hold:

- Trade qualification(s) appropriate to the work
- Current public liability insurance — minimum £2m
- Method statement and risk assessment for their work, where applicable (RAMS)
- Where required: gas-safe, NICEIC, asbestos training, working-at-height qualification, etc.

A signed contractor agreement (template in Drive's `06-legal-and-compliance/contractor-agreement.md` once populated) sets out the basics.

---

## 7. Review

Each risk assessment is reviewed:

- Annually
- After any incident relating to the assessed activity
- After any change to equipment, layout, materials, people, or methods
- After any change in legislation

The review log is held within each risk assessment file.

---

## 8. When something happens that the risk assessment didn't anticipate

The risk assessment is wrong, or incomplete. Don't sweep that under the rug. Update the assessment within 30 days of the event, with reference to the incident log entry.
