# Lone Working Policy

**Worksop Workspace** · Operations · Health & Safety · *Phase 1 reference*

Status: **Draft v1 — needs Connor review.** Last updated: 16 May 2026.

---

In Phase 1, Connor will frequently be the **only member of staff in the building**. This is lone working. UK health-and-safety law does not prohibit lone working but it does require employers (and the self-employed) to identify the specific risks of working alone and have a written plan for how to handle them. This document is that plan.

This policy will also cover any future staff who work shifts alone.

---

## 1. When lone working applies

- Opening the space in the morning before any member arrives
- Closing the space in the evening after the last member leaves
- Any moment Connor (or future staff) is on the premises with no other member or contractor in the building
- All out-of-hours visits (early-morning admin, late-evening maintenance)
- Cleaning shifts before opening / after closing
- Receiving deliveries when the space is otherwise empty

---

## 2. Specific risks

The risks lone working introduces, on top of normal premises risks:

1. **Medical incident** — slip, trip, fall, illness with no one to help or call for help.
2. **Aggressive intruder or member** — confrontation with no backup.
3. **Manual handling injury** — lifting alone, no one to spot.
4. **Fire or other emergency** — no one to assist evacuation or alert services.
5. **Mental health and isolation** — sustained solo working over long periods.
6. **Lock-in or being trapped** — door fault, lift trap (when phase 2 adds upstairs).
7. **Cash or valuables handling** — risk of targeting around closing time.

Each of these is also addressed in the [Risk Assessment Framework](risk-assessment-framework.md).

## 3. Controls

### 3.1 Mobile phone discipline

- **Phone on you at all times** when lone working. Not on a desk in another room — on your person, charged, signal confirmed.
- The phone has emergency services (999), Connor's emergency contact (next of kin), the landlord (Chris Wallace), and 111 NHS non-emergency saved as favourites.
- WhatsApp / Signal: a "check-in" contact (named below) is always live.

### 3.2 Check-in / check-out

When Connor is the only staff member working alone outside member-occupancy hours:

- **On entering** the building outside normal hours: send a "I'm in" message to the nominated check-in contact.
- **On leaving**: send "I'm out" message.
- **No reply within 90 minutes of an expected check-out**: contact escalates — call Connor, if no answer call 999 and the landlord.

**Nominated check-in contact:** [to be confirmed — likely partner / family member / trusted friend with 24/7 phone reach]. Their name and number to be on a card at reception and in this document once confirmed.

### 3.3 Door control

- Lone working **after dark or before sunrise**: the front door is on the latch (one-touch egress, locked from outside). No member or visitor can enter without being buzzed in.
- If anyone unknown buzzes outside published hours, the answer is "we're closed, please come back tomorrow" — no opening the door for unscheduled visitors when alone.

### 3.4 CCTV as backup

- The Ring cameras stream live and are recording during all lone-working hours.
- A motion alert is sent to Connor's phone on the main door — gives advance warning of anyone approaching.

### 3.5 Manual handling

- **Do not move heavy furniture alone.** Wait for a second person, or use the chair-trolley / sack truck.
- Lifting limit when alone: **15kg**. Anything heavier waits.

### 3.6 Personal safety on closing

- The closing routine ([SOP-002](../sops/sop-002-closing-the-space.md)) includes a quick walk of all areas before locking up — done with the phone in hand.
- Cash takings (if any from SumUp on-site) are not left visible. Float is locked away before main door is unlocked for exit.
- After locking up, Connor walks to the car or onwards with phone in hand.

### 3.7 Visiting contractors

- If a contractor is on site while Connor is also alone, that's still effectively lone working from the contractor's perspective and vice versa.
- A contractor doesn't count as a co-worker for check-in purposes. Connor stays in the same general area as them where possible.

## 4. What happens if something goes wrong

### 4.1 Medical incident

- **Severe** (chest pain, suspected stroke, serious bleed, unconsciousness, severe burn): 999 first, check-in contact second. Get yourself to the front of the building if you can, so paramedics can find you.
- **Moderate** (deep cut, fall with possible fracture, suspected concussion): 111, check-in contact, close space, get assessed.
- **Minor**: self-treat from the first aid kit, log it in the accident book the next working day.

### 4.2 Confrontation / intruder

- **Don't engage.** Get behind a locked door (back office, toilet — both lockable from inside).
- 999 immediately.
- Stay on the line with the operator.
- Do not try to retrieve cash, equipment, or anything else.

### 4.3 Fire

- Activate the alarm if not auto-sounding (see [SOP-010](../sops/sop-010-emergency-evacuation.md)).
- Leave by the nearest safe exit.
- Call 999 from outside.
- Account for yourself at the assembly point; tell incoming fire crews that the building should be empty.

## 5. Wellbeing

Sustained solo work over many weeks is hard. Worksop Workspace recognises this and the following are baked into how the business is run, not optional:

- **No working through illness.** If Connor is unwell, the space closes for the day and members are emailed. The recovery cost is lower than the alternative.
- **One full day off per week.**
- **Regular contact** with peer founders / family / mentors outside the business. Coworking has a way of feeling like company while still leaving the lead person isolated — the antidote is deliberate connection.
- **Mental health: keep an eye on it.** Things to watch for: persistent low mood, sleep disruption, withdrawal, irritability. If any of those persist for 2+ weeks, talk to the GP. The business will still be there.
