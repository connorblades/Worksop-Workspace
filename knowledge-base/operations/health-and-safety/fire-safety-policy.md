## Important — what this doc is and isn't

This policy sits **alongside** the statutory Fire Risk Assessment (FRA), which must be carried out for every non-domestic premises under the Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005. The FRA itself lives in Drive's `06-legal-and-compliance/fire-risk-assessment/` and is the legally-required document. This policy is the **internal commitment** that supports the FRA and operationalises it.

---

## 1. Our commitment

Worksop Workspace is committed to preventing fire and to ensuring that, if a fire occurs, the people in the space can leave safely. We will:

- Maintain a current Fire Risk Assessment, carried out by a competent person
- Maintain working fire detection, alarm, and emergency lighting systems
- Maintain clear escape routes at all times
- Train staff in fire response and the evacuation procedure
- Run evacuation drills twice a year, minimum
- Operate within the design occupancy limit of the building
- Maintain the records that fire safety law requires

## 3. The premises and the risks

The space at 30 Carlton Road, Worksop S80 1PH is a converted commercial unit operating as a coworking space. Phase 1 risks identified in the FRA:

- Electrical equipment (laptops, chargers, kitchen appliances, AV)
- Cooking equipment (kettle, coffee machine — no fryer-grade hazard)
- Stored combustibles (paper, packaging, cleaning chemicals)
- Ignition sources (kitchen, electrical panels)
- Vulnerable persons (any member or visitor with reduced mobility — see §6)
- Out-of-hours access (Phase 2+ only — different risk profile)

The FRA lists the controls for each. This policy doesn't restate them; it points to the FRA as the canonical detail.

---

## 4. Detection and alarm

- The space has an addressable fire alarm panel covering all areas
- Smoke detectors in circulation areas and offices
- Heat detectors in the kitchen
- Manual call points (red break-glass) at every fire exit
- Sounders audible from every part of the space
- Strobes (where fitted) to support hearing-impaired members

The panel is serviced annually by a BAFE-registered contractor (in Drive's suppliers register). The in-house quarterly check is [`../checklists/05-quarterly-fire-safety-check.md`](../checklists/05-quarterly-fire-safety-check.md).

---

## 5. Means of escape

- Two final exits — main front door (Carlton Road) and rear fire exit (yard / car park)
- All escape routes are kept clear at all times
- Maximum travel distance to a place of safety is within the FRA's stated limit
- Doors on the escape routes open in the direction of travel where required and open easily from inside without a key
- Emergency lighting covers every escape route and is tested per the quarterly check
- Signage uses the standard running-man pictogram with arrow direction

Means of escape diagrams are posted at the main entrance and inside the meeting room.

---

## 6. Vulnerable persons

We may have members or visitors with mobility, sensory, or cognitive impairments. A Personal Emergency Evacuation Plan (PEEP) is offered to any member who would benefit. The PEEP template is in Drive's `06-legal-and-compliance/peep-template.md`. Phase 1 of the building has a single accessible level (ground floor); the basement and upper floors come into use in Phase 2+ and PEEP planning must be re-done at that point.

---

## 7. Evacuation

The evacuation procedure is [`../sops/sop-010-emergency-evacuation.md`](../sops/sop-010-emergency-evacuation.md). Headlines:

- On alarm, everyone leaves immediately by the nearest fire exit
- Staff sweep the space (toilets, private offices, meeting room) on their way out
- Assembly point: car park, far side from the building, by the streetlight pole
- Head-count using the day's check-in log from the member app
- Don't re-enter until the fire service or competent person says it's safe

Drills run **twice a year** (March and September by default). Records held in Drive's fire safety log.

## 8. Equipment

| Equipment | Standard | Service interval |
|---|---|---|
| Fire alarm panel | BS 5839 | Annual + 6-monthly inspection |
| Smoke / heat detectors | BS 5839 | As part of the annual alarm service |
| Emergency lighting | BS 5266 | Monthly visual; 6-monthly short test; annual 3-hour drain test |
| Extinguishers (water, foam, CO2) | BS EN 3 | Annual service |
| Fire blanket (kitchen) | BS EN 1869 | Annual visual |
| Fire doors | BS 8214 (or equivalent) | 6-monthly self-check, annual external inspection |

All service contractors are in Drive's `07-facilities/suppliers-and-contractors.md`. Service certificates are kept in Drive's fire safety folder.

## 11. Smoking and naked flames

- No smoking inside the building or in the car park within 10m of the entrance
- No candles, incense, oil burners, or naked flames inside the space
- No vaping inside

---

## 12. Hot works

If any contractor needs to undertake hot works (welding, brazing, grinding, soldering producing sparks or significant heat), a Hot Works Permit is required. Template in Drive's `06-legal-and-compliance/hot-works-permit.md` (Tier 2 — not yet populated). Hot works are not anticipated in normal operation.

---

## 13. Records

All fire safety records — service certificates, drill outcomes, in-house checks, the FRA itself, training records — are held in Drive at `06-legal-and-compliance/fire-safety/`. We keep these records for **the lifetime of the business plus 3 years**.

---

## 14. Review

This policy and the FRA are reviewed:

- **Annually** as a minimum
- **After any fire** or near-miss
- **After any change** to the building, layout, occupancy, or operations that might affect fire safety
- **After any change** to relevant legislation
