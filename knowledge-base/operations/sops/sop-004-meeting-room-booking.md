# SOP-004: Meeting Room Booking and Changeover

**Worksop Workspace** · Operations · *Phase 1 reference*

Status: Draft v1 — needs Connor review · Last updated: 15 May 2026

---

The procedure for handling meeting room bookings from enquiry through to changeover. The meeting room is the only bookable enclosed space in Phase 1, so its presentation and reliability matter more than almost anything else we do.

**Price:** £30/hour. **Capacity:** see room spec sheet in Drive's `07-facilities/`. **Bookable by:** members via the app, visitors via phone or in person.

---

## 1. Receiving a booking enquiry

1. Member-app bookings appear automatically on the day's schedule.
2. Phone or walk-in enquiries — capture name, company, date, start time, finish time, headcount, any AV or refreshment requests. Use the script in [`../training/phone-and-enquiry-handling.md`](../../sales/conversion/phone-and-enquiry-handling.md).
3. Check the day's schedule for clashes — open the member app's admin view.
4. Quote the rate. £30/hour, billed in whole hours. Half hours round up.
5. Take the booking on the app on their behalf and process payment by card link.
6. Confirm by email using the meeting-room confirmation template in [`../../sales/communications/email-templates.md`](../../sales/communications/email-templates.md).

---

## 2. The day before

7. Run through the next day's bookings on the app at end-of-day. Note:
   - Who's coming in
   - What time slot
   - Headcount
   - Any AV / refreshment requests
8. Set the room up the way the brief suggests — chair count, table layout (boardroom, U-shape, classroom). Default is boardroom.
9. Power up the screen and confirm a clean HDMI signal from a test laptop.
10. Stock the room — water jug, glasses, notepads, pens, two boxes of tissues.

---

## 3. Thirty minutes before the booking

11. Walk the room. Smell test, light check, temperature check.
12. Wipe the table. Reset chairs. Tidy the cables.
13. Top up the water jug with fresh chilled water.
14. Wake the screen and re-test HDMI.
15. Make sure the meeting-room sign on the door is set to **Available — booked X:XX**.

---

## 4. On arrival

16. Greet the lead person by name. Welcome the group warmly but briefly — they're here to work, not to be entertained.
17. Show them in. Ask if they'd like tea or coffee (members and visitors both get free refreshments — see [`../training/coffee-and-refreshments.md`](../training/coffee-and-refreshments.md)).
18. Explain the basics in under 30 seconds: screen on/off here, Wi-Fi network and password card on the table, toilets out and right, fire exit out and left, and that you'll be at reception if they need anything.
19. Close the door, set the sign to **In use**, and leave them to it.

---

## 5. During the booking

20. Don't interrupt unless they ask. Glass walls give you a sense of the room without being intrusive — read the room from outside.
21. If a question comes up (refreshments, AV, extension), respond promptly but quietly.
22. Don't let other members or visitors knock on the door. They come to you, you handle it.

---

## 6. Extensions and overruns

23. Five minutes before the booked end, glance in. If they look like they're wrapping up, leave them.
24. If it's clear they'll overrun and the next slot is free, offer the extension at the door at the booked end. Charge another hour. Don't quote a half-hour rate.
25. If the next slot is booked, give a quiet five-minute warning two minutes before the end.

---

## 7. Changeover (between back-to-back bookings)

26. Allow **15 minutes** between back-to-back bookings on the schedule. This isn't optional — short changeovers mean rushed cleaning and poor presentation.
27. Walk in the second the previous group has left. Open the door for ventilation.
28. Reset, wipe, restock, re-power. (Steps 11–15 again.)
29. Two minutes before the next slot, set the sign back to **Available — booked X:XX**.

---

## 8. End of day for the room

30. Walk the room as part of [`../checklists/02-daily-closing.md`](../checklists/02-daily-closing.md). Bin emptied, screen off, chairs aligned, table clean, sign reset to **Available**.
31. Charge any chargeable extras to the booking on the app (refreshments are included; printing is chargeable).

---

## Common issues

| Issue | First action |
|---|---|
| Screen won't connect to laptop | Try HDMI cable swap. If still failing, offer the spare AV-USB-C dongle. Log in Drive's `07-facilities/maintenance-log.md`. |
| Booking dispute (who booked when) | The app is the source of truth — show them the booking record. Be calm and matter-of-fact. |
| Group exceeds stated headcount | Tell them in person at the door. The room has a max occupancy for fire safety. See [`./sop-010-emergency-evacuation.md`](./sop-010-emergency-evacuation.md). |
| Spill or stain on the table | Address immediately. Log if the surface is damaged. |
| Refund request | Refer to [`../legal-and-compliance/terms-of-business.md`](../legal-and-compliance/terms-of-business.md) §4. No refund for partly-used bookings; escalate to Connor for goodwill exceptions. |

## Estimated time per booking

| Activity | Time |
|---|---|
| Pre-booking enquiry handling | 5–10 minutes |
| Day-before setup and confirmation | 10 minutes |
| 30-minutes-before final check | 10 minutes |
| On-arrival greet and brief | 5 minutes |
| Changeover (between bookings) | 12 minutes inside a 15-minute slot |
