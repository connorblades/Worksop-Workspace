# SOP-011: First Aid and Minor Injury

**Worksop Workspace** · Operations · *Phase 1 reference*

Status: Draft v1 — needs Connor review · Last updated: 15 May 2026

---

The procedure for responding to a minor injury, medical complaint, or medical emergency in the space. The objective is calm competence: don't pretend to be a paramedic, don't make it worse, don't ignore it.

This SOP is paired with the [`../health-and-safety/first-aid-policy.md`](../health-and-safety/first-aid-policy.md) (the why) and the [`../health-and-safety/accident-and-incident-report.md`](../health-and-safety/accident-and-incident-report.md) (the after).

## 2. Triage in three questions

When someone is hurt or unwell, ask in this order:

1. **Are they conscious and breathing?**
2. **Are they able to speak normally?**
3. **What hurts, and how badly?**

That triage determines which branch below you follow. **Don't move on until question 1 is answered.**

---

## 3. Branch A — Suspected medical emergency

Triggers: unconscious, not breathing normally, chest pain lasting more than a minute, signs of stroke (FAST — face drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty, time), severe bleeding that won't stop with pressure, severe allergic reaction, severe head injury, suspected fracture of a major bone.

4. **Call 999 immediately.** Don't wait to see if it gets worse. Don't call a relative first.
5. Tell the operator: "Worksop Workspace, 30 Carlton Road, Worksop S80 1PH. [What's happening.] [Who's affected.] [Age and sex if known.]"
6. Stay with the person. Talk to them calmly. Don't move them unless they're in immediate danger (e.g. fire).
7. If they're unconscious but breathing — recovery position if trained. If you're not trained, leave them and watch their breathing.
8. If they're not breathing — start CPR if trained. The 999 operator will talk you through it if you're not.
9. Send another person (a member, a colleague) to:
    - Meet the ambulance at the front door
    - Keep the corridor and entrance clear
10. **Don't give food or water. Don't give medication.** Even painkillers.
11. Clear bystanders. The casualty needs space and dignity. *"Could you give us some room — they need air."*
12. When the ambulance arrives, brief the crew with what you observed. Hand over and step back.
13. Log the incident as soon as possible — see section 7.

---

## 4. Branch B — Minor injury (treatable with the first aid kit)

Triggers: shallow cut, graze, bumped head with no loss of consciousness, twisted ankle they can stand on, paper cut, mild burn, blister.

14. Take them somewhere private — the meeting room or quiet corner. Their dignity matters.
15. Ask before touching them or their wound. *"Mind if I take a look?"*
16. Wash your hands. Glove up (gloves in the kit).
17. Treat what's in front of you:
    - **Cut or graze** — clean with a sterile wipe, apply a plaster or dressing
    - **Bump to the head** — ice pack from the freezer, sit them down, monitor for 15 minutes
    - **Mild burn** (red but no blistering) — run under cool water for 10 minutes
    - **Twisted ankle** — sit them down, elevate if possible, ice pack
18. Ask twice: *"Are you OK? Do you want me to call anyone, or get you to a GP?"* Don't decide for them.
19. If they want to carry on with their day, fine. If they want to go home, offer to call them a taxi.

---

## 5. Branch C — Feeling unwell (no injury)

Triggers: faint, dizzy, nauseous, panic, period pain, migraine, hot flush, anxiety attack.

20. Get them to somewhere quieter. The meeting room is good if free.
21. Offer water. Don't offer painkillers or anything else — we don't dispense medication.
22. Sit with them quietly. Don't crowd. Don't ask probing questions.
23. If they need someone collecting them, help arrange that — but on **their** terms, not yours.
24. If symptoms escalate (suddenly worse, faint repeatedly, sudden confusion), treat as branch A and call 999.

---

## 6. Branch D — Someone else's emergency (heard, not seen)

Triggers: a shout from the toilets, a thud from a private office, a member running over saying *"my colleague's collapsed."*

25. Go to them immediately. Knock and call before entering a closed space.
26. Branch into A, B, or C as you find it.
27. If you can't reach them (locked toilet, etc.) — call out to confirm they're conscious. If no response, call 999 immediately and brief them on the situation.

---

## 7. After the event

28. Once the person is stable or has left, log the incident in the [`../health-and-safety/accident-and-incident-report.md`](../health-and-safety/accident-and-incident-report.md) — within 24 hours.
29. Note in the staff handover log (member app):
    - What happened
    - Who was involved (member, visitor, contractor, staff)
    - Time and location
    - What treatment was given
    - Whether 999 was called
    - Whether the casualty was taken to hospital
30. If the incident involved a **member**, check in with them the next day. Short, sincere, no fuss.
31. Restock the first aid kit. Anything used = replaced before the next shift.

---

## 8. RIDDOR-reportable incidents

32. Some incidents must be reported to the Health and Safety Executive under RIDDOR (Reporting of Injuries, Diseases and Dangerous Occurrences Regulations 2013). The main triggers:
    - A worker is killed or seriously injured (specified injury list — major fractures, amputations, etc.)
    - A worker is unable to work for over 7 consecutive days because of a workplace injury
    - A non-worker (member, visitor) is injured and taken directly from the scene to hospital
    - A "dangerous occurrence" — gas leak, structural collapse, electrical incident
33. If anything in the list above happens, **flag to Connor the same day**. Connor files the RIDDOR report at <https://www.hse.gov.uk/riddor/> within 10 working days (or immediately for fatalities and specified injuries).
34. Full guidance: <https://www.hse.gov.uk/riddor/reportable-incidents.htm>

---

## 9. What we don't do

- We don't give out painkillers, even paracetamol.
- We don't drive injured people to A&E ourselves unless 999 specifically recommends it.
- We don't take photos of injuries unless the casualty asks us to.
- We don't post about incidents on social media, ever.
- We don't speculate publicly about cause or fault. The incident report is the only place that gets written down.

## Common issues

| Issue | First action |
|---|---|
| Casualty refuses help and walks off | Document it. *"Member declined first aid offered and left the space at [time]"*. We can't force care. |
| You're not sure if it's 999-worthy | Call 111 — they triage and tell you whether to upgrade. Or err on 999. Better to call and be told to stand down than the reverse. |
| The casualty is a contractor (e.g. cleaner) | Same procedure. Plus notify their employer — the supplier-and-contractor register has the contact. |
| Mass-incident (e.g. multiple people unwell suddenly) | Treat as Branch A. Call 999. Consider evacuation per [`./sop-010-emergency-evacuation.md`](./sop-010-emergency-evacuation.md). |
| You're alone in the building and need help | Phone Connor immediately. Lock the door behind the ambulance leaving rather than leaving the space open with no one in it. |
