# SOP-008: Deliveries and Post

**Worksop Workspace** · Operations · *Phase 1 reference*

Status: Draft v1 — needs Connor review · Last updated: 15 May 2026

---

The procedure for receiving, logging, holding, and handing over deliveries and post that come in for members. This is a low-volume, high-trust task. Getting it wrong costs more than it earns — a missing package is a memorable bad day for a member.

**Scope:** small parcels and letters for active members. Heavy / oversized / signed-for-with-ID items follow special handling.

---

## 1. The general principle

- We accept post and small parcels for **active members** as a courtesy.
- We do **not** accept post or parcels for non-members, ex-members, or unaffiliated parties.
- We do **not** offer a registered office address or virtual-office service in Phase 1. If a member is using us as their registered office, that's on them and we're not contractually their post-handler.
- See [`../legal-and-compliance/terms-of-business.md`](../legal-and-compliance/terms-of-business.md) §3 — "mail/parcel handling beyond standard delivery" is not in the standard offer.

---

## 2. Accepting a delivery at the door

1. Couriers and posties typically arrive between 9am and 2pm. Don't sign for anything without checking the addressee.
2. Read the addressee. If you don't recognise the name, ask the courier: *"Is this for someone working here?"*
3. Cross-check the addressee against the active-member list on the member app.
4. If the addressee is a current active member — **accept**. Sign legibly with your own name.
5. If the addressee is unknown — **refuse** politely. *"Sorry, that's not someone we have here. You'll need to try the addressee directly."*
6. If you're unsure — accept with the courier's notes, then check, and contact the member.

---

## 3. Logging the item

7. Open the parcel log on the member app (admin → Parcels).
8. Record:
    - Member name
    - Time received
    - Courier / carrier
    - Item description (envelope, small box, medium box) — **never open it**
    - Any "Fragile", "Perishable", or "Signed for" markings
    - Your initials
9. Stick a numbered label on the item — sequential from the day's log.

---

## 4. Holding the item

10. Behind reception, on the parcels shelf. Out of public view.
11. Letters in the letter rack, ordered alphabetically by surname.
12. Larger parcels on the shelf, ordered by log number.
13. Fragile or perishable items — flag in the log and prioritise notification.

---

## 5. Notifying the member

14. Send a short notification via the member app (built-in messaging) within 30 minutes of receipt: *"You've had a delivery — pop by reception to collect."*
15. Don't text or call unless the item is perishable, fragile-and-urgent, or marked "Signed for personal delivery."
16. Don't message multiple times if they're not in that day. They'll see it next time they open the app.

---

## 6. Handing over

17. Check the app — confirm they're an active member at the moment of handover. (They may have cancelled between delivery and pickup.)
18. Match the item to the log entry by member name and log number.
19. Hand it over. Mark the log entry as **collected** with timestamp and your initials.
20. Don't ask about the contents. Not our business.

---

## 7. Items not collected after seven days

21. Day 3 — second app message: *"Just a reminder — there's a parcel for you at reception."*
22. Day 5 — direct call.
23. Day 7 — if the member is still active and we still can't get hold of them, hold the item indefinitely but log a note. We don't return-to-sender unless asked.
24. Day 7+ if the member has **cancelled** in the meantime: contact via the most recent email on record, give them 14 more days, then return-to-sender with a brief note: *"Addressee no longer based at this address."*

---

## 8. Outgoing post for members

25. We don't run an outgoing-post service. Don't offer to "send it for them."
26. Exception: if a member needs to drop a single small letter into a Royal Mail post box, point them to the nearest post box (a 4-minute walk — Newcastle Street). Don't take it from them.

---

## 9. Special cases

### Heavy or oversized items

27. If you can't carry it one-handed, log it where it lands. Don't drag it to the parcels shelf.
28. Tell the member where it is when they arrive. They lift it.

### Items requiring a signature with member ID

29. Many of these (legal letters, document deliveries) require the addressee themselves. If a courier says ID required, refuse. *"You'll need them to be here — they can give you a call when they're in."*

### Suspicious items

30. Anything unmarked, leaking, smelling unusual, ticking, or addressed to a name we can't verify — **refuse at the door**. If it's already been left at reception unaccompanied, isolate it, don't move it further, and follow [`./sop-010-emergency-evacuation.md`](./sop-010-emergency-evacuation.md) §"Unidentified item" if relevant.

### Subscription boxes, regular deliveries

31. Allowed for active members. If a single member is generating significant volume, raise it with Connor — there may need to be a chat about an additional handling arrangement.

### Cold-chain (food perishables)

32. We don't have a dedicated cold storage. Accept and notify immediately. If the member isn't on site that day, decline future cold-chain deliveries with a polite note.

---

## Common issues

| Issue | First action |
|---|---|
| Courier says "just leave it at the door" | Politely refuse — we need to know who it's for. Take the item or send it back with them. |
| A delivery for a name that **was** a member but cancelled | Refuse if you can. If already accepted, hold for 14 days and contact via email on file. |
| Member says a parcel never arrived | Check the log. If it's not there, we can't help — politely refer them to the courier's tracking. Don't speculate. |
| Parcel for the **landlord** or building | Accept, log under "Building", notify the landlord. |
| Parcel for our own use (supplies) | Log under "Worksop Workspace" with your name — same handling as a member item but unbox at end of day. |
