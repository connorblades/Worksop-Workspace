# SOP-009: Lost and Found

**Worksop Workspace** · Operations · *Phase 1 reference*

Status: Draft v1 — needs Connor review · Last updated: 15 May 2026

---

The procedure for handling items left behind in the space — from a charger to a laptop. Done right, this is a small act of competence that members notice. Done wrong, it's a stolen laptop story on Google reviews.

---

## 1. Daily sweep

1. End of every day, as part of [`../checklists/02-daily-closing.md`](../checklists/02-daily-closing.md), walk the floor and check:
   - Every desk surface
   - Every chair seat and back
   - The kitchen counters and seating
   - The meeting room
   - The toilets
   - Hooks, coat stands, behind the door
2. Anything left out goes to the lost-and-found box (behind reception, lid on).

---

## 2. Logging

3. Open the lost-and-found log on the member app (admin → Lost and found).
4. For each item, record:
    - Brief description (don't be cute — *"black Macbook Pro 14"* not *"laptop"*; *"silver Yeti water bottle, half full"* not *"bottle"*)
    - Where found
    - Time found
    - Who found it (initials)
5. For valuable items (laptops, phones, wallets, keys, jewellery, headphones over £50 retail) — additionally:
    - Lock them in the staff cupboard, not the open box
    - Flag them in the log with a star
    - Notify Connor by message

---

## 3. Matching to a likely owner

6. Cross-check the day's check-in log on the app. Who was in at the time the item was found? Who sat where?
7. If you can identify a likely owner — message them via the member app within 60 minutes: *"Did you leave [item] behind today? It's safe behind reception."*
8. Don't guess publicly. *"Anyone lose a wallet?"* in the open space invites the wrong claimant. Quiet, targeted contact only.

---

## 4. Returning to owner

9. Owner shows up. Confirm by:
   - Asking them to describe the item before you show it
   - Or, for unambiguous items (e.g. a laptop with their wallpaper visible), unlocking it
10. Sign it out in the log: time returned, member name, your initials.
11. Hand it over. Move on.

---

## 5. Items unclaimed after 30 days

12. Low-value items (chargers, cheap mugs, single socks, etc.) — bin or donate after 30 days. Update the log.
13. Mid-value items (over ~£20 retail, things like decent water bottles, books) — donate to a local charity shop after 60 days. Update the log.
14. High-value items (laptops, phones, wallets, keys, jewellery) — **never bin**. Keep indefinitely in the locked cupboard. Annually, review with Connor. After 12 months, take advice on whether to hand to the police as found property.

---

## 6. Items containing data or identification

15. Wallets, ID cards, bank cards, passports — treat as high-value regardless of monetary value.
16. Don't open a wallet beyond what's needed to find a name. Don't photograph the contents.
17. If you find an ID inside, attempt direct contact via the member app first. If the wallet doesn't belong to a member, hand to the police as found property within 7 days.

---

## 7. Items found in the toilets

18. Same process, but with care. If the item suggests a member in distress (medication, a personal item that looks deliberately abandoned, etc.), see [`../training/member-conflict-handling.md`](../training/member-conflict-handling.md) for the empathy escalation. Don't broadcast publicly.

---

## 8. Items found broken or damaged

19. Log them anyway, with the damage noted. The member might come asking; we don't want to be in the position of having binned something we should have flagged.

---

## 9. When a member reports a lost item

20. Open the log. Check for matches.
21. If no match, search the space immediately — same routine as the daily sweep, but with their description in mind.
22. If still no match, log their report in the **Reported lost** section of the log with their description, the time they say they lost it, and their best guess of where.
23. If the item resurfaces later, contact them immediately.
24. **Do not promise to find anything.** *"I'll have a look right now and let you know the moment I find anything."* — never *"don't worry, we'll definitely find it."*

---

## 10. Theft suspected

25. A member reports a high-value item missing and **theft is plausible** (e.g. they had it on the desk, stepped away for five minutes, came back, gone).
26. Treat seriously. Don't be defensive. *"I'm so sorry — let's go and look right now, and let's also check the CCTV."*
27. Walk the space with them. Check the lost-and-found.
28. Pull the CCTV footage from the time window. Process per [`../legal-and-compliance/cctv-policy.md`](../legal-and-compliance/cctv-policy.md).
29. If CCTV shows a likely theft — call Connor immediately. We then decide together whether to involve the police. The member can also report directly.
30. Log the incident on the [`../health-and-safety/accident-and-incident-report.md`](../health-and-safety/accident-and-incident-report.md) form (incident, not accident).

---

## Common issues

| Issue | First action |
|---|---|
| Two people claim the same item | Don't release it. Ask both to describe it without seeing it. The real owner can describe specific marks, the contents, or unlock it. |
| Member says "you must have it" but the log shows nothing | Polite, firm — *"I've checked the log and walked the space; I don't have it. I'll keep looking and message you the moment anything turns up."* |
| Lost item is left by a visitor we can't identify | Hold for 30 days. If a card or business card is inside, contact directly. After 30 days, donate or bin as per policy. |
| A member's keys are found | High-value (data implications — could open their house, car). Lock away. Direct contact immediately. |
