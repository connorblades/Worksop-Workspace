# SOP-010: Emergency Evacuation

**Worksop Workspace** · Operations · *Phase 1 reference*

Status: Draft v1 — needs Connor review and final sign-off after fire risk assessment is complete.
*Last updated: 14 May 2026.*

---

What to do in a fire or other emergency requiring evacuation of the building. Read this when you first start working in the space. Refresh annually. **Do not skip this.**

This SOP must be cross-referenced with the completed fire risk assessment (lives in private Drive at `06-legal-and-compliance/fire-risk-assessment.md`) once that document is filled in by a qualified assessor.

---

## 1. If you discover a fire

1. Raise the alarm immediately:
   - Activate the nearest manual call point (red break-glass unit)
   - Shout "Fire! Evacuate the building!" so everyone within earshot hears
2. Do **not** attempt to fight a fire larger than a small wastebin. Personal safety first.
3. For small contained fires (e.g. a bin), if you have been trained and the appropriate extinguisher is at hand, you may attempt to extinguish — only if it can be done safely and quickly. If in doubt, leave it and evacuate.
4. Close the door of the room containing the fire if you can do so safely. This slows fire and smoke spread.

---

## 2. When the alarm sounds

5. Stop what you're doing. The alarm is never tested without prior notice — assume it's real.
6. Calmly tell everyone in your area: "This is a fire alarm. Please leave the building via the nearest fire exit."
7. Do not stop to:
   - Collect laptops, bags, or personal belongings
   - Make a final call or send a final email
   - Use the toilets
   - Use any lift (if present in a future phase)
8. Lead members to the nearest fire exit.

## 5. Roll call

9. Open the member app QR check-in log on your phone. This shows everyone currently signed into the building.
10. Cross-reference against the people at the assembly point.
11. If anyone is missing, do **not** go back inside. Inform the fire service immediately and tell them the name of the missing person and where they were last seen.
12. Note: day-pass visitors and walk-ins must also have scanned in. If for some reason they didn't, rely on memory of who you saw arrive — and brief the fire service.

---

## 6. Calling emergency services

13. Dial **999** as soon as you're safely outside the building.
14. Ask for the **Fire Service**.
15. Give:
    - The full address: 30 Carlton Road, Worksop, Nottinghamshire, S80 1PH
    - The nature of the emergency (fire / smoke / other)
    - Whether everyone is accounted for
    - Whether anyone is trapped inside or unaccounted for
16. Stay on the line until told you can hang up.

---

## 7. Other emergencies

| Situation | Action |
|---|---|
| **Medical emergency** | Call 999 and ask for an ambulance. Do not move the casualty unless they're in further danger. Use the first aid kit. |
| **Power cut** | Calmly inform members. Open blinds for natural light. Wait for restoration. If extended, consider closing the space for the day. |
| **Suspicious package** | Do not touch. Clear the immediate area. Call 999 (police). |
| **Aggressive or violent behaviour** | Get other members to safety first. Call 999 (police). Do not engage physically. Use the incident reporting flow in `../training/member-conflict-handling.md` after. |
| **Gas leak** | Do not use any electrical switches (including lights). Evacuate. Call National Gas Emergency line: **0800 111 999**. |

---

## 8. Re-entry

17. **Never** re-enter the building before the fire service confirms it's safe.
18. Once cleared, walk the building before letting members back in. Check for damage and ongoing hazards.
19. If the building is unsafe to re-enter, close for the day. Notify all members via the app and email.
20. Log the incident in detail (in Drive's `99-archive/incidents/` or wherever incident logs end up — create that folder if needed).
21. Notify insurance broker if any damage occurred.

---

## 9. Drill schedule

Fire drills should be carried out at least:

- **Twice per year** in Phase 1 (more frequent during the first 90 days)
- Drill dates logged in the fire risk assessment in Drive
- All staff and members on site at the time should participate

Schedule with at least 24 hours' notice given to members so they're not surprised — except for one annual unannounced drill (typical fire safety best practice).
