# SOP-018: Complaints Handling

**Worksop Workspace** · Operations · *Phase 1 reference*

Status: Draft v1 — needs Connor review · Last updated: 15 May 2026

---

The procedure for handling a member complaint — front-of-house, by email, or via the member app's "report an issue" route. This pairs with the [`../legal-and-compliance/complaints-policy.md`](../legal-and-compliance/complaints-policy.md) (the externally-stated policy) and the conflict-handling training at [`../training/member-conflict-handling.md`](../training/member-conflict-handling.md) (the soft skills).

The principle: a complaint is a gift. The members who say nothing and just leave are the expensive ones. Take complaints seriously, log them, and close them.

---

## 1. The intake — first 60 seconds

1. Wherever the complaint reaches you (someone walks up to reception, an email, an app message, a comment on a Google review), the first response is the same:
    - **Acknowledge.** *"Thanks for telling me — I want to make sure I understand."*
    - **Listen.** Without interrupting. Without defending.
    - **Repeat back.** *"So what you're saying is [X]. Have I got that right?"*
2. Don't apologise on autopilot — *"I'm so sorry that happened"* before knowing what happened sounds insincere. Apologise specifically once you understand.
3. Don't promise an outcome you can't deliver.

---

## 2. Categorise

Three tiers determine the response path:

| Tier | What it looks like | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **1 — Operational** | Day-to-day annoyance with the space | Coffee machine broken, meeting room was too cold, parking was tricky |
| **2 — Service** | Something we got materially wrong | Booking double-charged, locker broken, app showing wrong information |
| **3 — Conduct** | A member's experience of another person | Another member behaving inappropriately, harassment, sales-pitch nuisance, staff rudeness |

Tier 3 is the only one that should go straight to Connor regardless of who took the intake.

---

## 3. Tier 1 — Operational

4. Fix on the spot where you can. If the coffee machine's broken, make them a tea while you fix it.
5. If a fix needs a contractor, give a realistic time and book it. Don't fudge a timeline.
6. Log in the member app's complaints log: who, what, when, what you did, status.
7. Follow up with the member within 24 hours, even just to say *"All sorted — thanks for flagging."*

---

## 4. Tier 2 — Service

8. Acknowledge in writing the same day (email or in-app message). Don't make them chase.
9. Investigate properly:
    - Check the booking / Stripe record
    - Pull the relevant log entries
    - Talk to anyone else who was on shift
10. Refund / credit promptly when we got it wrong. Don't make people argue for what's owed.
11. Send a clear close-out message:
    - What you found
    - What you did about it
    - What we're changing (if anything) to prevent it again
    - One short apology
12. Log in the complaints log with the close-out date.

---

## 5. Tier 3 — Conduct

13. Take the report somewhere private. Even a corner of reception works if there's no one nearby.
14. Listen fully. Don't ask the complainant to confront the other person.
15. Take notes during, not after — *"Mind if I take notes while we talk? I want to get this right."*
16. **Don't promise confidentiality you can't deliver.** Investigating may require talking to others. Say so: *"I'll handle this discreetly, but to look into it I might need to talk to one or two people."*
17. Brief Connor immediately. Connor takes ownership from here.
18. Connor's response steps:
    - Hear the complainant's account in full
    - Hear the other party's account separately
    - Talk to any witnesses
    - Decide on an outcome — a quiet word, a warning, suspension, or termination per [`../legal-and-compliance/terms-of-business.md`](../legal-and-compliance/terms-of-business.md) §8
    - Communicate the outcome to the complainant
    - Document on the incident log per [`../health-and-safety/accident-and-incident-report.md`](../health-and-safety/accident-and-incident-report.md) (incident tab, not accident)
19. The complainant gets a written outcome. The other party gets a written outcome. They may or may not be the same level of detail (e.g. we tell the complainant *"the member has been warned"* without disclosing the warning's specifics).

---

## 6. Anonymous and third-party complaints

20. Anonymous complaints are still investigated — but without a complainant to follow up with, we can only act on what we can corroborate ourselves.
21. Third-party complaints (e.g. a member raising a concern on behalf of another) — speak with the affected person directly where possible. If they don't want to engage, log the concern and monitor.

---

## 7. Vexatious or unfounded complaints

22. Rare. Almost never. Most complaints have a real grain.
23. If a member is repeatedly complaining about a non-issue (e.g. complaints about other members' breathing patterns), or escalating things that don't merit it:
    - Take each one seriously the first time
    - The second time, log and acknowledge but explain we don't see grounds for action
    - The third time, raise to Connor — there may be a fit issue
24. Don't get into rebutting the complainant. Document, respond, move on.

---

## 8. Complaints about staff (including Connor)

25. Members may complain about you. That's fine and right.
26. Don't react defensively. Listen the same way you would to a complaint about the coffee machine.
27. If the complaint is about you, brief Connor — let Connor investigate without your involvement on the response side.
28. If the complaint is about Connor — escalate to **an external mediator**. We don't have a director-level grievance process yet. Until we do, use ACAS Early Conciliation or a local mediation service if needed. We're a single-director company; there's no internal HR.

---

## 9. Escalation outside the company

29. If a complaint touches:
    - **Data protection** — escalate path is the ICO at <https://ico.org.uk>. See [`../legal-and-compliance/data-protection-and-gdpr.md`](../legal-and-compliance/data-protection-and-gdpr.md).
    - **Discrimination / harassment** — the complainant's external route is the Equality and Human Rights Commission or ACAS for employment matters.
    - **A potential criminal act** — Nottinghamshire Police, 999 (in progress) or 101 (after).
30. We don't try to hold complaints in-house when they belong with a regulator. We tell members what their options are.

---

## 10. The complaints log

31. The log is on the member app (admin → Complaints) and includes:
    - Date received
    - Tier
    - Complainant (member name)
    - Summary (one sentence)
    - Action taken
    - Date closed
    - Outcome category (resolved, refunded, suspended other member, terminated other member, no action — unfounded)
32. Reviewed monthly per [`../checklists/04-monthly-business-review.md`](../checklists/04-monthly-business-review.md). Patterns get fed back into operations.

---

## 11. Tone — do and don't

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Acknowledge specifically | Apologise vaguely |
| Listen fully first | Defend before understanding |
| Take notes openly | Take notes secretly |
| Repeat back what you heard | Assume you heard right |
| Commit to a time | Commit to an outcome |
| Document everything | Document only the wins |
| Say *"I don't know yet"* | Make something up to fill the silence |

---

## Common issues

| Issue | First action |
|---|---|
| Complainant says *"I don't want to make a formal complaint, but…"* | Treat it as a complaint anyway. Log it. *"Even if you'd rather not call it that, I want to make sure I do something about it."* |
| Complainant escalates publicly (Google review, social) | Respond to the public post **calmly and briefly** — acknowledge, invite a private conversation. Don't argue in public, ever. |
| Complainant threatens legal action | Stop responding informally. Brief Connor. Don't admit liability, don't deny — *"thanks for letting us know — we'll respond formally"*. |
| Complainant won't engage with the outcome | We've still done our job. Log the outcome with their response (or non-response) noted. |
