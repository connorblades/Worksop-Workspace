# SOP-003: Member Onboarding Tour

**Worksop Workspace** · Operations · *Phase 1 reference*

Status: Draft v1 — needs Connor review · Last updated: 14 May 2026

---

How to welcome and onboard a new member when they walk in for the first time. The goal: make them feel they made the right choice, get them set up to work, and let them get on with it. Tour script (what to say) lives at `../training/tour-script.md`.

**Timing:** Allow 20–30 minutes for a first-time onboarding tour.

---

## 1. Initial greeting (first 2 minutes)

1. Make eye contact, smile, greet warmly. Use their name if you have it (check the member app or any prior enquiry record).
2. Confirm what brought them in:
   - First visit (day pass)?
   - New member signing up for a rolling plan?
   - Booked a tour ahead of time?
3. Offer them a drink — tea, coffee, or water. While the kettle boils or coffee machine runs, you have natural time to chat.

**Tone:** warm, low-pressure, not salesy. The brand voice is in `../../brand/brand-guidelines.md` — read it if you haven't.

---

## 2. Understand what they actually need (5 minutes)

4. Ask, in plain language:
   - What do you do for work?
   - Where are you working from right now?
   - What's not working about that?
   - How often do you reckon you'd use a space like this?
5. Listen for the signal. The four most common matches:
   - **Sole trader / tradesperson taking calls from a van** → probably day pass or hot desk weekly to try it
   - **Remote employee escaping a noisy home** → hot desk weekly or dedicated desk
   - **Small business owner with 1–4 staff** → private office
   - **"I'm not sure yet"** → day pass first, decide after they've spent a day in the space
6. Don't push the most expensive option. Push the one that fits.

---

## 3. The tour route (10 minutes)

7. Walk them through the space in this order (Phase 1):
   - **Front door / reception** — point out where they'll come in each day, where lockers are
   - **Hot desk area** — explain how to grab any available seat, point out power and light
   - **Dedicated desk area** — explain how dedicated desks are reserved and theirs is theirs every day
   - **Private offices** — show through the glass; only enter if no one's working in them
   - **Refreshment area** — show the coffee machine, kettle, water. Tell them: it's all included, help themselves.
   - **Toilets**
   - **Quiet zones vs. social zones** — make clear that the space has both, depending on what they need on any given day
   - **Fire exits** — point out at least one, casually, so it registers without feeling formal
   - **Parking** — point out the car park is on site and free
8. Show them the Wi-Fi sign or hand them the Wi-Fi card. Help them connect on the spot.

---

## 4. The member app (3 minutes)

9. Help them download the member app from the relevant app store.
10. Help them set up their account.
11. Show them how to:
    - Find their QR code (for entry/exit scanning)
    - Book a desk, room, or session
    - Check real-time availability
    - Raise an issue or complaint
    - View / manage their membership
12. Remind them: the QR scan is automatic check-in/out. They scan in when they arrive, scan out when they leave. We need this for fire safety.

---

## 5. Sign-up and payment

13. If they're signing up for a rolling membership today:
    - Walk them through the Stripe checkout link (sent to them via email or scanned via QR)
    - Direct debit for weekly subscriptions (Private Office, Dedicated Desk, Hot Desk Membership)
    - Card payment for day passes and hourly bookings
14. Confirm the first charge has gone through before they leave.
15. Hand them the welcome pack from `../../members/welcome-pack.md` (printed copy at reception).

---

## 6. Settle them in

16. Walk them to a seat that suits what they said they wanted (quiet zone if they mentioned focus, social area if they mentioned wanting some energy around them).
17. Let them know:
    - You're around for the rest of the day if they need anything
    - The coffee is unlimited
    - They can come and go via QR
    - They cancel any time, no notice period
18. Step back. They came here to work — let them work.

---

## 7. End of day or first visit follow-up

19. Before they leave, say something like: "Hope it's been a good first day. Anything you'd change about the space?" This is the start of the feedback loop.
20. Log anything noteworthy in `../../members/feedback-log.md` (if/when created).
21. If they're a day-pass visitor, mention the option of a weekly pass or dedicated desk without pushing it. *"If you fancied coming in regularly the weekly pass works out about £6 a day."*

---

## What never to do

- Pitch them other services they didn't ask about. They came here to work, not to be sold to.
- Pressure them to upgrade or commit. Pricing is fixed; the gym-style model means we don't sweat retention through urgency.
- Compare to specific competitors by name unless they bring it up first.
- Use sales language: "act now", "limited offer", "don't miss out". See `../../brand/brand-guidelines.md` — words to avoid.

---

If anything in this tour didn't land for the member, edit this SOP. This is a living document.
