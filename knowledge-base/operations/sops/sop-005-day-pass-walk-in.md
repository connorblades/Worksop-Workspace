# SOP-005: Day Pass Walk-In Handling

**Worksop Workspace** · Operations · *Phase 1 reference*

Status: Draft v1 — needs Connor review · Last updated: 15 May 2026

---

The procedure for taking someone who walks in off the street, getting them paid up, set up at a desk, and on with their work in under five minutes.

**Price:** £12/day. **Access:** any available hot desk during opening hours. **Included:** Wi-Fi, refreshments, locker (shared, subject to availability), parking.

---

## 1. The first 30 seconds

1. Greet them at the door. Stand up if you're at reception. Eye contact. Smile.
2. Ask **"Have you been in before?"** — that one question splits the conversation into "first-timer" or "returning visitor" and tells you which script to run.
3. If first-timer, give a 30-second mental orientation: *"We're a coworking space — pay for the day, grab any free desk, free coffee and tea, Wi-Fi on the card on the table. £12 for the day. Want me to set you up?"*
4. If they say yes, go to section 2. If they want a tour first, run the short version from [`../../sales/conversion/tour-script.md`](../../sales/conversion/tour-script.md) — keep it under two minutes for a walk-in.

---

## 2. Taking payment

5. Open the member app's day-pass flow. Tap **New day pass**.
6. Capture their name, email, mobile number, and the company name if they offer one.
7. Send the card payment link to their phone, or take card payment at reception on the terminal.
8. Once paid, the app issues:
   - A QR code that goes to their phone (for door access on future visits — Phase 2+)
   - A Wi-Fi card (printed, on the table) — for today

---

## 3. Settling them in (60 seconds)

9. Walk with them to a free desk. Don't just point — walk. Hand them the Wi-Fi card.
10. Show them:
    - The coffee/tea area
    - The toilets
    - The fire exit
    - The lockers (free use today — show how the keypad works)
    - The reception/where you'll be
11. Tell them: *"If you need anything at all, come grab me. Welcome."*
12. Leave them. Don't hover. They came here to work.

---

## 4. During the day

13. Half-glance at the day-pass count on the app once an hour. Capacity check.
14. If they hit refreshment supplies (coffee out, milk low), restock immediately. They shouldn't have to ask.
15. Don't strike up unnecessary conversation. They're not a hostage — they're a person who paid £12 to focus.

---

## 5. End of day

16. As people pack up, say a warm goodbye **by name** if you caught it. *"Thanks for coming in — see you soon."*
17. Don't push membership. If they bring it up, mention the hot-desk weekly (£30/week — that's effectively 2.5 day passes). Show, don't sell.
18. If they had a good day, ask them to leave a Google review — and remind them that a review earns them a free day pass (the only standing incentive).
19. The app logs their visit automatically. No manual close-out needed.

---

## 6. Returning visitor (third visit or more)

20. Different conversation now. Greet them by name as a regular.
21. After the payment step (same flow), ask: *"You've been in a few times — have you thought about the weekly hot desk plan? £30 for the week, you can come and go, cancel any time. About the cost of two and a half day passes."*
22. If they say yes, switch to [`./sop-006-new-member-sign-up.md`](./sop-006-new-member-sign-up.md).
23. If they say no, drop it cleanly and let them get on with the day. **No pressure.** We promised that on the website and we keep it.

---

## Common issues

| Issue | First action |
|---|---|
| Card payment declines | Try the second terminal. If still declined, ask politely if they'd like to try another card. Don't make a scene. |
| They want to pay in cash | We're cashless. Explain politely — *"We don't take cash, but I can take any card or send you a link to pay on your phone."* |
| They want a tour but won't commit to staying | Give a quick tour anyway. Tours are cheap; relationships are not. They might be back. |
| Person who clearly just wants free Wi-Fi for ten minutes | Day pass or nothing. We're not a coffee shop. Be polite, be clear. |
| Walk-in arrives at 4:55pm | We close at 5pm in Phase 1. Apologise, explain, invite them back tomorrow. Don't process a five-minute day pass. |

---

## What we never do

- Discount the day pass. It's £12, full stop. Discounting devalues every membership tier above it.
- Pressure anyone into a membership. The numbers will speak. If we keep the place worth turning up to, people will sign up.
- Ask why they're working. None of our business.
