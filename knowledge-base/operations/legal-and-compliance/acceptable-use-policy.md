# Acceptable Use Policy

**Worksop Workspace** · Operations · Legal & Compliance · *Phase 1 reference*

Status: **Draft v1 — needs Connor review.** Last updated: 16 May 2026.

---

This policy covers what members may and may not do with Worksop Workspace's network, wifi, and IT facilities. It applies to anyone connecting any device (laptop, phone, tablet) to our network — members, day-pass users, guests, and staff.

The principles are simple: use the network for normal lawful work, don't take the piss with bandwidth, and don't put other members at risk.

---

## 1. What you can use the network for

- Normal work — email, web, video calls, cloud apps, document editing, code, design, AI tools.
- Streaming for breaks is fine in moderation.
- Personal browsing is fine.

## 2. What you can't use the network for

1. **Anything illegal under UK law.** This includes accessing or sharing child sexual abuse material, terrorism content, copyrighted material without licence, fraud, hacking, or anything that breaches the Computer Misuse Act 1990.
2. **Harassment, threats, or abuse** directed at other members, staff, or anyone else.
3. **Heavy peer-to-peer / torrenting traffic.** Even where the content is legal, the bandwidth pattern is disruptive. Don't.
4. **Crypto mining** on our network or power.
5. **Attempting to access other members' devices** (port scanning, packet sniffing, ARP spoofing, anything in that family). The network has client isolation enabled — leave it alone.
6. **Bypassing or attempting to bypass** any filtering, monitoring, or security controls.
7. **Sustained high-bandwidth uploads** (large dataset transfers, continuous video uploads) without telling us first — we may need to schedule them outside peak hours.

## 3. Devices

- You're responsible for the devices you bring in. Keep them patched and free of malware.
- We may ask you to disconnect a device if it's flooding the network, broadcasting suspect traffic, or otherwise causing problems.
- Don't plug random devices into our switches or routers. Wifi only unless we've agreed otherwise.

## 4. Wifi credentials

- The wifi password is given to members. **Don't share it outside the space.** Bringing a guest in is fine; they get our guest network, not the member network.
- We rotate the password periodically and after any incident. You'll be told the new one.

## 5. Monitoring

- We don't log your browsing, read your traffic, or do deep-packet inspection. We don't want to.
- We do record connection-level data (which devices connected, when, for how long, how much bandwidth) for operational reasons.
- If we have a credible reason to believe the network is being used illegally — police request, court order, or evidence of obvious abuse — we will cooperate with lawful requests.

## 6. Email, file storage, and member data

Worksop Workspace does not provide member email accounts or file storage. Your work, files, and accounts are yours and your responsibility.

## 7. AI tools

You can use any AI tool you like on the network for your own work. Be aware that anything you upload to a third-party AI tool leaves the building and is governed by that provider's terms — that's on you, not us.

## 8. What happens if you break this policy

1. First minor incident — a conversation.
2. Repeated minor incidents or any deliberate breach — written warning and possible temporary disconnection.
3. Serious or unlawful use — immediate disconnection, membership termination, and depending on what happened, a report to the police.

Decisions on what counts as serious are Connor's, applied consistently in line with the [Member Agreement](member-agreement.md) and [Complaints Policy](complaints-policy.md).

---

*Short version: use it for work, don't break the law, don't be a dick about bandwidth. If in doubt, ask.*
