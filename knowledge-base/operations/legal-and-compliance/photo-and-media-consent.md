# Photo and Media Consent

**Worksop Workspace** · Operations · Legal & Compliance · *Phase 1 reference*

Status: **Draft v1 — needs Connor review.** Last updated: 16 May 2026.

---

This is the consent form used whenever Worksop Workspace wants to use a recognisable photo, video, or audio recording of a specific person for marketing, social media, the website, press, or any other published material.

The general space-atmosphere shots (people in wide shots, blurred, or unidentifiable) are covered by the [Member Agreement](member-agreement.md) §5. **This form is for identifiable, named, or focal use** — interviews, testimonials, headshots, video features, podcast guests.

---

## 1. Why a separate consent

UK GDPR treats an identifiable image as personal data. Publishing it requires a lawful basis. For marketing of an identifiable individual, the cleanest basis is **explicit consent** — given freely, in writing, after the person has been told what they're agreeing to. This form does that.

For incidental capture in wide shots of a public space, we rely on the contractual notice in the [Member Agreement](member-agreement.md) and the prominent signage in the space. That's a separate route and doesn't need this form.

## 3. How we handle the signed form

- Signed forms go in `Worksop Workspace HQ > 06-legal-and-compliance > consent` in Drive, named `consent-yyyy-mm-dd-firstname-lastname.pdf`.
- A row goes into the **consent register** with: name, date signed, scope, expiry, withdrawal status. This is so we can find the consent quickly if someone asks us to remove an image.
- When a withdrawal arrives, we update the register the same day and remove the material from owned channels within 14 days.

---

## 4. Children, vulnerable adults, third-party subjects

Worksop Workspace does not admit under-18s, so this should be rare. If it ever arises (a school visit, a special event), consent must come from the parent or legal guardian, on the form above adapted to name the child.

---

## 5. Photographers and videographers we engage

When we hire an external photographer or videographer, we ensure their contract makes clear:
- They get model release from anyone they recognisably photograph for our use
- They don't retain images for their own portfolio without separate consent
- They hand over the raw files to us within an agreed timeframe
