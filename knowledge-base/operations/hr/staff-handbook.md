## Important — what this doc is and isn't

This is the **internal source of truth** for the staff handbook. The version issued to an employee is generated from this, formatted for print, and signed for receipt as part of induction.

Phase 1 of Worksop Workspace has **no employees** — the space is run single-handed by Connor. This handbook exists ahead of the first hire so that the framework is in place before the conversation. It is not yet a contractual document; the contractually-binding terms are in the individual employment contract issued at the point of hire.

When the first employee starts, this handbook must be:

- Reviewed by a UK employment law adviser
- Brought to the attention of the employee on day 1
- Acknowledged in writing as part of induction
- Reviewed annually thereafter

## 1. Welcome

You've joined a coworking space being built from scratch in Worksop. The mission is simple: a focused, professional, welcoming place where people can get their work done. Every part of how we operate exists to serve that.

The space is small. There's no corporate layer. The person who decides things is the person you talk to most days. Take that as an invitation: if something's not working, say so. If you think we should change something, say so. We move quickly when we agree.

## 4. Your employment

The specific terms of your role — pay, hours, holiday allowance, notice period — are in your **employment contract**, not here. The handbook covers the rules and expectations that apply to all staff.

### Probation

Most roles start with a **3-month probationary period**. During probation, either side can end the employment with one week's notice. After probation, contractual notice periods apply.

### Working hours

Phase 1 manned hours are 8am–5pm Monday to Friday. Specific shift patterns are in your contract. Breaks are unpaid and untracked beyond the 20-minute statutory minimum for a 6+ hour shift.

### Pay

Pay is monthly, by direct credit, on the last working day of each month. Payslips are issued via the payroll system; you have legal entitlement to see them.

### Right to work

We check right-to-work documents before your first day. We can't employ anyone who can't legally work in the UK.

### Confidentiality

Anything you learn about members during the course of your work is confidential. That includes who's in the space, what they're working on (if you see it), their business details, their personal circumstances, and anything they tell you. This continues after your employment ends.

### Conflicts of interest

Tell us if you have one. We define a conflict as broadly as you would in a small-town business: a close personal relationship with a member or supplier, a side business that competes with or supplies us, that kind of thing. We trust you to recognise the rough shape of one and raise it.

## 6. Workplace expectations

### Dress

There's no uniform. Dress in something you'd happily greet a corporate-tour visitor in. Smart-casual is the default — clean, ironed, no slogans, no political messaging on your clothing.

### Phones

Phones on you, on silent. Use them as you would in any office — not constantly, not never. If you're on a personal call for more than two minutes, step away from reception.

### Social media

Don't post about members or what members are working on. Don't post photos of the space showing identifiable members without permission. Don't engage in arguments about Worksop Workspace publicly — if a public concern is raised online, brief Connor and let it be handled formally.

If you're personally on social media in a way that identifies you as a member of Worksop Workspace staff, your posts reflect on the business. Apply common sense.

### Smoking and vaping

Not inside the building, not in the car park within 10m of the entrance.

### Alcohol and drugs

You do not work under the influence. If you're on medication that may affect you, tell us so we can think about it together. Misuse of drugs or alcohol at work is gross misconduct — see [`./conduct-and-disciplinary.md`](./conduct-and-disciplinary.md).

### Members

Treat members the way we describe in [`../training/member-conflict-handling.md`](../training/member-conflict-handling.md). Calm, warm, respectful. Even — especially — when they're frustrated.

### Each other

We're a small team. We talk plainly to each other. Disagree directly, not via passive remarks. If you have a problem with how someone is working, raise it with them first; if you can't, raise it with Connor.

### Romantic relationships with members

Best avoided while the membership is active. If one forms, tell Connor — we won't ask invasive questions, we just need to know so we can think about anything that might create a conflict.

## 10. Performance reviews

You'll have a structured **1:1 with Connor every month** during your first six months, then quarterly. Reviews are informal, focused on:

- What's working
- What isn't

- What we need from you
- Any objectives for next period

## 12. Resolution of issues

| Problem | First step |
|---|---|
| Practical (e.g. broken equipment, a member behaving badly) | Address it on the day. Use the relevant SOP. |
| Concerns about another member of staff | Talk to them directly first. If that doesn't resolve, talk to Connor. |
| Concerns about Connor | We don't have an internal HR layer above Connor. Use ACAS Early Conciliation (<https://www.acas.org.uk>) or an independent mediator. We commit to engaging with any complaint raised that way. |
| Pay or contract dispute | Talk to Connor. If unresolved, see grievance procedure in [`./conduct-and-disciplinary.md`](./conduct-and-disciplinary.md). |

## 13. Leaving

When you decide to leave:

- Notice period as per your contract
- Hand back keys, devices, anything else issued
- Final pay including any accrued holiday in your last payslip
- Exit chat with Connor — what worked, what didn't, what we should change

We'll provide a reference. We'll keep your record for the retention period set out in [`../legal-and-compliance/privacy-policy.md`](../legal-and-compliance/privacy-policy.md).

We try to leave on good terms. Worksop is small.
