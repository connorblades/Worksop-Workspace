# Sickness and Absence Policy

**Worksop Workspace** · Operations · HR · *For when we hire*

Status: Draft v1 — needs Connor review and HR adviser sign-off · Last updated: 15 May 2026

---

## Important — what this doc is and isn't

This is the policy for staff sickness and unplanned absence. Phase 1 currently has no employees. The policy is in place ahead of the first hire. Specific terms in an individual employment contract take precedence where they differ.

We're a small operation. One person being out has a real impact. We need to balance taking sickness seriously (you should rest when you're ill) with running a space members can rely on.

---

## 1. Headline rules

- If you're sick, you don't come in.
- Tell us **by 8am on the day** — the earlier the better.
- We honour Statutory Sick Pay (SSP) for everyone who qualifies.
- We don't punish genuine sickness. We do address patterns that look like something else.

---

## 2. Reporting an absence

### On the day

1. Call or text **Connor** before 8am (or before your shift start time, whichever is earlier).
2. Tell us:
    - That you're not coming in
    - The reason (broadly — you don't owe us a diagnosis)
    - How long you think you'll be off
3. Do **not** delegate this to a partner or family member unless you genuinely can't make the call yourself.

### Subsequent days

If your absence continues, message daily by 8am until you're back. If you know your full duration on day one (e.g. you have a sick note for a week), say so and we don't need a daily update.

### When you're back

- Return to work for your next scheduled shift unless you've been told otherwise
- Have a brief **return-to-work conversation** with Connor on your first day back, even if it's a 30-second chat by the coffee machine

---

## 3. Self-certification

For absences of **7 calendar days or less**, you self-certify. We complete a short Self-Certification (SC2-equivalent) form on your return — duration, broad reason, fit to return.

The template lives in Drive's `09-hr/self-certification-template.md`.

---

## 4. Fit notes

For absences **longer than 7 days**, you need a **Statement of Fitness for Work** (a "fit note") from a GP or NHS clinician. Email a scan to Connor on the day it's issued.

If the fit note says you're fit for some work with adjustments (e.g. "no heavy lifting"), we'll talk about whether we can accommodate those adjustments. If we can, we will. If we can't, you stay off and the fit note remains in effect.

---

## 5. Statutory Sick Pay (SSP)

You qualify for SSP if:
- You're classed as an employee and have done some work
- You earn at least the Lower Earnings Limit
- You've been sick for 4 or more days in a row (including non-working days)
- You've told us within the deadline

SSP is paid from the **fourth qualifying day** for up to **28 weeks** at the current SSP rate (set by GOV.UK).

We don't pay company sick pay above SSP in Phase 1. If that changes, your individual contract will reflect it.

---

## 6. Return-to-work conversation

Every absence gets a short return-to-work conversation. It's not a disciplinary; it's:

- A welcome back
- A check that you're actually well enough to be in
- A factual update on what happened while you were off
- An opportunity for you to raise anything (workload, working environment, anything else that might have contributed)

The conversation is logged in the sickness record — a few sentences, no medical details beyond the broad reason for absence.

---

## 7. Patterns

Some patterns prompt a more formal conversation:

| Trigger | Response |
|---|---|
| 3 or more separate absences in a rolling 12-month period | Wellbeing conversation. No assumption of fault. |
| Total of 8 or more days off in a rolling 12-month period | Wellbeing conversation. |
| Recurring absences on the same weekday | Specific conversation about what's going on. |
| Absence pattern around weekends, holidays, or contentious work | Direct, supportive conversation. |

The conversations are documented but they aren't disciplinary unless a pattern of dishonesty or misconduct emerges (see [`./conduct-and-disciplinary.md`](./conduct-and-disciplinary.md)).

---

## 8. Long-term sickness (4+ weeks)

If you're off for four weeks or more:

- We maintain contact at a pace that suits you. We don't pressure you to return.
- You're invited to a welfare meeting (in person, video, or phone — your choice).
- We may suggest an **Occupational Health** referral. We pay for it.
- Where possible, we plan a phased return.
- We comply with the Equality Act 2010 if the condition meets the legal definition of a disability — including making reasonable adjustments.

---

## 9. Doctor's appointments

| Type | How we treat it |
|---|---|
| Routine GP appointment | Arrange outside working hours where possible. If it can't be, we agree a flex on the day. Not deducted from leave. |
| Specialist / hospital appointment | Paid time off. We don't deduct from leave. |
| Ante-natal | Paid time off (statutory entitlement). |
| Counselling / therapy | Treated the same as a specialist appointment. We won't pry. |

## 10. Sickness involving someone else

If you can't come to work because a dependant is sick, see [`./holiday-and-leave-policy.md`](./holiday-and-leave-policy.md) §5 — time off for dependants. Different framework, same general response.

---

## 11. Honesty and trust

This whole policy works on trust. We trust you to tell us when you're ill, and to rest. You trust us not to penalise genuine sickness.

If we find evidence that an absence was dishonest — you were well, you were doing something else, you were claiming sickness while working a second job — that's gross misconduct under [`./conduct-and-disciplinary.md`](./conduct-and-disciplinary.md).

We also reserve the right to ask for medical evidence in unusual circumstances, but we wouldn't do this lightly.

---

## 12. Privacy

Sickness records are personal data — they're held securely, accessed only by Connor (and a future HR-named person), retained per the [`../legal-and-compliance/privacy-policy.md`](../legal-and-compliance/privacy-policy.md) schedule.

Where the absence relates to a disability, ongoing condition, or mental-health matter, the record is treated as special-category data under UK GDPR. We process it under Article 9(2)(b) — employment-law purposes.
