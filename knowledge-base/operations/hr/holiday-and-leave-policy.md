## Important — what this doc is and isn't

This is the policy on paid and unpaid leave for Worksop Workspace employees. It applies to all employees once we have any. Phase 1 currently has no employees. The specific entitlements in your individual employment contract take precedence over anything here if they differ.

---

## 1. Annual leave

### Entitlement

- **Full-time employees** — 28 days per year **including** the 8 UK bank holidays. This is the UK statutory minimum (5.6 weeks pro rata).
- **Part-time employees** — pro rata to hours worked. Worked example: a 3-day-a-week role gets 16.8 days per year including bank holidays.
- Any role-specific enhancements are in the employment contract.

### Holiday year

Our holiday year runs **1 April to 31 March**. New starters' entitlement is pro-rated from their start date.

### Carry-over

We don't generally carry leave forward. The expectation is that you take what you accrue, when you accrue it. Statutory carry-over rules apply in specific circumstances (long-term sickness, parental leave). Talk to Connor before the end of the holiday year if you have an exceptional reason.

### How to book

- Submit holiday requests at least **two weeks** in advance for any block of more than three days; one week for shorter requests.
- Use the holiday tracker (Drive's `09-hr/holiday-tracker.md`, or whatever the payroll/HR system is when we're using one).
- We approve based on staffing and the rota — most requests get a "yes." We reserve the right to decline where it would leave the space understaffed; in that case we'll suggest alternative dates.

### Bank holidays

The space is **closed on the eight standard UK bank holidays**. These count against your annual leave allowance (since 28 days is inclusive). If your shift pattern doesn't include the bank-holiday day, you neither use a day nor gain one.

### Christmas closure

We close from the last working day before Christmas through to the first working day in the new year. Days that fall in that window are deducted from your annual leave (typically 3 working days).

## 2. Sickness

See [`./sickness-and-absence-policy.md`](./sickness-and-absence-policy.md) — the full sickness procedure.

Headline: if you're sick, tell Connor by 8am on the day. Self-certify for up to 7 calendar days; fit note for anything longer. Statutory Sick Pay (SSP) applies once the qualifying conditions are met.

## 3. Parental and family leave

We follow UK statutory entitlements:

| Leave type | Entitlement | Pay |
|---|---|---|
| Maternity leave | Up to 52 weeks | Statutory Maternity Pay if eligible (39 weeks) |
| Paternity leave | 1 or 2 consecutive weeks | Statutory Paternity Pay if eligible |
| Shared parental leave | Up to 50 weeks split between parents | Statutory Shared Parental Pay if eligible |
| Adoption leave | Up to 52 weeks | Statutory Adoption Pay if eligible |
| Parental leave (unpaid) | 18 weeks per child up to age 18 | Unpaid; max 4 weeks per year per child |
| Time off for dependants | Reasonable; unpaid | For urgent dependants' issues |
| Bereavement leave | See §5 | Per the bereavement section |

Notification and evidence requirements follow current UK law (e.g. notify maternity leave by the 15th week before the expected week of childbirth).

We follow current entitlements as published by GOV.UK; if anything in the above conflicts with the current statutory position, the statute wins.

---

## 4. Compassionate and emergency leave

If something major happens in your life — a serious illness, a sudden bereavement, a family crisis — talk to Connor. We don't have a rigid policy because every situation is different. We will:

- Be reasonable about time off
- Try to pay for short absences where we can
- Not pressure you to return before you're ready
- Hold the role open within reason

For bereavement, the baseline is **5 working days paid leave** following the death of a partner, child, parent, sibling, or close family equivalent. Statutory **Parental Bereavement Leave** of 2 weeks applies for the loss of a child under 18.

---

## 5. Time off for dependants

You have a statutory right to a reasonable amount of unpaid time off to deal with an unexpected dependant's emergency — childcare collapsing, an elderly parent rushed to hospital, school closure. Tell Connor as soon as you can. We don't deduct this from annual leave.

---

## 6. Jury service and public duties

We give the time off. The court pays you for it; we don't top up, but the time isn't deducted from your annual leave.

---

## 7. Time off for medical appointments

Routine appointments are arranged around your working hours where possible. For specialist appointments, scans, ante-natal care, and other appointments that can't be moved, we give the time off — usually paid for the appointment duration plus reasonable travel.

---

## 8. Unpaid leave / sabbatical

Not a standard offering. We'll consider requests case by case, particularly for tenure of two years or more.

---

## 9. When the space is closed

When we close the space outside the standard Christmas closure (e.g. an unplanned closure for repairs, a one-off event), staff scheduled to work are paid for their scheduled hours.

---

## 10. Recording and pay treatment

| Type | How it's recorded | How it's paid |
|---|---|---|
| Annual leave | Holiday tracker | Normal salary |
| Bank holidays | Annual leave allowance | Normal salary |
| Christmas closure | Annual leave allowance | Normal salary |
| Sickness | Sickness log + return-to-work | SSP-eligible per [`./sickness-and-absence-policy.md`](./sickness-and-absence-policy.md) |
| Maternity / paternity etc. | Payroll records | Statutory pay where eligible |
| Bereavement | Manager note | Normal salary for the days |
| Dependant emergency | Manager note | Unpaid |
| Jury service | Manager note | Unpaid (court pays separately) |
| Medical appointment | Manager note | Paid time off |
| Unpaid leave | Payroll record | Unpaid |

---

## 11. Holiday on exit

On leaving, any accrued but untaken holiday is paid out in the final salary payment. Any holiday taken in excess of accrual at the leaving date may be deducted from final pay — agreed in writing on your contract.
