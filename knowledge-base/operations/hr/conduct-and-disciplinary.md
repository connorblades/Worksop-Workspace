# Conduct and Disciplinary Policy

**Worksop Workspace** · Operations · HR · *For when we hire*

Status: Draft v1 — needs Connor review and HR adviser sign-off · Last updated: 15 May 2026

---

## Important — what this doc is and isn't

This is the staff conduct and disciplinary policy for Worksop Workspace. Phase 1 currently has no employees. It applies to all employees once we have any. Specific terms in an individual employment contract take precedence where they differ. This policy is **non-contractual** (we may amend it from time to time) but reflects how we expect to behave in practice.

It follows the **ACAS Code of Practice on Disciplinary and Grievance Procedures**. Anything we do under this policy must be consistent with the ACAS code — that's the standard tribunals apply.

---

## 1. The principles

- We deal with conduct issues quickly and fairly.
- We address the behaviour, not the person.
- We give a chance to improve before escalating, except in cases of gross misconduct.
- We document properly. Both sides keep copies.
- You have the right to be accompanied at any formal meeting by a colleague or trade union representative.
- Outcomes are proportionate. We don't go straight to "you're fired" for a forgotten checklist.

---

## 2. What conduct looks like

What we expect:

- Turning up to your shifts, on time, ready to work
- Following the SOPs and policies
- Treating members, visitors, contractors, and each other with respect
- Looking after the space and its equipment
- Honest communication
- Reporting things — incidents, accidents, mistakes
- Confidentiality about members and the business

What's a problem (this is illustrative, not exhaustive):

| Tier | Examples |
|---|---|
| Minor misconduct | Lateness without notice; not following an SOP without good reason; small recurring mistakes; not completing a checklist |
| Misconduct | Repeated minor misconduct after warning; persistent poor attitude with members; unauthorised use of equipment; misuse of social media in a way that affects the business |
| Gross misconduct | Theft, fraud, dishonesty; physical violence or threatening behaviour; serious breach of health & safety; serious breach of data protection; substance abuse at work; deliberate damage to property; serious harassment or discrimination; bringing the business into serious disrepute |

Gross misconduct can result in **summary dismissal** — dismissal without notice or pay in lieu of notice — at the conclusion of a fair process.

---

## 3. The informal stage

Most issues are resolved by a quiet conversation. If something needs to change, Connor will:

- Raise it with you privately
- Be specific about what's happened, when, and why it's a problem
- Listen to your side
- Agree what changes
- Confirm by short email or note

Informal conversations are not disciplinary action and don't go on a permanent record. They are noted in the management log so we can recognise patterns.

---

## 4. The formal procedure

If informal resolution doesn't work, or if the issue is too serious for informal resolution, we follow this process.

### Step 1 — Investigation

Connor (or, where appropriate, an external investigator) investigates the facts. This might involve:

- Talking to you
- Talking to other staff, members, or contractors
- Reviewing CCTV, system logs, paper records, written communications
- Reviewing relevant SOPs and policies

The investigation is proportionate. We don't dig into your private life beyond what's directly relevant.

If suspension is considered necessary while we investigate (rare; usually only for gross misconduct allegations), it is on full pay and is not a disciplinary outcome in itself.

### Step 2 — Letter and meeting

If the investigation finds a case to answer, we send you a letter that:

- Sets out the allegation(s)
- Refers to the evidence
- Invites you to a disciplinary meeting
- Gives you reasonable notice (at least 48 hours unless we both agree sooner)
- Reminds you of your right to be accompanied
- Tells you the possible outcomes

### Step 3 — The meeting

At the disciplinary meeting:

- Connor (or the chair) sets out the case
- You respond — bring your own version, any evidence, witnesses if relevant
- Questions both ways
- You're given a clear chance to make your case

A note is taken. You get a copy.

### Step 4 — The decision

After a reasonable interval (usually within 5 working days), Connor issues the decision in writing. Possible outcomes:

| Outcome | What it means | How long it stays on record |
|---|---|---|
| No action | The complaint isn't upheld | Removed from file |
| Informal feedback | Issue noted; no formal warning | Removed from file after 6 months if no recurrence |
| First written warning | Formal warning on file | 6 months |
| Final written warning | Next step is dismissal | 12 months |
| Demotion or restructure | Where appropriate and contractual | As specified |
| Dismissal with notice | Misconduct stays-on-record dismissal | Permanent |
| Summary dismissal | Gross misconduct, no notice | Permanent |

The decision letter explains the reasoning and tells you about the right to appeal.

### Step 5 — Appeal

You can appeal any formal disciplinary outcome. Notify in writing within **5 working days** of receiving the decision. The appeal:

- Is heard by someone not previously involved — for a small company that means an external appointed person
- Is not a re-hearing of the original case unless new evidence is in play
- Considers whether the original outcome was fair, proportionate, and procedurally sound

The appeal outcome is final at the internal level.

---

## 5. Special handling — gross misconduct

If the allegation is of gross misconduct:

- Connor may suspend you on full pay pending investigation
- Suspension is not assumed guilt — it's a containment step
- The investigation is the same standard as any other; the burden of fairness is higher because the stake is higher
- A finding of gross misconduct can lead to summary dismissal
- You still have the right to be accompanied, to appeal, etc.

---

## 6. Grievances — when you have a complaint

A grievance is what we call it when **you** are raising a concern about how you've been treated — by another member of staff, by a member, or by Connor.

### How to raise

- **Informal** — talk to Connor directly. Often this is enough.
- **Formal** — write to Connor, setting out the issue, what you'd like to happen, any evidence.
- **Complaint about Connor** — write to an **external mediator** (we don't have an internal HR layer). ACAS Early Conciliation is the standard route; or a local employment mediation service.

### What happens

| Step | What |
|---|---|
| Acknowledge | Within 1 working day |
| Meeting | Within 10 working days (you can be accompanied) |
| Investigation | Up to 4 weeks; longer with notice if needed |
| Outcome letter | Within 10 working days of conclusion |
| Appeal | Within 5 working days of outcome |

The structure mirrors the disciplinary procedure — fair, proportionate, documented.

### Retaliation

We do not retaliate against staff for raising grievances in good faith. Retaliation by anyone — including Connor — is itself grounds for action.

---

## 7. Whistleblowing

A whistleblowing concern is when you have reasonable belief that something serious is happening — illegal, dangerous, a serious breach of policy — and you're raising it in the public interest. The **Public Interest Disclosure Act 1998** protects you from being dismissed or treated detrimentally for raising it.

If you have something to whistleblow on:

- Raise it directly with Connor where it doesn't involve Connor
- Where it does involve Connor, raise with the external mediator route in §6
- You can also go to a "prescribed person" under PIDA — the HSE, the ICO, etc., depending on the topic

We treat whistleblowing seriously. You are protected.

---

## 8. Bullying, harassment, and discrimination

Bullying and harassment are unacceptable. Discrimination based on any protected characteristic under the Equality Act 2010 — age, disability, gender reassignment, marriage and civil partnership, pregnancy and maternity, race, religion or belief, sex, sexual orientation — is unacceptable and unlawful.

A standalone bullying & harassment policy and equality & diversity policy are Tier 2 docs (not yet written). Until they exist:

- The conduct standard is in §2 of this document
- A complaint about bullying, harassment, or discrimination is treated as a **formal grievance** by default — see §6
- Connor may also choose to treat it as a disciplinary matter against the alleged perpetrator if appropriate

We have **zero tolerance** for harassment or discrimination of members, visitors, contractors, or each other.

---

## 9. Records

All formal disciplinary and grievance records are held securely in Drive's restricted HR folder. Retention is **6 years after employment ends** (consistent with the Limitation Act for contract claims).

Informal notes are held for the duration of the issue plus 12 months unless the matter escalates.
