# Visitor Sign-In Log

**Worksop Workspace** · Operations · Facilities · *Phase 1 reference*

Status: **Draft v1 — needs Connor review.** Last updated: 16 May 2026.

---

This is the log every non-member visitor signs when they enter Worksop Workspace. It exists so that, in the event of a fire or other emergency, we know who is in the building. It also supports our duty of care and our records for billing where a member's guest stays beyond what's free under [Member Agreement](../legal-and-compliance/member-agreement.md) §2.

---

## 1. Who signs in

| Who | Sign in? |
|---|---|
| Members | No — their entry is recorded by the access system |
| Day-pass walk-ins | Yes — but this is captured on the day-pass sign-up form, not a separate visitor log |
| A member's guest (meeting, brief visit) | Yes |
| Contractor (cleaner, engineer, delivery) | Yes |
| Prospect on a tour | Yes |
| Connor or staff arriving outside opening hours | Recorded in the [Key Holder and Out-of-Hours Log](key-holder-and-out-of-hours-log.md) instead |

---

## 2. The log itself

The log is a paper book kept at the reception desk. Bound (not loose-leaf) so entries can't be removed or re-ordered.

Each entry captures:

| Date | Time in | Time out | Name | Company / who they're with | Visiting (member name) | Reason | Signed |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

A standard sign-in book from any stationer is fine — the columns above match the common WHSmith / Office Outlet visitor book layout, so we don't need a custom print.

---

## 3. What we tell the visitor at sign-in

Anyone signing in is briefly told (verbally, or via a small notice at reception):

> "Thanks for signing in. While you're with us:
>
> - This is the fire alarm sound — it's a continuous loud tone.
> - If it goes off, please leave by the nearest exit (signposted) and gather at the assembly point in the car park.
> - The toilets are [direction]. Hot drinks and water are in the kitchen.
> - We have CCTV across the public areas — see the signs.
> - Please sign out when you leave so we know everyone has gone."

This is the visitor's induction. It satisfies our duty under [Fire Safety Policy](../health-and-safety/fire-safety-policy.md) and [SOP-010 Emergency Evacuation](../sops/sop-010-emergency-evacuation.md).

---

## 4. GDPR — keeping the log lawful

The visitor log is personal data. We minimise what we collect and keep it only as long as we need:

- **Lawful basis:** legitimate interest (safety / fire evacuation accountability)
- **Retention:** the bound book is kept for **12 months** from the last entry in it, then shredded. (Fire risk assessment evidence does not require longer; if an incident occurred we keep the relevant pages on a separate file.)
- **Privacy notice on the desk:** a small visible card next to the log says "We use this log only to know who is in the building in an emergency, and for records of who has visited. We keep it for 12 months. Full notice: worksopworkspace.co.uk/privacy." This is the §13 transparency requirement.
- **Other visitors' details:** the book is positioned so people aren't reading the previous entries while signing in.

---

## 5. Procedure on a fire alarm

1. Connor (or whoever is on shift) grabs the visitor book on the way out — it's small, sits in a known place, and lives by the door for that reason.
2. At the assembly point, the book is used alongside the member access list to account for everyone.
3. Sign-out times are written in once everyone is confirmed safe. Anyone unaccounted for is reported to the incoming fire crew.

This is in [SOP-010 Emergency Evacuation](../sops/sop-010-emergency-evacuation.md).
