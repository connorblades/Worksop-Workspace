The master cleaning schedule. Pairs with [`../training/coffee-and-refreshments.md`](../training/coffee-and-refreshments.md) for the refreshments side and with [`../checklists/03-weekly-deep-clean.md`](../checklists/03-weekly-deep-clean.md) for the once-a-week deep clean.

The premise: a coworking space that smells right, looks right, and feels right turns visitors into members. Cleaning is brand work. Don't outsource the thinking.

---

## 1. Who does what

| Cleaner | What they do | When |
|---|---|---|
| Member / visitor | Self-service tidying — push chair in, wipe up own spills | All day |
| Worksop Workspace staff | In-day tidying, refreshments restock, member-facing presentation | Throughout opening hours |
| External cleaning contractor | Daily deep-clean of toilets, floors, kitchen surfaces; weekly deeper clean | Out-of-hours (after close, before open, or weekend) |

The external contractor's brief lives in Drive's `07-facilities/cleaning-contractor-brief.md` and is updated when this schedule changes.

## 2. The schedule at a glance

| Frequency | Tasks |
|---|---|
| **Throughout the day** | Tidy desks as they free up. Empty the coffee grinds drawer mid-afternoon. Top up toilet paper if any cubicle is low. Wipe spills immediately. |
| **End of day (in-house)** | See [`../checklists/02-daily-closing.md`](../checklists/02-daily-closing.md) — coffee machine empty, bins emptied, surfaces wiped, sign reset |
| **Overnight (contractor)** | Toilets fully clean, floors vacuumed and mopped, kitchen worktops sanitised, bins lined fresh, refreshment area sparkling |
| **Weekly** | [`../checklists/03-weekly-deep-clean.md`](../checklists/03-weekly-deep-clean.md) — descale, inside-fridge, lockers, window glass |
| **Monthly** | Inside-windows external clean (contractor); plant rotation; soft furnishing vacuum |
| **Quarterly** | Carpet shampoo (if any carpet); upholstery clean of chairs; behind heavy furniture; air vents |
| **Annually** | Deep clean of HVAC vents and ducts; oven and dishwasher (if applicable) deep service; window cleaning external full |

## 3. Hot desk area

### Daily

- Tidy any visible items
- Wipe each desk in passing if there's a moment
- Empty bins at the end of day
- Check chairs are aligned at close

### Weekly (deep clean)

- Move every desk and chair, hoover under
- Wipe every desk with wood-safe cleaner (oak is sensitive; no acidic / abrasive products)
- Wipe every chair arm with antibacterial wipe
- Check each chair for damage; log to maintenance log if any need replacing

- Reset before, clean between — see [`../sops/sop-004-meeting-room-booking.md`](../sops/sop-004-meeting-room-booking.md) §3 and §7

- End of day — wipe table, reset chairs, screen off, sign reset

### Weekly

- As per [`../checklists/03-weekly-deep-clean.md`](../checklists/03-weekly-deep-clean.md) meeting room section

## 5. Kitchen / refreshments

### Daily

- Wipe counters at every refresh
- Empty grinds drawer mid-afternoon
- Refresh milk supply
- Empty refreshment bin at close
- Quick wipe of fridge handles and microwave (if present)

### Weekly

- Empty inside of fridge; discard anything over 5 days old in communal areas (members' own items are theirs)
- Descale coffee machine if not done in the last 7 days (process in [`../training/coffee-and-refreshments.md`](../training/coffee-and-refreshments.md))
- Clean kettle of limescale if needed
- Wipe inside the microwave (if present)
- Replace dishcloths
- Restock paper towels, sugar, tea, coffee

---

## 6. Toilets

### Daily (contractor)

- Bleach toilet bowls; scrub if needed
- Wipe seats, flush handles, door handles
- Clean sinks and taps
- Mop floor
- Restock toilet roll, hand soap, paper towels
- Empty sanitary bins (or contracted weekly with sanitary bin company)
- Wipe mirrors with glass cleaner
- Refresh air freshener

### Weekly (in-house — top-up check)

- Same items as the contractor list but lighter — confirm the contractor's standard is being met

---

## 7. Floors

### Daily (contractor)

- Hoover hard-floor areas
- Mop hard floor with neutral cleaner
- Spot-clean any stains

### Weekly (in-house)

- Move furniture to hoover under
- Check oak floor for damage / wear — log if found
- Replace welcome mat if stained

---

## 8. Soft furnishings

### Weekly

- Vacuum sofas, lounge seating
- Check for stains; spot-clean

### Monthly

- Rotate cushions
- Check seam integrity

### Quarterly

- Full upholstery clean (contractor)

---

## 9. Glass and windows

### Daily

- Front-door glass — wipe at open
- Reception desk surface — wipe at open and at close

### Weekly

- Inside windows — soft cloth, no harsh chemicals near oak surrounds

### Monthly

- External windows — contractor

---

## 10. Plants

### Daily

- Visual check — drooping plants get watered

### Weekly

- Water all plants (don't drench — overwatering kills more than underwatering)
- Remove dead leaves
- Slight position shift

### Monthly

- Replace any plant clearly declining

### Quarterly

- Repot if the plant has outgrown its pot
- Refresh top soil
- Feed if recommended for the species

## 12. Chemicals (COSHH)

We use:

- **General neutral cleaner** — for most surfaces
- **Wood-safe cleaner** — oak desks, meeting room table
- **Antibacterial wipes** — high-touch surfaces, hands
- **Glass cleaner** — windows, mirrors
- **Bleach** — toilet bowls only, never elsewhere
- **Floor cleaner** — hard floor mopping
- **Descaling agent** — coffee machine and kettle
- **Sanitary bin liner** — bathrooms

Safety data sheets for each are in Drive's `07-facilities/cosh/` folder. All chemicals are stored in the locked cleaning cupboard. Anyone using them follows the label and uses gloves.

We **don't**:
- Mix bleach with anything else
- Decant into unlabelled bottles
- Use scented anything near members' workspaces

## 13. Equipment & supplies

The cleaning equipment lives in the cleaning cupboard:

- Cordless vacuum (charged on the wall)
- Mop, bucket, microfibre heads
- Spray bottles (labelled)
- Microfibre cloths (colour-coded: yellow for kitchen, red for toilets, blue for general, green for glass)
- Bin liners (small for desks, medium for kitchen, large for bathrooms)
- Wet-floor sign

Supplies are reordered on the supplies-reorder checklist (Tier 2 doc — not yet written; in the meantime, restock weekly during the deep clean).

## 15. Recording

A short check after each contractor visit:

- Toilets: pass / fail (specific issues if fail)
- Floors: pass / fail
- Kitchen surfaces: pass / fail
- Bins: emptied / not
- Anything else flagged

Three consecutive partial-fails triggers a conversation with the contractor.
