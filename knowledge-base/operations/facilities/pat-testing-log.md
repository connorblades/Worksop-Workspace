# PAT Testing Log

**Worksop Workspace** · Operations · Facilities · *Phase 1 reference*

Status: **Draft v1 — needs Connor review.** Last updated: 16 May 2026.

---

Portable Appliance Testing (PAT) is the periodic inspection and electrical test of mains-powered equipment. UK law (Electricity at Work Regulations 1989) doesn't *mandate* PAT testing by name, but it does require employers to make sure electrical equipment is safe. PAT is the practical way most workplaces do that.

For a coworking space with shared kettles, coffee machines, microwaves, lamps, monitors, screens, and a lot of bring-your-own laptops — PAT testing the **business-owned** equipment annually is the right call. Member laptops and phones are the member's responsibility.

This document is the **log** of what's been tested and when.

---

## 1. Scope — what gets tested

Tested annually:

- All mains-powered kitchen equipment owned by Worksop Workspace (coffee machine, kettle, microwave, fridge, dishwasher when added)
- Desk lamps and floor lamps
- Worksop Workspace–owned monitors, screens, projectors, smart TVs
- Power strips and extension leads
- Networking equipment (router, switches, access points) where owned by Worksop Workspace
- Any other Worksop Workspace–owned class I or class II equipment plugged into a mains socket

Not tested:

- Member-owned laptops, chargers, phones, headphones, peripherals — these are the member's responsibility, and we do not have a basis or expertise to test them
- Hard-wired installation (sockets, switches, lighting fixtures) — that's covered by the **EICR** (Electrical Installation Condition Report) on a 5-year cycle, separate to PAT

---

## 2. Visual inspection — done in-house

Before the annual PAT, and at any time, anyone can visually inspect an item for:

- Damaged cable (cuts, exposed conductors, kinks)
- Damaged plug (broken case, bent pins, scorching)
- Loose mounting, cracked casing
- Signs of overheating (discolouration, smell)

If any of those are present, **unplug the item, tag it "do not use", and tell Connor**. Don't test it — replace or get it inspected.

---

## 3. Annual PAT — done by a competent person

Worksop Workspace books an external PAT engineer annually. The engineer:

- Tests every in-scope item
- Affixes a "tested" label with the test date
- Issues a written report listing all items, pass/fail outcomes, and any items withdrawn from use
- Charges per-item or per-visit — typical Worksop area pricing is £1–£2 per item plus a call-out

The written report is kept in `knowledge-base/operations/facilities/pat-reports/` (PDF, named by year). This log is updated with the date of test.

## 5. Adding new equipment

When new mains equipment is bought:

1. Record it in the [Asset Register](asset-register.md).
2. New equipment from a reputable supplier doesn't need PAT before first use — it's deemed safe under PUWER as it's just been bought.
3. Add it to the in-scope list for the **next** annual PAT.
4. If it's a high-use item (kettle, coffee machine, microwave) and it's been heavily used in its first year, consider an interim check at 6 months.

---

## 6. When a PAT fails

- The engineer tags the item as failed and leaves it on site if it cannot be made safe.
- Worksop Workspace either **repairs** (rare, only for cables — by a competent electrician), **replaces**, or **withdraws** the item.
- Failed items must not be plugged back in until a replacement or fix is verified.
- The fail and what was done is recorded in the log row for that year.
