# Quarterly Fire Safety Check

**Worksop Workspace** · Operations · *Phase 1 reference*

Status: Draft v1 — needs Connor review · Last updated: 15 May 2026

---

Done once a quarter — first week of January, April, July, October. ~45 minutes. Goes alongside the annual Fire Risk Assessment (statutory; lives in Drive's `06-legal-and-compliance/fire-risk-assessment/`).

This isn't a substitute for the FRA or the annual inspection by a competent fire safety adviser — it's the in-house check that runs between those.

The reference policy is [`../health-and-safety/fire-safety-policy.md`](../health-and-safety/fire-safety-policy.md). The evacuation procedure is [`../sops/sop-010-emergency-evacuation.md`](../sops/sop-010-emergency-evacuation.md).

---

## Alarms and detection

- [ ] Trigger the fire alarm at the panel (test mode where supported, or send a member of staff outside before triggering and inform anyone on site)
- [ ] Confirm all sounders can be heard from every part of the space — front door, every desk, kitchen, every toilet, meeting room, every private office
- [ ] Confirm strobes (where fitted) trigger visibly
- [ ] Reset the panel afterwards. Log the test in the fire safety logbook (next to the panel).
- [ ] Check every smoke and heat detector (visual — no dust, no damage). Press the test button on at least three random detectors and confirm response.
- [ ] Replace the battery in any detector signalling low battery
- [ ] Check the panel for any fault indicators or service-due lights

## Extinguishers

- [ ] Walk every extinguisher location and confirm:
    - Extinguisher is present, in place, unobstructed
    - The pressure gauge is in the green
    - The pin and tamper seal are intact
    - The annual service tag is in date (annual service is contractor-led; this is a visual check)
- [ ] Confirm the right type is in the right location (water/foam in office areas; CO2 near electrical equipment; wet chemical near kitchen if fryer-grade hazard exists)
- [ ] If anything is missing, depleted, or expired — contact the extinguisher contractor in Drive's suppliers register

## Fire blanket

- [ ] In the kitchen, accessible, unobstructed
- [ ] Packaging intact
- [ ] Wall mount secure

## Emergency lighting

- [ ] Use the key switch at the consumer unit to drop the mains for emergency lighting (briefly)
- [ ] Confirm every emergency light unit illuminates and stays lit during the test
- [ ] Note any that fail and contact the electrical contractor
- [ ] Restore mains and confirm units return to standby

## Escape routes

- [ ] Walk every escape route from every part of the space
- [ ] All routes clear — no boxes, no furniture, no storage
- [ ] All doors on the route open easily (no swelling, no sticking)
- [ ] All final exit doors open from inside without a key (push bars, thumb turns)
- [ ] Stair treads in good condition, no loose carpet, no obstructions
- [ ] External final discharge point clear (no parked cars blocking, no skips)

## Signage

- [ ] Every escape route signed correctly (running-man pictogram, arrow direction matches the route)
- [ ] "Fire exit, keep clear" on every internal escape door
- [ ] "Assembly point" sign at the assembly area outside (per [`../sops/sop-010-emergency-evacuation.md`](../sops/sop-010-emergency-evacuation.md))
- [ ] "Fire action" notice near the panel and at the main entrance — up to date with the assembly point and 999 instructions
- [ ] All signs photoluminescent / illuminated where required

## Fire doors

- [ ] Every fire door closes fully under its own closer (no propping)
- [ ] Intumescent strips and cold smoke seals present and undamaged on each fire door
- [ ] No visible damage to fire-door cores
- [ ] Magnetic hold-opens (where fitted) release on alarm activation — tested during the alarm test
- [ ] Door signage present ("Fire door — keep shut")

## Electrical hazards

- [ ] Walk the space, look for trailing leads, overloaded extension sockets, daisy-chained adapters
- [ ] Confirm PAT testing schedule for portable appliances is up to date (annual; contractor-led)
- [ ] Note any equipment showing wear

## Risk-assessment update

- [ ] Re-read the Fire Risk Assessment (Drive)
- [ ] Note any change to the space that may invalidate the FRA — new furniture layout, new equipment, new tenants — and flag for the next FRA review
- [ ] If a significant change has happened, get the FRA reviewed by the competent person ahead of the annual cycle

## Drills

- [ ] Confirm the next planned evacuation drill date. Drills run every six months.
- [ ] Past drills' findings actioned

## Records

- [ ] Sign the fire safety logbook with date, your name, all items above ticked
- [ ] Photograph the logbook page (audit trail)
- [ ] Update Drive's `06-legal-and-compliance/fire-safety-log.md` with the date and any issues

---

## When the check surfaces a problem

- **Minor** (a missing sign, a stuck door, a torn intumescent strip): fix this week. Add to maintenance log.
- **Material** (a non-firing detector, a discharged extinguisher, an obstructed escape route): fix today. Don't open the space tomorrow with a known fire safety defect.
- **Significant** (the panel has a fault, multiple detectors not responding, emergency lighting widespread failure): stop operating until resolved. Don't trade. Brief Connor immediately.
