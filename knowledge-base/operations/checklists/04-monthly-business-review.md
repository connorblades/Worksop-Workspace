# Monthly Business Review Checklist

**Worksop Workspace** · Operations · *Phase 1 reference*

Status: Draft v1 — needs Connor review · Last updated: 15 May 2026

---

Done once a month — first working day of the new month. ~90 minutes. The purpose is to step back, look at the numbers and the patterns, decide what changes for next month. Pair the output with a short summary written into Drive's `04-finance/monthly-summaries/YYYY-MM.md`.

---

## Pre-review — pull the data

- [ ] Member app — active memberships count (split: Hot Desk Weekly, Private Office, Dedicated Desk if applicable)
- [ ] Member app — new sign-ups this month
- [ ] Member app — cancellations this month
- [ ] Member app — day passes sold this month
- [ ] Member app — meeting room hours booked this month
- [ ] Stripe — gross revenue this month
- [ ] Stripe — refunds and credits issued
- [ ] Stripe — failed-payment count (and resolution status)
- [ ] Drive — last month's monthly summary, for comparison
- [ ] Reviews — Google review count this month, average rating, any 1–3 star reviews
- [ ] Complaints log — count and tier breakdown
- [ ] Maintenance log — outstanding items
- [ ] Marketing — paid ad spend, leads generated, ad-to-tour conversion

## Membership

- [ ] Net member movement this month (signups minus cancellations)
- [ ] Trend over the last three months — growing, flat, or shrinking?
- [ ] Average cancellation tenure — are people leaving in week 1, month 1, month 6?
- [ ] Reason patterns in cancellations (where given)

## Revenue

- [ ] Total gross revenue vs forecast
- [ ] Revenue mix — what % from Hot Desk Weekly, Private Office, Day Passes, Meeting Room?
- [ ] Day-pass-to-membership conversion — how many day-pass users joined this month?
- [ ] Outstanding failed payments — what's still unresolved?

## Operations

- [ ] Incidents and complaints — anything systemic to address
- [ ] Maintenance log — items resolved, items outstanding
- [ ] Wi-Fi or service outages — count and impact
- [ ] Member-feedback themes (from the in-app issue reports)

## Marketing and pipeline

- [ ] Paid ads — spend, click-through, cost-per-tour-booked
- [ ] Tours run this month
- [ ] Tour-to-signup rate (compare to last month)
- [ ] Website traffic and form submissions (from analytics — track via the SYPB analytics pattern if applicable)
- [ ] Google reviews — any to respond to, any to chase

## People

- [ ] Staff hours / cost (when there's staff in Phase 2+)
- [ ] Cleaning contractor performance
- [ ] Any contractor or supplier issues

## Decisions for next month

- [ ] Pricing — any change? (No standing discount practice; intro offers only at launch — see [`../legal-and-compliance/terms-of-business.md`](../legal-and-compliance/terms-of-business.md) §4)
- [ ] Marketing — what's on / off for next month?
- [ ] Capex — anything to buy or repair?
- [ ] Hires — capacity question
- [ ] Members at risk — any to personally check in with?
- [ ] Wins to celebrate — share with members in the monthly update

## Write the summary

Single markdown file in Drive at `04-finance/monthly-summaries/YYYY-MM.md`:

- One-line headline: *"April 2026 — flat membership, two new private offices, no major incidents."*
- Numbers (table)
- Three wins
- Three things to fix
- Three decisions for next month

## Share

- [ ] Connor reads the summary, edits, approves
- [ ] (When there's staff) — share with the team in the next 1:1
- [ ] Member-facing monthly update written (separate doc — see [`../../marketing/members/`](../../marketing/members/) once populated)

---

## When the review surfaces a problem

A monthly review should produce decisions, not just observations. If the numbers say "membership is shrinking three months in a row" — the decision isn't "watch it again next month." The decision is what we're going to do about it. Write that down in the summary, with a date by which we expect to see the effect.
