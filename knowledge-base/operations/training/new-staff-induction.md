# New Staff Induction

**Worksop Workspace** · Training · *Phase 1 reference*

Status: Draft v1 — needs Connor review · Last updated: 14 May 2026

---

The first two weeks of being on the Worksop Workspace team. This isn't a 50-page HR manual — it's the rhythm of how a new hire gets up to speed without being overwhelmed. Mostly: read, shadow, practise, get reviewed, then run with it.

This doc kicks in the moment a contract is signed, not before. Pre-hire (recruitment, references, right-to-work) is a separate process owned by Connor.

---

## Before day one

A few things to be sorted before the new starter walks in:

- **Right to work check** completed and documented (statutory — UK employer obligation)
- **Contract signed** by both parties, with start date and probation period (typically 3 months) confirmed
- **Bank details** received for payroll
- **Emergency contact** for the new starter recorded
- **Member app staff access** provisioned at the appropriate permission level
- **Email account** created at @worksopworkspace.co.uk
- **Keys / alarm code** prepared — but not handed over until induction starts
- **A printed copy of `../05-members/welcome-pack.md`, `../05-members/house-rules.md`, and this doc** on their desk on day one

## Day one (Monday is ideal)

### 8am — Arrival and welcome

- Connor opens up and waits for the new starter
- Coffee made together — chat, not formal. This is where the tone is set.
- Quick walk of the building — every room, every fire exit, where the alarm panel is, where the stopcock is, where the main breaker is, where the first aid kit lives

### 9am — Reading window

- Quiet 90 minutes at a desk with the printed materials
- Reading order (the same one in `../essentials/start-here.md` if it's there, or the Drive `00-essentials/01-start-here.md`):
  1. `essentials/02-points-of-truth.md` (Drive) — what the business is
  2. `02-brand/brand-guidelines.md` — how we sound
  3. `05-members/house-rules.md` — what's allowed in the space
  4. `03-operations/operating-manual.md` — how the space runs
- Questions encouraged. Write them down.

### 10:30am — Q&A with Connor

- Half an hour to go through whatever came up in the reading
- Connor confirms understanding on the key points: the no-networking rule, the no-pressure rule, the "drop your shoulders" feeling, the gym-style pricing model

### 11am — Shadow the opening routine

- Even though it's mid-morning, walk through the opening checklist (`../03-operations/checklists/daily-opening.md`) as if it's 7:30am
- Show, don't just tell — actually open the coffee machine, actually check the milk, actually run the Wi-Fi test
- New starter takes notes

### 12pm — Lunch

- Take a proper hour. Eat something. Don't make day one a marathon.

### 1pm — Shadow members arriving

- Sit in a hot desk area and watch how members come in, scan, settle
- Observe Connor handling any walk-ins or queries
- Don't engage yet — just watch

### 2:30pm — Walkthrough of the member app

- Use `app-and-booking-walkthrough.md` as the spine
- Live demo on Connor's staff dashboard
- Practice: look up a real member, view today's bookings, do a manual check-in on a test account

### 3:30pm — The tour script

- Read `tour-script.md` together
- Connor delivers a sample tour (with new starter as the "prospect")
- Then new starter delivers a sample tour (with Connor as the "prospect")
- Honest feedback, no ego

### 4:30pm — Shadow the closing routine

- Walk through `../03-operations/checklists/daily-closing.md`
- New starter does each step under supervision
- Lock up together

### 5pm — Day one debrief

- What landed? What was confusing? What needs reinforcing tomorrow?
- Day two priorities agreed
- Go home

## Days two to five

| Day | Focus | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Day 2 | Open with Connor supervising; staff a quiet hour solo while Connor stays in the building; tour-script practice with a friendly tester (Connor or a friend) | Supervised |
| Day 3 | Open solo, Connor arrives at 10; conflict-handling reading + scenario role-plays; staff the floor through lunch | Supervised |
| Day 4 | Run an actual tour for a real visitor (Connor present); cover phone-handling training; handle one booking issue end-to-end | Supervised |
| Day 5 | Open and close fully — Connor present but hands-off all day; daily summary written by new starter at the end of the day | Light-touch |

Anything that didn't land in week 1 gets repeated in week 2.

---

## Days six to ten (week 2)

The new starter runs the day. Connor checks in by phone or in person twice a day max. Specific learning goals:

- **Day 6**: Lead a tour without Connor present
- **Day 7**: Handle a member issue independently (use the framework, log it)
- **Day 8**: Close the building solo
- **Day 9**: Do the weekly deep-clean (`../03-operations/checklists/` will get a weekly-deep-clean.md once written)
- **Day 10**: Two-week review with Connor

---

## The two-week review

A proper sit-down conversation. About 30 minutes.

**Connor covers:**

- What's gone well — be specific
- What needs more work — be specific
- Any rules / decisions that came up in week 1–2 that need to be documented for future starters
- Confirmation of probation length and what "passing" looks like

**New starter covers:**

- What's working
- What's confusing or missing
- Anything in the docs they'd change after using them for two weeks
- Any tools / kit / training they need

**Updates afterwards:**

- Any feedback that's general enough to be useful gets folded back into the relevant training doc — they're living documents
- Anything that's about this individual stays in their personal file (not in this folder)

## Probation

Standard 3-month probationary period in the contract. During probation:

- Weekly 15-minute check-in between Connor and the new starter
- Either party can end the arrangement with a week's notice
- After successful completion, full notice period (per contract) kicks in

If anything goes seriously wrong during probation (safeguarding, dishonesty, repeated rule breaches), Connor handles it directly — possibly with HR / legal support. Not in scope for this training doc.

## Day-one practical kit list

Things to physically hand over on day one:

- Keys (front door + alarm fob if separate)
- Alarm code (written down once, learned, then the paper is destroyed)
- 1Password access (joined to a shared vault by Connor)
- Email login (Google Workspace)
- Staff access to the member app
- Stripe dashboard access (read-only initially; full access after probation)
- A staff badge / t-shirt if those exist
- A copy of all the printed docs above
- A notebook and pen (don't underestimate this — most useful tool in week 1)

## What new starters consistently struggle with

Real patterns, useful to flag upfront:

- **The brand voice.** Reading "no salesy language" isn't the same as instinctively not using it. Expect 2–3 weeks before the voice is automatic. Connor's role is to gently call out drift — never publicly.
- **The networking rule.** Some hires from a sales / business-dev background find it counter-intuitive. Reinforce: we're not anti-business. We're pro-focus. Members can chat at the coffee machine; they can't pitch each other.
- **Pricing confidence.** "It's £30 a week" feels too cheap to some people who've worked in other office sectors. Reinforce: that IS the deal. Don't apologise for it.
- **Saying "I don't know".** Some people feel they need to have an answer for everything. They don't. "Let me find out" is fine — better than a wrong answer.

## What success in the first month looks like

- The new starter can open and close the space solo
- They can deliver a tour that lands the brand
- They've handled at least one real member issue and logged it correctly
- They've taken at least one phone enquiry from start to finish without escalation
- The member app feels like a tool they reach for, not one they avoid
- Members have started to recognise them by face

If any of those is missing at the four-week mark, that's not a fail — it's a flag to spend more time on it.
