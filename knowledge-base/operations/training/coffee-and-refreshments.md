# Coffee and Refreshments

**Worksop Workspace** · Training · *Phase 1 reference*

Status: Draft v1 — needs Connor review · Last updated: 14 May 2026

---

Tea, coffee, water — all included for every member and visitor. This doc covers how to set it up in the morning, keep it tidy through the day, and shut it down properly at night. Plus restocking, allergens, and what to do when the machine throws a wobbly.

The full equipment list with model numbers lives in `../../../knowledge-base/operations/operating-manual.md` and Drive's `07-facilities/equipment-inventory.md`.

---

## The promise to members

Coffee, tea, and water are unlimited and free. No buying anything to justify the seat. Help yourself.

That promise has to be true every single day, all day. If the milk runs out at 2pm, the promise is broken. Stay ahead.

---

## Morning setup (part of `sop-001-opening-the-space.md`)

1. Switch on the coffee machine — give it 15 minutes to warm up before doors open
2. Refill the bean hopper (check level; top up if less than half)
3. Check the milk fridge:
   - Whole milk — at least 2 litres for the morning
   - Semi-skimmed — at least 2 litres
   - Oat milk — at least 1 carton open + 1 spare
   - Almond / soya / other alternatives — at least 1 carton each
4. Boil the kettle once to warm it
5. Check the tea caddy / box — black tea, herbal tea, decaf, fruit options
6. Check sugar, sweetener, honey, biscuits if offered
7. Empty and rinse the drip tray
8. Empty the grounds container
9. Wipe down the counter with surface spray
10. Make yourself the first coffee — confirms the machine is working before the first member arrives

---

## Through the day

Every couple of hours, do a quick refreshment-area walk:

- Wipe down spills
- Empty drip tray if approaching full
- Empty grounds container if full
- Refill milk if a carton is running low (don't wait for empty)
- Top up bean hopper if low
- Wipe coffee machine wand after use
- Check the kettle's still got water
- Replace tea bag stock if visibly low
- Wash any cups left out (or run the dishwasher if there's a load)

Set a phone timer if you forget. The refreshment area is the most visible expression of the brand promise — if it looks neglected, the whole space feels neglected.

---

## Coffee machine — daily clean (end of day, part of `sop-002-closing-the-space.md`)

1. Run the machine's automated cleaning cycle (model-specific — see the machine manual in `../../../07-facilities/` or on the wall behind the machine)
2. Empty + rinse the grounds container
3. Empty + rinse the drip tray
4. Wipe the steam wand with a damp cloth — important, milk crystallises and ruins shots if left
5. Wipe the front and sides with a damp cloth
6. Switch off — let it cool down before you lock up

---

## Coffee machine — weekly deep clean (typically Friday after close)

1. Descale per the manual — usually once a month, but weekly check
2. Backflush with cleaning tablet (espresso machines specifically)
3. Remove and rinse the bean hopper
4. Clean the milk frother thoroughly — disassemble if possible
5. Wipe seals and gaskets — replace if they look worn
6. Date the cleaning in `../../../07-facilities/maintenance-log.md`

---

## Tea station

- Boil the kettle on demand — don't keep boiling repeatedly through the day, just when needed
- Wipe spills around the kettle base
- Store teabags in a dry container (humidity kills the taste)
- Check sugar and sweetener for clumping — replace if hard
- Refill honey, lemon, ginger options if stocked

---

## Milk handling — non-negotiable

- **Always check dates** — discard anything past the date, even if it smells fine
- Rotate cartons: open the older one first (write the opening date on the carton with a marker)
- Never serve from a carton that's been out of the fridge more than 30 minutes — bin it
- Plant-based milks (oat, almond, soya) usually keep slightly longer once opened — check the brand's note
- If any member ever complains about milk taste, bin the carton without arguing and apologise

## Allergens and dietary needs

A member with an allergy can ask any of us at any time. Have an answer ready. Default stock should cover:

- **Dairy** — whole, semi-skimmed (cow)
- **Plant-based** — at least oat; ideally also almond and soya
- **Gluten-free options** — keep a clearly-labelled tin of GF biscuits if any biscuits are offered
- **Nut-free** — be clear about which milks contain nuts (almond does, oat doesn't)
- **Decaf** — both decaf coffee beans and decaf tea bags available

**Cross-contamination matters.** Use separate jugs for plant-based milks. Don't pour oat milk into a jug that just had cow's milk in it.

When in doubt: tell the member exactly what we stock and let them decide. Never improvise an answer to "is this safe for me?" — if you're not sure, say "let me check the packaging" and actually read it.

## Restocking — who and when

Until staff are hired, Connor does the order. Once a staff member is on board, this is where the rota lives.

Typical pattern:

- **Milk** — bought every 2–3 days during launch period; more once member numbers grow
- **Beans** — weekly bulk order, kept airtight
- **Tea bags** — weekly or fortnightly depending on consumption
- **Plant-based milks** — weekly
- **Sweeteners, sugar, biscuits** — fortnightly check
- **Cleaning supplies** — monthly (machine cleaner, descaler, dishwasher tablets)

Supplier contacts live in `../../../07-facilities/suppliers-and-contractors.md` (Drive only, sensitive).

When stock of anything drops below 50% of the typical "full" level, reorder. **Never run out.** That's the rule.

## Common issues

| Problem | First action | If that fails |
|---|---|---|
| Coffee machine error light | Check water tank, drip tray, grounds container all in place | Refer to manual; if still failing, contact supplier (suppliers-and-contractors in Drive) |
| Milk frother not foaming | Wipe the steam wand — it's probably blocked with dried milk | Deep clean per weekly procedure |
| Coffee tastes bad | Beans might be stale; descale due; or grinder needs cleaning | Replace beans, run descale, brush out grinder |
| No hot water from machine | Wait — machine may be still warming up | If been on >15 min, refer to manual |
| Member asks for something we don't stock | Apologise, note it in the feedback log, decide whether to add it to standing order | — |

## What never to do

- **Never reuse coffee grounds.** Bin them after every shot.
- **Never re-heat tea or coffee in a microwave.** Make a fresh one.
- **Never serve from a milk carton with no date written on it** if it's been opened. If you can't tell when it was opened, bin it.
- **Never let the coffee machine run dry of water.** It damages the heating element.
- **Never use bleach on anything that contacts food or drink.** Use food-safe sanitiser only.
