For tradespeople, builders & sole traders

A workspace for tradespeople
in Worksop and Bassetlaw.

Quotes, invoices, certs and calls. Out of the van, off the kitchen table, sat at a proper desk for an hour. £12 a day. No contract.

Run the business from a desk, not a car park.

Worksop Workspace is a coworking space on Carlton Road in Worksop, used by tradespeople across Bassetlaw and North Nottinghamshire to handle the part of their business that does not happen on site. A quiet floor, free coffee, free parking with space for a van, 900 Mbps fibre and a printer. £12 for a day pass, £30 a week if you want to keep coming back.

We are the first modern coworking space in Worksop. The nearest alternatives are in Sheffield or Nottingham, neither of which is much help when you are between jobs and have an afternoon to clear paperwork.

When this is for you

Pop in when the office side of the day starts.

A few common reasons tradespeople use the space.

Running quotes

Three quotes to write up, photos to attach, terms to copy in. An hour at a desk with proper Wi-Fi and you are sending them out the same day.

End-of-month invoicing

Twenty invoices, CIS returns, VAT paperwork. Sat at a desk you will finish in half the time you would at the kitchen table.

Certs and compliance

Gas safety records, electrical installation certs, building control submissions. Printer on site, scanner if you need it.

Client calls without traffic noise

You sound like a serious business when the background is a quiet office, not the A1.

Meeting a client face-to-face

The meeting room books by the hour at £20. A proper place to walk a client through a quote, sign-off, or change order.

The job dropped through

Cancellation. Rain day. Material not delivered. Rather than head home and lose the day, get a week ahead on admin instead.

Year-end and self-assessment

January in particular. Bring the receipts, the books, the laptop. Get it done somewhere that isn't your sofa.

Between site visits in Worksop

A finished job in the morning, another in the afternoon, three hours in the middle. Park, sit down, get the admin done before you drive home.

Getting here

By van, car, train, bus, or on foot.

By van or car

30 Carlton Road, Worksop S80 1PH. Free on-site parking with space for vans and pickups. A60, A57, A1 and M1 J30 all feed into Worksop within 20 minutes. Open in Google Maps →

By train

Worksop station is a short walk from Carlton Road. The station is on the Sheffield.Lincoln line (operated by Northern) and the Robin Hood Line to Mansfield and Nottingham (operated by East Midlands Railway). Live times and journey planning at National Rail.

By bus

A bus stop sits directly outside the building. Worksop bus station is a 10 minute walk away. Most local services are run by Stagecoach East Midlands. Plan any route. Bus or multi-mode. At Traveline East Midlands.

On foot or by taxi

Carlton Road is central. A short walk from the station and Bridge Street. Taxi from the station rank is around five minutes if you would rather not walk.

FAQs

The questions tradespeople actually ask.

Where can I do my quotes and invoices if I work out of a van?

Worksop Workspace on Carlton Road is built for exactly this. Drop in for £12 a day, park free outside, sit down at a proper desk and clear an afternoon of admin in a quiet professional space. No contract, no membership required for a day pass.

Do I need to be a member to use the space as a tradesperson?

No. You can drop in on a Day Pass at £12 a day or buy a Weekly Hot Desk at £30 per week. Membership is rolling weekly and you can stop any time. Tradespeople tend to start on the day pass and move to weekly once they realise how much admin they get through.

Is there parking for a van or pickup?

Yes. There is free on-site parking on Carlton Road with space for vans and pickups. No time limits during opening hours. Worksop town centre charges for most car parks, so this is one of the few free options.

Can I print certificates and gas safety paperwork?

Yes. There is a printer on site for everyday A4 and A3 print. Members and day-pass users can print test certificates, gas safety records, electrical certs, invoices and quote PDFs.

What broadband speed do you have for uploading photos and videos to job apps?

Worksop Workspace runs on 900 Mbps dedicated fibre. Uploading job photos to apps like Checkatrade, MyJobQuote, or your CRM is fast even on busy days. No throttling, no shared home routers.

Can I take a private call about a quote?

Yes. The main floor has a relaxed working volume so most phone calls are fine without leaving your seat. For private or longer calls, the meeting room is bookable by the hour from £20 per hour.

How do I get to Worksop Workspace from Tickhill, Bawtry or Harworth?

By road: the A60, A638 and A1 all feed into Worksop within 10 to 15 minutes from those towns. Free parking on Carlton Road. By bus: Stagecoach East Midlands runs regular services into Worksop bus station, which is a 10 minute walk from the workspace. Plan a bus route at Traveline East Midlands.

Do tradespeople actually use the space, or is it just office workers?

Both. The space is built for anyone serious about their work. Tradespeople, builders, electricians, plumbers, gas engineers, mobile mechanics and groundworkers come in to handle the side of their business that doesn't happen on site. Quotes, invoices, CIS returns, VAT, scheduling, client calls. The mix is part of why it works.
