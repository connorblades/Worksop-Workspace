I'm a Tradesperson . Where Do I Do My Quotes and Invoices?

16 May 2026 5 min read

The shortest answer: book a £12 day pass at Worksop Workspace on Carlton Road for the afternoon you have to do quotes, invoices and certs. Free parking with space for a van, 900 Mbps fibre, a printer, and a quiet desk for the admin you cannot do from a layby. If you do admin most days, the £30/week Hot Desk Weekly costs less than two daily passes.

Most tradespeople do not love the office side of their business. You did not become an electrician or a plasterer or a joiner because you wanted to write 14 quotes in a day. But every job that gets sold and paid is a job that involves an hour of paperwork. Quoting, invoicing, scheduling, chasing, certifying. And that hour has to happen somewhere.

The default: in the van

Most tradespeople we know start out doing admin in the van. After a long day on site, you sit in the cab in some car park, fingers cold, screen glare from the windscreen, signal flaky, knocking out invoices with one finger on the phone.

It works, sort of. It is also why most one-man bands hate the admin and let it pile up. The environment fights the work. You hit "send" on three invoices and call it done, when actually six more should have gone out.

The kitchen table option

Once you get home, the laptop comes out, dinner is in 20 minutes, the kids want bedtime stories, and your partner reminds you about Saturday's thing. The admin gets started and abandoned. Same problem, different room.

A different kind of fix

A coworking space designed for exactly this is what most tradespeople do not realise exists. £12 buys you a day pass at Worksop Workspace on Carlton Road. Park the van outside (free, no time limit), bring the laptop, sit at a real desk in a quiet room for three hours, and clear the backlog properly.

What that buys you specifically:

  • 900 Mbps fibre. Upload job photos to Checkatrade, MyJobQuote or your CRM without staring at a progress bar
  • A printer and scanner. Print test certificates, gas safety records, electrical certs, invoices
  • A meeting room at £20/hour if a client wants to walk through a quote in person
  • Free unlimited tea, coffee and water. You do not have to buy a flat white to justify the seat
  • Quiet floor. When you take a call about a quote, you sound like the serious tradesman you are

Most tradespeople who try the space switch from "day pass when I need it" to the £30/week Hot Desk Weekly within a fortnight. Once you realise how much faster the admin is at a proper desk, you stop doing it from the van.

When to actually go in

The high-value moments for tradespeople using a workspace:

  • End-of-month invoicing day. Twenty invoices in two hours instead of a week of bits and pieces
  • The quote-heavy week. When you have a backlog of jobs to scope and price
  • VAT return week. Every quarter. Receipts, bank statements, your accountant's questions. Faster done in one sitting.
  • Cert and compliance day. Gas safety records, electrical installation certs, building control submissions. Printer on site
  • The rained-off day. Material did not arrive, weather turned, client cancelled. Rather than head home and waste the day, get a week ahead on admin instead
  • January. Self-assessment, year-end, accountant emails. Genuinely a good week to be at a real desk for a few days

Where you can get to it from

Worksop Workspace is at 30 Carlton Road in Worksop town centre. By road, you can get to it from across North Notts and the south Doncaster area:

  • Tickhill. 8 miles, 15 min via A60
  • Bawtry. 10 miles, 18 min via A1/A638
  • Maltby. 8 miles, 18 min via A60/B6463
  • Dinnington. 8 miles, 20 min via A57
  • Harworth & Bircotes. 7 miles, 12 min straight down the A1
  • Clowne. 7 miles, 15 min via A619/A60
  • Retford. 8 miles, 15 min via A620

Free on-site parking. No Pay-and-Display, no time limits during opening hours.

The bigger picture

Most one-man bands undercharge for the admin side because the admin side is miserable. When the environment changes, the rate of clearing it doubles, and the quality of what you send out goes up.
