Got questions?

The most common ones, answered.

When do you open?
We're building the waiting list now and will open once we have sufficient interest confirmed. Everyone on the waiting list will be the first to hear and will get first pick of desks and offices before we open to the public.
Can I just turn up and work?
Yes. Just bring your laptop. Everything else is already here. WiFi, coffee, desk, chair, parking. You walk in, sit down, and get on with it. That's the whole point.
What are the opening hours?
We are open Monday to Friday during business hours. Exact times will be confirmed at launch. Join the waiting list to be the first to know.
Where exactly are you?
We're on Carlton Road, Worksop, Nottinghamshire, close to the town centre with free parking directly on-site. Easy to get to, quick to leave.
Can I try before committing?
There is no committing. A day pass is £12, the weekly pass is £30. Walk in, get your day's work done, leave. If you like it, come back. If you don't, don't.
What if I need to cancel?
Just stop. The weekly pass is rolling. You cancel whenever, no notice, no penalty. If life gets in the way for a few months, that's fine, come back when you're ready. Same price waiting for you.
Can my team book multiple desks?
Yes. Companies regularly book several day passes or weekly passes under one account. If you want lockable space, the private office is built for 1 to 4 people. Talk to us if you've got a bigger team in mind.
Do I have to prove what I'm working on?
No. Corporate job escaping the kids for a few hours? Running a business? Freelancing? Studying for professional exams? All fine. You don't owe us an explanation, just bring your laptop.
Is there an age limit?
Yes. 18 and over only. This is a working space, not a study lounge. Everyone here is here to work.
Will people try to pitch me or sell to me?
No. This is a place for execution, not networking. Everyone here is focused on their own work, not looking for their next client. There's no pressure, no business cards being handed around, and no expectation to socialise. If a conversation happens naturally, great. But no one is here to sell to you.
Is it actually quiet?
Yes. The ground floor workspace is a dedicated quiet zone. Everyone there is focused, and that shared intent creates a naturally calm atmosphere. The basement is more relaxed, with ambient music in the background. Either way, it's nothing like a café on a Saturday morning. Come in, drop your shoulders, and get on with it.
