Working from home alternative · Worksop

When home isn't working today.

Builders, broken Wi-Fi, a full house, a coffee shop that won't shut up. £12 a day, drop in, get the day back.

Worksop's working-from-home alternative.

Worksop Workspace is the only dedicated coworking space in Worksop. £12 buys you a Day Pass with free parking, 900 Mbps fibre, unlimited free coffee, a quiet professional floor with plugs at every seat. No contract, no membership, no booking required. Drop in on the day home isn't working — when the builders arrived early, when the Wi-Fi engineer is "in the area, sometime today", when the kids are off, when the coffee shop tax has finally got too much.

Most people come once, get more done than they'd expected, and start coming the weeks home gets in the way.

Getting here

By car, train, bus or on foot.

By car

30 Carlton Road, Worksop S80 1PH. Free on-site parking, no time limits. A60, A57, A1 and M1 J30 all feed into Worksop. Open in Google Maps →

By train

Worksop station is a short walk away. Sheffield–Lincoln line (Northern) plus the Robin Hood Line to Mansfield and Nottingham (East Midlands Railway). Live times at National Rail.

By bus

Bus stop directly outside the building. Worksop bus station is a 10-minute walk. Most services run by Stagecoach East Midlands. Plan at Traveline East Midlands.

On foot or by taxi

Carlton Road is central. Short walk from the station and Bridge Street. Taxi from station rank takes about five minutes.

FAQs

The questions people ask.

Where can I work in Worksop when I can't work from home today?

Worksop Workspace on Carlton Road is the only dedicated coworking space in Worksop. Drop in for £12 a day with no contract — free parking, 900 Mbps fibre, free coffee, a quiet professional floor. Suitable for the day the builders arrive, the Wi-Fi drops, the house is full, or the coffee shop is too loud.

Is there a coworking space near me I can use for just one day?

Yes. Worksop Workspace offers a Day Pass at £12 with no membership and no contract. You can drop in any time during opening hours (8am–5pm Monday to Friday).

Where to work when the builders are in?

If you're in Worksop, Retford, Tickhill, Bawtry, Maltby, Dinnington, Harworth or anywhere in Bassetlaw, the answer is Worksop Workspace on Carlton Road. £12 a day, no commitment, come in for one day or the whole renovation.

Where can I go if my home Wi-Fi is down?

Worksop Workspace runs on 900 Mbps dedicated fibre with no throttling. If your home line drops, drop in on a Day Pass for £12 — same day, no booking needed.

Is this better than working from a coffee shop?

For getting work done, yes. No music or background chatter, plugs at every seat, free unlimited coffee, fast Wi-Fi, no glares for taking up a table. About the same daily cost as three coffees from Costa.

Can I drop in without booking?

Yes. Day Pass is walk-in. Join the waiting list for the latest opening updates, but you don't need to book a specific desk.

How do I find Worksop Workspace?

30 Carlton Road, Worksop S80 1PH — short walk from Worksop railway station and the bus station, on a bus route, free on-site parking.

Will I have to commit to a contract?

No. Day pass is £12 for one day. Hot Desk Weekly is £30/week, rolling, cancel any time. No setup fees, no notice period beyond the current week.
