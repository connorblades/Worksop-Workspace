Coworking in Bassetlaw

The district's first flexible, community-focused coworking space. Free parking. No contract.

Bassetlaw finally has a proper coworking space

Bassetlaw has more than 117,000 residents and over 5,000 registered businesses. Until now, professionals who work from home in the district have had nowhere local to go when they needed a proper workspace. The only options were government-run innovation centres aimed at startups, or a trip into Doncaster, Sheffield, or Nottingham.

Worksop Workspace changes that. Based in Worksop town centre, it serves the whole of Bassetlaw: from Harworth and Bircotes in the north to Tuxford in the south, Carlton in Lindrick to the east and Whitwell to the west. A genuinely flexible, community-focused workspace with free parking and real value for money.

Who uses Worksop Workspace?

Remote workers, self-employed professionals, and small business owners from across Bassetlaw.

Remote workers

Employed professionals working from home for companies in Sheffield, Nottingham, Doncaster, or beyond who need a proper local workspace a few days a week.

Freelancers and self-employed

Creatives, consultants, designers, writers, and tradespeople who need a professional address and somewhere to work that is not their kitchen table.

Small business owners

One or two-person businesses in Bassetlaw who need flexible office space without the cost and commitment of a long-term lease.

Content creators

Business owners, coaches, and podcasters who want access to a professional recording studio without travelling to Sheffield or Doncaster.

Teams who need to meet

Hybrid and remote teams who need a proper meeting room to come together once a month without going into a city centre.

People who want their home back

Professionals who moved to Bassetlaw for the space and the affordable housing but need somewhere to actually work without the distractions of home.

Serving the whole of Bassetlaw

Worksop is the largest town in Bassetlaw and is centrally located within the district. Most places in Bassetlaw are within 20 minutes of the workspace. Free parking means no one is paying extra to get there.

Carlton in Lindrick

5 minutes. One of Bassetlaw's most affluent villages, with a high proportion of professional households.

Harworth and Bircotes

8 minutes. A growing residential community with a significant proportion of remote-working professionals.

Langold and Rhodesia

5 to 10 minutes. Quiet residential areas close to Worksop with strong demand for local professional facilities.

Blyth and Styrrup

12 minutes. Village communities between Worksop and Doncaster with no local workspace provision.

Retford

15 minutes. Bassetlaw's second town. No flexible coworking space in the town itself.

Whitwell and Clowne

10 minutes. Derbyshire border communities with easy access to the A619 and A60.

The only flexible workspace in Bassetlaw

There is no other day-pass-accessible, community-focused coworking space in the Bassetlaw district. The council-run Worksop Turbine is aimed at startups and does not offer day passes or hot desks. Retford Enterprise Centre offers full office suites only.
